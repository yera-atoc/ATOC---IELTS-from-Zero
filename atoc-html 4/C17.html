Parallel Structures & Rhetorical Balance

Как симметрия делает аргумент убедительнее?

Тематика: Образование и социальная мобильность · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Problem-Solution Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Два финала эссе об образовании и мобильности:

Band 6: Education is important for social mobility. Without education, people cannot get good jobs. Governments should invest more in education because it will help society.

Band 8: Education systems that expand access not merely to credentials but to genuine intellectual formation — not merely to qualifications but to the capacity for critical thought — are not simply an instrument of economic development; they are, above all, a mechanism through which societies reproduce themselves as genuinely open rather than merely nominally meritocratic.

Посмотри на Band 8: 'not merely to X but to Y — not merely to X but to Y'. Это не просто повторение. Это риторический баланс — структура, которая несёт смысл через симметрию. Читатель чувствует, что два элемента сопоставлены, взвешены, оценены. Именно это и есть риторическая сила параллельных структур.

📘 Grammar 1 · Correlative Conjunctions: парные союзы как риторические весы

Not only X but also Y · Not X but Y · Both X and Y · Neither X nor Y · Either X or Y · Not so much X as Y
🟣

В уровне A ты знал: 'not only... but also', 'both... and', 'neither... nor'.

В уровне C мы изучаем их риторическую функцию: каждый парный союз создаёт разные весы — разные отношения между двумя элементами.
Конструкция Риторическая функция Формальные требования Академический пример
Not only X but (also) Y Добавление с усилением: Y важнее или неожиданнее X Строгий параллелизм: X и Y одного синтаксического типа Educational inequality perpetuates not only economic disadvantage but also, and more insidiously, the systematic attenuation of intellectual aspiration itself.
Not X but Y Замена / коррекция: X — ложное, Y — истинное X и Y — прямая оппозиция; Y опровергает X The primary obstacle to social mobility is not the absence of talent in disadvantaged communities but the structural suppression of the conditions under which talent can develop and be recognised.
Not merely X but Y Уточнение масштаба: Y больше, чем предполагает X Y включает X и превосходит его Education must address not merely the transmission of knowledge but the cultivation of the reflective capacity that enables individuals to interrogate and extend that knowledge.
Not so much X as Y Переключение акцента: X менее важен, чем Y X уменьшается, Y усиливается; тонкое разграничение The challenge is not so much the lack of financial investment as the structural disincentives that prevent existing resources from reaching those who most need them.
Both X and Y Двойное включение: оба истинны X и Y — равнозначные элементы одной категории Both the content of the curriculum and the pedagogical methods through which it is delivered require fundamental reform.
Neither X nor Y Двойное исключение: ни один не истинен Отрицание обоих элементов как неправильных ответов Neither increased spending alone nor structural reform alone is sufficient; what is required is their coordinated and sustained combination.

⚡ Not only X but also Y: правило инверсии при фронтинге

Стандартная позиция (X в середине предложения): 'Education perpetuates not only economic disadvantage but also cognitive deprivation.'

Fronted (X в начале — C9 + инверсия): 'Not only does education perpetuate economic disadvantage; it also systematically suppresses the cognitive ambitions of those born into poverty.'

Правило: Когда 'Not only' стоит в начале предложения, главная клауза требует инверсии (does/is/has + subject). Это комбинация Fronting (C9) + Correlative conjunction (C17).

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The persistent underrepresentation of students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds in elite educational institutions reflects not merely the unequal distribution of financial resources but, more fundamentally, the systematic reproduction of cultural capital — the tacit knowledge, the linguistic confidence, the horizon of legitimate aspiration — that elite environments both require and produce.

① not merely X but, more fundamentally, Y — 'not merely... but' + sentence adverb внутри: Y значительно важнее X

② the tacit knowledge, the linguistic confidence, the horizon of legitimate aspiration — tricolon: три параллельных NP, каждый с определителем

③ that elite environments both require and produce — 'both X and Y': двойное включение в одном relative clause

④ cultural capital — Bourdieu's term: precise sociological register signals TA
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Education is not only important for jobs but also it helps society.

Правильно: Education is important not only for individual economic outcomes but also for the reproduction of the social trust upon which democratic governance depends.

Почему: Параллелизм нарушен: 'for jobs' (PP) vs 'it helps society' (clause). 'Not only X but also Y' требует одинаковой синтаксической формы для X и Y

Ошибка: Neither the government or the private sector can solve this alone.

Правильно: Neither the government nor the private sector, acting in isolation, possesses the capacity to address the structural roots of educational inequality.

Почему: 'Neither... or' — неправильно. Только: 'neither... nor'. 'Neither... or' — распространённая ошибка, немедленно замечается экзаменатором
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Correlative Conjunctions

✔ 'Not X but Y' для коррекции ложной дихотомии → Band 8 TA: автор точно формулирует реальную проблему

✔ 'Not merely X but Y' → показывает концептуальную глубину: X верно, но недостаточно

✔ Fronted 'Not only' + inversion → комбинация C9 + C17 = редкая, безупречно формальная структура
⚠ Снижает балл: 'Not only... but also' с нарушенным параллелизмом (разные синтаксические формы) — систематическая ошибка Band 6; снижает GRA

📙 Grammar 2 · Comparative Parallelism: симметрия через сравнение

The more X, the more Y · The greater X, the more Y · As X increases, so Y · X, just as Y
🟣

Сравнительные параллельные структуры — это не просто грамматика.

В уровне C они становятся аналитическим инструментом: автор не просто утверждает корреляцию — он структурно демонстрирует её через форму самого предложения.
Конструкция Значение Синтаксические требования Пример
The more X, the more/less Y Пропорциональная зависимость: рост X → рост/падение Y Оба клауза начинаются с 'the + comparative'; подлежащее может быть в любой позиции The more stratified an education system becomes, the less it functions as an instrument of social mobility and the more it operates as a mechanism for the intergenerational reproduction of advantage.
The greater/higher/deeper X, the more/greater Y Та же логика с существительными вместо прилагательных 'The + comparative adj + NP' вместо 'The more + adj' The greater the degree of socioeconomic segregation in schooling, the more pronounced the divergence in cognitive and non-cognitive outcomes between advantaged and disadvantaged pupils.
As X increases/grows, so Y increases/grows Параллельный рост: оба движутся вместе 'As + S + V, so + V + S' (инверсия в второй клаузе — опционально) As access to higher education has expanded, so too has the value placed on postgraduate credentials — a development that risks simply displacing, rather than resolving, the bottleneck of credentialism.
Just as X, so Y / X, just as Y Аналогическое сравнение: Y работает по той же логике, что X 'Just as' + full clause + 'so' + full clause; или Y, just as X Just as the expansion of secondary education in the twentieth century failed to deliver the meritocratic society its proponents promised, so the current expansion of higher education shows every sign of reproducing a more credentialised version of the same hierarchies.
X no less than Y / X no more than Y Уравнивание или преуменьшение через отрицательное сравнение Parallel NPs or VPs The structural barriers to educational attainment are a consequence of policy choice no less than of resource constraint — and are therefore no less amenable to policy intervention.

⚡ The more X, the more Y: точность синтаксиса

Базовая форма: 'The more unequal a society, the lower the social mobility.'

Расширенная (Band 8): 'The more entrenched the socioeconomic stratification of schooling, the less likely it is that educational institutions will function as genuine engines of social mobility rather than as sophisticated instruments of its simulation.'

Анализ расширенной формы:

'The more entrenched' — comparative adj + complex NP: не просто 'unequal' а 'entrenched socioeconomic stratification'

'the less likely it is that' — it-extraposition: формально и гибко

'rather than as' — corrective clause добавляет второй элемент контраста

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The greater the degree to which schooling systems sort pupils by socioeconomic background at an early age, the more deeply those systems embed the very inequalities they are ostensibly designed to address — and, crucially, the less likely it is that any subsequent policy intervention, however well-designed, will be capable of reversing outcomes that have been structurally determined long before formal education begins.

① The greater the degree to which — 'the greater' + complex NP with relative clause: akademische Tiefe

② the more deeply those systems embed — 'the more + adverb + V': parallel comparative clause

③ the very inequalities they are ostensibly designed to address — 'the very' для иронического контраста; sentence adverb 'ostensibly' (C13)

④ however well-designed — concessive clause (embedded): даже хорошая политика бессильна
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: The more education people get, the more they will earn money.

Правильно: The greater the level of educational attainment, the higher the probability of sustained upward occupational mobility — though the relationship is mediated by the type and prestige of credentials acquired.

Почему: Параллельная структура требует NP в обеих клаузах: 'The greater X, the higher Y'. 'The more education people get' — лучше: 'The higher the level of educational attainment'

Ошибка: As inequality grows, also social mobility decreases.

Правильно: As structural inequality deepens, so the capacity of educational systems to counteract its effects diminishes — suggesting that education and redistribution must be pursued as complementary rather than alternative strategies.

Почему: 'As X grows, also Y' — неправильно. Формула: 'As X grows, so Y grows/diminishes'. Инверсия 'so + V + S' — опциональна, но 'also' не работает в этой позиции
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Comparative Parallelism

✔ 'The greater X, the more Y' вместо 'If X increases, Y will increase' → немедленный Band 8 GRA: структура несёт аргумент

✔ 'Just as X, so Y' для исторической аналогии → сильнейший TA-инструмент: принцип из прошлого применяется к настоящему

✔ 'As X grows, so too does Y' — 'so too' → более формально, чем 'so also'; LR Band 7+
⚠ Снижает балл: 'The more X, the more Y' с нарушенным параллелизмом (разные синтаксические типы) — GRA снижается; строгость формы обязательна

📗 Grammar 3 · Tricolon & Anaphoric Parallelism

Три элемента · Anaphora (повторение в начале) · Epistrophe (повторение в конце) · Chiasmus
🟣

Параллелизм не ограничивается двумя элементами.

В уровне C мы изучаем трёхэлементные структуры (tricolon) и риторические фигуры повторения, которые придают академическому тексту ритмическую убедительность.
Приём Структура Риторическая функция Пример
Tricolon (ascending) A, B, and C — каждый элемент сильнее предыдущего Кульминация: третий элемент — самый значимый Educational systems must address access, quality, and — above all — the structural reproduction of inequality that renders both access and quality insufficient without redistribution.
Tricolon (balanced) A, B, and C — все элементы равнозначны Полнота охвата: три аспекта одного явления Social mobility requires material resources, institutional opportunity, and the cultural capital that mediates between the two.
Anaphora Повторение слова/фразы в начале последовательных клауз Нагнетание, акцент, риторическое убеждение Not the absence of talent in disadvantaged communities, not the unwillingness of individuals to invest in their own development, but the systematic suppression of the conditions under which both talent and investment can yield returns.
Epistrophe Повторение слова/фразы в конце последовательных клауз Эхо: одна идея звучит настойчиво Education that reproduces hierarchy is not education. A meritocracy that perpetuates advantage is not a meritocracy. A mobility agenda that leaves structures intact is not a mobility agenda.
Chiasmus (A-B / B-A) Перекрёстная симметрия: первый элемент переворачивается во втором Антитетическая глубина: реверс выявляет парадокс We do not reform education to create a more equal society; we require a more equal society to permit education reform to succeed.
Parallelism + ellipsis Параллельные клаузы с пропущенным общим элементом Ритм + плотность одновременно (C16 + C17) Access has increased; quality has not; equity has diminished — and the gap between the three has widened with each successive reform cycle.

📐 Anaphora в академическом тексте: тонкая грань

Anaphora в риторике — повторение начальных слов для нагнетания. В академическом тексте она работает иначе: повторение структуры (не обязательно слова) создаёт ощущение систематического перечисления:

Слабая anaphora: 'Not talent is missing. Not ambition is missing. Not resources are missing.'

Сильная anaphora: 'Not the absence of talent among disadvantaged populations, not the insufficiency of individual motivation, but the structural organisation of opportunity itself — this is what educational reform must address.'

Разница: сильная форма использует сложные NP (C12) внутри каждого элемента, а не простые клаузы — это анафора плюс номинализация плюс фронтинг (C9).

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

It is not the intellectual capacity of students from disadvantaged backgrounds that is found wanting; not their willingness to invest effort in educational attainment; not, even, the dedication of the teachers who work within under-resourced institutions — but the structural architecture of a system that simultaneously claims to reward merit and systematically ensures that merit itself is unequally distributed.

① Not X; not Y; not, even, Z — tripartite anaphora with ascending intensity: 'even' marks the third as most surprising

② but the structural architecture of a system — the 'but' closes the anaphoric series: all three negations lead here

③ that simultaneously claims to... and systematically ensures that — parallel relative clauses with correlative contrast ('claims to X and ensures that not-X')

④ merit itself is unequally distributed — chiastic paradox: the criterion of meritocracy is itself unmeritocratically distributed
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Education needs more money, teachers should be better, and also schools need resources.

Правильно: Educational reform demands sustained financial investment, the systematic professionalisation of teaching, and a fundamental restructuring of the resource allocation mechanisms that currently perpetuate inequality.

Почему: Tricolon требует строгого параллелизма: все три элемента одного синтаксического типа. 'Money' (NP) / 'teachers should be better' (clause) / 'schools need resources' (clause) — три разных типа

Ошибка: We need access. We also need quality. We also need equity.

Правильно: What educational reform demands is not merely expanded access, nor improved quality in isolation, but the coordinated pursuit of both within a framework that places equity — not efficiency — at its normative centre.

Почему: Повторение 'We need' — слабая anaphora с личным местоимением. В академическом тексте: используй NP anaphora ('Not X, not Y, but Z') или 'What is required is not X but Y'
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Tricolon & Anaphora

✔ Tricolon с ascending intensity → финальный элемент получает максимальную риторическую силу (TA + CC)

✔ Anaphora с complex NPs (не простыми словами) → сигнал C12 + C17 = Band 8 GRA + LR

✔ Chiasmus для парадокса → демонстрирует концептуальную изощрённость; редко используется студентами ниже Band 8
⚠ Снижает балл: Триколон с нарушенным параллелизмом хуже, чем одно хорошо сформулированное предложение — форма должна работать, а не украшать

📕 Grammar 4 · Rhetorical Architecture & Band 8 Upgrade

Как симметрия, контраст и ритм работают вместе в академическом аргументе
🟣

Параллельные структуры — это не украшение.

В Grammar 4 мы видим, как они становятся аргументативной архитектурой: симметрия формы создаёт симметрию мысли — и читатель это чувствует как убедительность.
Аргументативная задача Структура Пример Эффект
Опровергнуть ложную причину Not X but Y Educational failure is not a consequence of individual inadequacy but of the structural organisation of opportunity. Прямое, точное, необратимое опровержение
Показать двойной провал Neither X nor Y Neither expanded access nor improved pedagogical quality, in isolation, constitutes an adequate response to structural inequality. Отклоняет оба ложных решения одним движением
Установить пропорциональную зависимость The greater X, the more Y The greater the degree of early tracking in school systems, the more permanently those systems determine the life chances of children. Структура несёт причинность: форма = аргумент
Применить исторический принцип к настоящему Just as X, so Y Just as the grammar school system reproduced class advantage under the rhetoric of meritocracy, so selective university admissions systems risk doing today. Историческая аналогия как предупреждение
Создать кульминацию аргумента Ascending tricolon + anaphora Not the shortage of resources, not the absence of political will, but — above all — the structural indifference to the question of whose interests educational systems actually serve. Нагнетание + кульминация = максимальная риторическая сила

📐 Band 8 Conclusion: полная риторическая архитектура

Предложение 1 — Not X but Y (коррекция): What educational reform demands is not merely expanded access — though access matters — but a fundamental redistribution of the cultural, institutional, and material resources through which educational outcomes are determined.

Предложение 2 — The greater X, the more Y (причинность): The longer governments defer this redistribution, the more deeply entrenched the structural barriers to mobility become — and the more costly, in both social and economic terms, the eventual reckoning.

Предложение 3 — Just as X, so Y (исторический урок): Just as the expansion of formal education in the twentieth century failed to resolve the class divisions it promised to transcend, so the current expansion of credentialised access risks generating, at greater expense, a more elaborate version of the same outcome.

Предложение 4 — Ascending tricolon (кульминация): What is required is not reform of curricula, not expansion of institutions, but a serious political commitment to dismantling the structural conditions under which educational advantage is systematically reproduced across generations.
🚀 Band 8 Upgrade

Band 6: Education is important for social mobility. Governments should improve education and this will help poor people to succeed. It is not fair that rich people have better education.

Band 7: Educational inequality represents a significant barrier to social mobility. Governments must address both access and quality, as neither alone is sufficient to dismantle the structural barriers that disadvantaged students face.

Band 8: Educational inequality is not a consequence of differential effort or innate capacity — it is the systematic product of a social arrangement that distributes not merely material advantage but the cultural capital, the institutional familiarity, and the horizons of legitimate aspiration upon which educational success depends. Not more funding alone, not more qualified teachers alone, but a fundamental restructuring of the conditions under which educational outcomes are determined: this, and nothing less, is what equity in education demands.
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Full Rhetorical Architecture

✔ Not X but Y + The greater X, the more Y + Just as X, so Y + tricolon в одном заключении → Band 8 GRA + CC

✔ Параллельные структуры, несущие аргумент (не просто украшение) → высший TA: форма и содержание едины

✔ Ascending tricolon в финальном предложении → читатель чувствует завершённость и убедительность
⚠ Снижает балл: Параллельные структуры без смыслового содержания — пустая риторика; форма должна служить мысли, а не заменять её

📖 Reading 1 · The Myth of Meritocracy

Академическое чтение: параллельные структуры как аргументативная архитектура
📖 The Myth of Meritocracy: Educational Systems, Cultural Capital, and the Reproduction of Privilege

It is not the intelligence of children from disadvantaged socioeconomic backgrounds that is measured and found wanting by elite educational systems; nor is it their motivation, their resilience, or their capacity for intellectual engagement. What is being measured — and what such systems are, in large part, designed to reward — is the possession of a particular form of cultural capital: the ease with which privileged children navigate institutional environments that have been constructed, often unconsciously, in their image. The greater the degree to which educational assessment rewards this tacit familiarity rather than the cognitive capacities it purports to measure, the more effectively the system reproduces the social hierarchies it claims to transcend.

Not merely the content of what is taught, but the manner in which it is taught, the expectations embedded in how learning is assessed, and the cultural assumptions that pervade the institutional environment — all of these systematically advantage students whose home environments have already prepared them for precisely this kind of engagement. As educational credentials have become both more widely distributed and more economically necessary, so too have the informal markers of cultural distinction that allow elite institutions and employers to differentiate among an increasingly credentialised applicant pool.

Just as the expansion of secondary education in the mid-twentieth century failed to deliver the classless society its proponents envisioned — producing instead a more credentialised version of the existing class hierarchy — so the expansion of higher education risks generating the same outcome at greater cost. Neither structural reform alone nor funding increases alone is adequate; what is required is, above all, a serious political commitment to challenging the cultural frameworks through which educational advantage is distributed, transmitted, and — crucially — legitimated as the natural reward of individual merit.

📖 Reading 2 · What Educational Reform Cannot Do Alone

Параллельные структуры и ограничения аргумента
📖 What Educational Reform Cannot Do Alone: The Structural Limits of Meritocratic Policy

It is one of the characteristic features of contemporary policy discourse on social mobility that educational reform is simultaneously treated as the primary mechanism for addressing structural inequality and subjected to criticism for its persistent failure to deliver the outcomes it promises. This apparent paradox is, however, less contradictory than it appears: the more that education is asked to compensate for structural inequalities that originate outside the school system, the less adequate any purely educational response will prove to be. Not merely is this a structural limitation of educational policy; it is, more fundamentally, a symptom of a political unwillingness to address the social and economic conditions that make educational advantage heritable in the first place.

Just as it would be unreasonable to expect medicine to compensate for poverty-driven ill health without simultaneously addressing its social determinants, so it is unreasonable to expect educational systems to generate social mobility while the wider social environment systematically reproduces the conditions that render such mobility structurally improbable. Neither expanding university access nor improving school quality, however necessary both may be, is capable of resolving an inequality that is reproduced not merely through educational institutions but through the entire complex of housing markets, labour markets, cultural industries, and social networks through which advantage is transmitted and consolidated.

What is required, therefore, is not a choice between educational reform and structural economic redistribution — the former without the latter is insufficient, the latter without the former is incomplete — but the coordinated and sustained pursuit of both within a political framework that refuses to accept the existing distribution of advantage as either natural or permanent. The greater the political commitment to such a framework, the more likely it is that educational reform will generate the outcomes its advocates promise; the weaker that commitment, the more certainly educational reform will reproduce — at public expense and with the consent of its beneficiaries — precisely the hierarchies it purports to dismantle.

✍️ Writing Task 1 · Bar Chart Description

Параллельные структуры в описании данных: сравнение через симметрию
✍️ Writing Task 1 · Bar Chart (170 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

The bar chart below shows the percentage of students from the highest and lowest socioeconomic quintiles who completed upper secondary education and who enrolled in higher education in six countries in 2022. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

The data reveal a pattern of consistent and, in several cases, striking socioeconomic divergence in educational participation: not merely at the point of upper secondary completion — where the gap between the highest and lowest quintiles averages 22 percentage points across the six countries — but, more consequentially, at the point of higher education enrolment, where the differential widens to an average of 34 points, suggesting that each successive educational transition amplifies rather than diminishes the initial disadvantage.

① not merely at the point of X... but, more consequentially, at the point of Y — 'not merely... but' parallel PP structure

② amplifies rather than diminishes — correlative contrast: 'rather than' shows the system worsens, not corrects

③ the initial disadvantage — nominal substitute (C16): avoids repeating the full description

④ the differential widens to an average of 34 points — precise academic register: 'differential' not 'gap difference'

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Два уровня перехода сравниваются; тенденция усиления неравенства выявлена

CC (7–8): 'Not merely X but Y' создаёт структурную прогрессию от secondary к tertiary

LR (7–8): socioeconomic quintiles, differential, enrolment, amplifies, participation — точный академический регистр

GRA (7–8): 'Not merely... but' + 'rather than' + participial clause в одном сложном предложении

📐 Структура эссе:

Overview (2 пр.): 'Not merely X but Y': двухуровневая структура. 'The greater the socioeconomic gap, the more pronounced...'

Body 1 (3–4 пр.): 'Both X and Y show...': страны с наименьшим расхождением. Comparative parallelism.

Body 2 (3–4 пр.): 'Just as upper secondary gaps are large, so higher education gaps are larger still.' Ascending tricolon для итогов.

✍️ Writing Task 2 · Problem-Solution Essay

Риторическая архитектура в аргументативном эссе
✍️ Writing Task 2 · Problem-Solution Essay (270 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

In many countries, students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds are significantly less likely to complete higher education than their more advantaged peers, despite having similar academic ability. What are the main reasons for this, and what measures could be taken to address the problem?

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

The underrepresentation of students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds in higher education is attributable not merely to financial barriers — though such barriers are real and substantial — but, more fundamentally, to the systematic attenuation of educational aspiration that occurs when individuals are raised in environments that have been, structurally and culturally, organised around the assumption that higher education is not for people like them.

① not merely to X — though X is real — but, more fundamentally, to Y — 'not merely... but' with embedded concession

② the systematic attenuation of educational aspiration — complex nominalised NP (C12): 'aspiration is attenuated'

③ environments that have been, structurally and culturally, organised around the assumption that — parallel adverbs + complex relative

④ higher education is not for people like them — indirect discourse: the internalised voice of structural exclusion

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Причины структурные (не индивидуальные); меры адресуют структуру, а не симптомы

CC (7–8): Not merely X but Y (причины) → Neither X alone nor Y alone (меры) → ascending tricolon (выводы)

LR (7–8): attenuation, aspiration, credential, intergenerational, structural — C2 лексика

GRA (7–8): 'Not merely... but' + embedded concession + complex NP + 'neither X nor Y' в разных частях

📐 Структура эссе:

Intro (2 пр.): Not merely X but Y для постановки проблемы. Тезис через 'what is required is not X but Y'.

Reasons (3–4 пр.): The greater X, the more Y для механизма передачи неравенства. Tricolon для трёх уровней барьеров.

Solutions (3–4 пр.): Neither X alone nor Y alone. Both X and Y must be pursued. Just as X worked in context A, so Y could work here.

Conclusion (2 пр.): Ascending anaphora + chiasmus финального тезиса.

🎓 Урок C17 завершён!

✅ Correlative conjunctions: Not only X but Y / Not X but Y / Not merely X but Y / Not so much X as Y / Both X and Y / Neither X nor Y — каждый создаёт разные риторические весы

✅ Comparative parallelism: The more/greater X, the more Y · As X grows, so Y · Just as X, so Y · X no less than Y — форма несёт причинность и аналогию

✅ Tricolon & anaphora: ascending tricolon / balanced tricolon / anaphoric series / epistrophe / chiasmus — ритм как аргумент

✅ Rhetorical Architecture: четыре структуры в одном заключительном абзаце создают профессиональную убедительность

✅ Band 8 Parallelism = строгий синтаксический параллелизм + смысловая необходимость формы + риторический эффект

💡 Central Question — Ответ:

Симметрия делает аргумент убедительнее по одной причине: когда форма отражает структуру мысли, читатель не просто понимает — он воспринимает мысль как неизбежную. 'Not X but Y' не просто опровергает X — структура опровержения встроена в форму предложения. 'The more X, the more Y' не просто утверждает корреляцию — она демонстрирует её через синтаксическую симметрию. Именно это и есть риторическая сила параллельных структур: мысль и форма — одно.
