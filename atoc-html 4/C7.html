Урок C7 · Upper-Intermediate / Advanced

Ellipsis & Substitution

so / not / do so / one / ones — академическая компрессия

Что остаётся, когда убираешь всё лишнее?

Тематика: Демография · Словарь: C1–C2 · Writing: Mixed Data + Problem-Solution (260 сл.)

🎯 Зачем это нужно? — Момент озарения

Прочитай два фрагмента. Оба говорят одно и то же:

Band 6: "Some countries invest in pension reform. Other countries do not invest in pension reform. Countries that invest in pension reform have better outcomes. Countries that do not invest in pension reform have worse outcomes."

Band 8: "Countries that invest systematically in pension reform tend to achieve considerably more resilient demographic outcomes than those that do not — and the evidence suggests that those which delay doing so face compounding fiscal pressures that grow progressively more difficult to address."

В первом фрагменте 'pension reform' повторяется четыре раза. Во втором — один раз. Всё остальное — do not, those that, doing so — это система замены: не упрощение, а элегантность. Ellipsis и substitution превращают тяжёлый повтор в текучую академическую прозу. Именно это экзаменатор называет 'skilful use of cohesive devices'.

📐 Grammar 1 · VP Ellipsis — do so / do not / auxiliary ellipsis

«Some do; others do not» — замена глагольной группы

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне A ты использовал do/does/did как вспомогательные глаголы в вопросах и отрицаниях.

В уровне B ты познакомился с эмфатическим do (C4).

В уровне C VP ellipsis — это третья функция do: замена всей глагольной группы, уже упомянутой в тексте. Разница: эмфатическое do ДОБАВЛЯЕТ акцент; elliptic do ЗАМЕНЯЕТ повтор. Это принципиально разные операции.
Конструкция Что заменяет Пример Band 8 Академический эффект
do so повторный VP с теми же аргументами Governments in rapidly ageing societies face mounting pressure to reform pension systems. Those that do so proactively — by raising contribution rates and extending working lives before fiscal crisis forces the issue — consistently achieve more sustainable long-term demographic outcomes than those that defer doing so until structural imbalances become acute. Элегантная замена 'reform pension systems' — дважды в одном предложении
do not / does not / did not отрицание VP без повтора The demographic dividend that rapid urbanisation once generated in East Asian economies has largely been exhausted; the structural conditions that made rapid industrialisation possible for that cohort of developing nations no longer exist for those currently attempting to replicate the model, and many of the policy instruments that proved effective in that context do not transfer readily to contemporary circumstances. Отрицание через auxiliary: компактно и формально
Auxiliary alone (will / would / can / could / may / might / should) VP после модального Population ageing will place unprecedented strain on public health infrastructure. In countries with predominantly private healthcare systems, it already does. Голый auxiliary как полное предложение — высокая компрессия
have / has / had (perfect ellipsis) Perfect VP уже упомянутый Several OECD nations have substantially reformed their public pension architectures in response to demographic pressures; others have not, whether for reasons of political economy or institutional inertia. 'Have not' заменяет 'have not reformed' — чистая элипсис

⚡ Do so vs. Do it vs. Do this — академические различия

Форма Пример Регистр / Ограничения
do so Govts should reform pensions. Those that do so early achieve better outcomes. Формальный, академический — Band 7–8. Для действий/процессов.
do it The policy was controversial. Few countries did it. Разговорный — Band 5–6. Избегать в IELTS Writing Task 2.
do this Reform is necessary. Countries that do this will benefit. Нейтральный — Band 6–7. Приемлем, но менее элегантен, чем do so.
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
Those countries that do so reform their pension systems... Those countries that do so achieve more resilient fiscal positions. Do so уже заменило VP — не нужно снова называть то, что оно заменяет.
Governments should act. Those which don't will suffer. Governments that fail to act proactively will face compounding fiscal pressures; those that do not may find structural reform progressively more difficult to achieve. 'Don't' — разговорная форма. В академическом тексте: do not / fail to.
Some countries reformed. Others did so not. Some countries have implemented reforms; others have not. Порядок слов: do not / does not / did not — отрицание идёт непосредственно после auxiliary, не после so.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Pronatalist policies have been adopted by several European governments in response to below-replacement fertility rates; others have declined to do so, whether on grounds of fiscal constraint, ideological aversion to demographic engineering, or scepticism about the empirical evidence for their effectiveness — a scepticism that, where it has prevailed, has not, it should be noted, been consistently vindicated by subsequent demographic trajectories.
① do so — заменяет 'adopt pronatalist policies'; формально и элегантно
② whether on grounds of X, Y, or Z — триколон (C18) объясняет причины без VP повтора
③ a scepticism that — nominalization + relative clause: анафорическая ссылка
④ has not... been consistently vindicated — perfect ellipsis внутри relative clause
⑤ it should be noted — comment clause (C13 preview): академический метакомментарий

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ Do so (вместо повторного VP) → +CC: skilful cohesive device; +GRA: VP ellipsis

✅ have / have not (perfect ellipsis) → +GRA: показывает контроль над auxiliary system

✅ Those that do / those that do not — параллельная структура → +CC: ритм и баланс

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Смешивать do so (формальное) и do it (разговорное) в одном эссе — register inconsistency, снижает LR

📐 Grammar 2 · Clausal Substitution — so / not

«I believe so» / «I believe not» — замена целого придаточного

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне B ты встречал 'I think so' и 'I hope not' как разговорные формы.

В уровне C академическая версия этих конструкций — с глаголами appear, seem, suggest, indicate, demonstrate, expect, anticipate — создаёт элегантную clausal substitution, которая звучит профессионально и избегает повтора целых придаточных предложений.
Структура Что заменяет Пример Band 8 Академический глагол
V + so That + clause (affirmative) Whether pronatalist fiscal incentives can meaningfully alter fertility decisions among well-educated urban cohorts remains contested; the available cross-national evidence would appear to suggest so, albeit with effect sizes considerably more modest than their advocates typically claim. appear to suggest so / seem to indicate so / would appear to do so
V + not That + clause (negative) Critics of mass immigration as a demographic solution argue that cultural integration at scale is not feasible; the empirical record of several Northern European nations would suggest not, though the conditions enabling successful integration are neither universal nor self-replicating. suggest not / indicate not / appear not
if so / if not Conditional reference to previous clause The fiscal viability of pay-as-you-go pension systems depends critically on the dependency ratio remaining within sustainable bounds. If so, gradual parametric reform may suffice; if not, systemic structural transformation of retirement income architecture will become unavoidable. if so / if not — conditional bridge между двумя предложениями
whether or not Двойная замена (both outcomes) The question of whether declining fertility rates reflect genuine preference shifts or structural constraints on family formation — and whether or not policy can meaningfully distinguish between the two — has significant implications for the design of pronatalist interventions. whether or not + NP/clause

⚡ Глаголы, совместимые с so/not в академическом регистре

✅ Академические (Band 7–8) ⚠️ Нейтральные (Band 6) ❌ Разговорные (избегать)

seem to indicate so

would appear to do so

the evidence suggests not

one might expect so

❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
The data suggest so this trend continues. The data suggest that this trend will continue / The data would appear to suggest so. 'Suggest so' уже заменяет придаточное. Нельзя иметь оба: 'so' + 'this trend continues'.
If so or not, the policy needs revision. Whether or not the policy achieves its intended outcomes, fundamental revision appears warranted. 'If so or not' — неграмматично. Правильно: 'whether or not' или раздельно: 'if so... if not...'.
I think not the government should act. The available evidence would suggest not — though a stronger normative case for intervention can be made on grounds of intergenerational equity. 'Think not' требует инверсии или просто 'I think not'. Но в академическом IELTS избегай I think — используй evidential глаголы.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Whether declining fertility rates in post-industrial economies represent a structural feature of late modernity or a cyclical response to temporary economic conditions remains a matter of genuine scholarly debate; the weight of longitudinal evidence would appear to suggest the former, though demographers who contest this reading argue not unreasonably that the heterogeneity of national trajectories resists any single-factor explanation.
① the former — nominal substitution: заменяет 'the proposition that declining fertility is structural'
② would appear to suggest — hedged epistemic verb + so (implied): академическая версия 'suggests'
③ argue not unreasonably — double negative: изящная форма 'argue with some reason'
④ that the heterogeneity... resists any single-factor explanation — nominalization + academic precision
⑤ any single-factor explanation — 'any' как universal quantifier: риторически более сильно, чем 'a simple'

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ appear to suggest so / would seem to indicate so → +LR: epistemic hedging + substitution в одной конструкции

✅ if so / if not — conditional bridge → +CC: создаёт сценарную логику между предложениями

✅ whether or not → +GRA: показывает контроль над nominal clauses и substitution

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Использовать 'so' substitution с глаголами типа 'know', 'understand', 'realise' — эти глаголы не принимают clausal substitution через 'so' в письменном академическом регистре

✏️ Задание 2.1 — Clausal elegance

Перепишите следующие фрагменты, используя so/not или if so/if not:

1. «Some demographers argue that immigration can solve demographic decline. Other demographers argue that immigration cannot solve demographic decline.» → используй suggest so / suggest not

2. «It is possible that urbanisation suppresses fertility rates in developing economies. If urbanisation suppresses fertility rates, then pronatalist policies in rural areas may be less effective.» → используй if so

3. «We do not know whether age-related cognitive decline is inevitable or preventable. The answer to whether age-related cognitive decline is inevitable or preventable has significant implications for healthcare planning.» → используй whether or not

📐 Grammar 3 · Nominal Substitution — one / ones / the former / the latter / such

«the former... the latter» — замена именной группы

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне B: использование местоимений (it, they, this, that) для ссылки на ранее сказанное.

В уровне C nominal substitution — это более точный и формальный набор инструментов: one/ones заменяет классификационную NP, the former/latter устанавливает бинарную структуру, such создаёт категориальную ссылку. Каждый выполняет разную дискурсивную работу.
Инструмент Что заменяет Пример Band 8 Функция
one / ones Countable NP с классификационным смыслом Two broad models of demographic adjustment have been proposed: a supply-side one, focused on increasing labour force participation among older workers, and a demand-side one, prioritising the reallocation of resources from consumption to productive investment in human capital. Разграничивает два варианта одной категории: one = 'model'
the former / the latter Два ранее упомянутых элемента Population ageing and declining fertility are frequently conflated in demographic discourse; the former, however, is principally a function of increasing longevity, while the latter reflects complex interactions between economic development, gender norms, and housing markets. Бинарное различение: the former = 'population ageing'; the latter = 'declining fertility'
such Категориальная ссылка на тип Effective demographic policy requires a combination of short-term fiscal measures and long-term structural reforms. Such policies, to be sustainable, must command cross-party political support — a condition rarely met in systems characterised by short electoral cycles. Such = 'policies of the type just described'; более формально, чем 'these policies'
that of / those of Сравнительная NP-замена The dependency ratio of Japan — a nation that reached demographic maturity considerably earlier than most comparable economies — now substantially exceeds that of any other OECD member, with projections suggesting that of South Korea will surpass it within two decades. that of / those of = заменяет повторную NP в сравнении

⚡ The former / the latter — правила использования

1. The former/latter работают только с ДВУМЯ ранее упомянутыми элементами — не с тремя и более.

2. Элементы должны быть упомянуты непосредственно в предшествующем предложении или clause.

3. The former = первый упомянутый; the latter = второй упомянутый — никогда не наоборот.

4. В академическом тексте, если читателю может быть неясно, что именно заменяется, лучше повторить NP, чем рисковать потерей ясности.

✅ «Between immigration and pronatalism, the former tends to produce faster demographic effects; the latter, more culturally contentious ones.»

❌ «Three approaches exist: immigration, pronatalism, and automation. The former involves...» — с тремя элементами the former/latter не работает.
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
One policy is better than other. One approach is considerably more effective than the other / ...than the alternative. 'Other' без артикля — неграмматично. С one: the other. Или используй 'the alternative'.
The former and latter both have merits. Both approaches have merit, though the former is more immediately practicable. 'The former and latter' — избыточная пара. Если оба имеют достоинства — скажи 'both'. Резервируй former/latter для различения.
Such a policy is good. Such policies, properly implemented, have demonstrated measurable effectiveness in several comparable jurisdictions. 'Such a policy is good' — разговорная оценка. В академическом тексте: 'Such policies, when X, have demonstrated Y' + конкретика.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Two competing paradigms dominate contemporary demographic policy debate: the expansionist model, which advocates immigration-led demographic replenishment, and the natalist one, which prioritises structural incentives for indigenous population growth. The former has demonstrated greater short-term efficacy in sustaining labour force participation rates in ageing economies; the latter, while politically more tractable in culturally homogeneous societies, has produced consistently modest fertility effects across the range of OECD nations in which such policies have been systematically evaluated.
① the natalist one — 'one' заменяет 'model': различает две версии категории
② The former / the latter — бинарное различение двух парадигм: чёткость без повторения NP
③ the latter, while politically more tractable — concession внутри nominal substitution
④ in which such policies have been systematically evaluated — relative clause + 'such' как категориальная ссылка
⑤ modest fertility effects — precise academic understatement: 'modest' вместо 'small' или 'limited'

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ The former / the latter → +CC: бинарная структура организует сравнение без повтора

✅ Such + N → +LR: формальная категориальная ссылка; сигнал академического регистра

✅ that of / those of (сравнительная) → +GRA: sophisticated comparative NP substitution

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Использовать the former/latter для трёх и более элементов — грамматически некорректно и дезориентирует читателя; снижает CC

📐 Grammar 4 · Nominal Ellipsis + Полная интеграция C7

«the most effective [Ø]» — опущение существительного

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне A 'the best solution' — всегда с существительным. В уровне C ты можешь опустить существительное, если оно уже было упомянуто и читателю ясно, что имеется в виду.

Nominal ellipsis в академическом тексте создаёт ощущение авторской уверенности: тот, кто не повторяет, знает, что уже сказал достаточно.
Тип Полная форма → Редуцированная Пример Band 8 Условие
Determiner ellipsis the effective policy → the effective [Ø] Of the range of demographic policy instruments currently available to OECD governments, the most cost-effective and politically sustainable are those that address both the supply and demand dimensions of the problem simultaneously — a relatively uncommon combination, but not an impossible [Ø]. Adj/quantifier + noun уже упомянут; опускается при повторе
Post-modifier ellipsis policies that work → [Ø] that work The pension reforms that have proven most durable are not necessarily the most structurally ambitious but [Ø] most carefully calibrated to the specific institutional constraints of each national context. NP опускается когда relative clause достаточно идентифицирует референт
Comparative ellipsis more effective than X is → more effective than [Ø] Targeted childcare subsidies have demonstrated more consistent fertility effects across comparable national contexts than universal cash transfers [Ø], possibly because the former address the structural barriers to family formation rather than simply supplementing household income. В сравнительных конструкциях повторная NP или VP опускается
Parallel structure ellipsis X does Y; Z does [Ø] Immigration-led demographic replenishment addresses workforce shortages rapidly; natalist policy, far more slowly [Ø]. VP полностью опускается в параллельной структуре: только наречие остаётся

Band 6 Many countries have an ageing population. This is a problem. Governments need to address this problem. Some governments are addressing this problem and some governments are not addressing this problem.

📌 Band 8 абзац: интеграция C5+C6+C7 — эталонный пример

The demographic challenge confronting post-industrial societies — whereby declining fertility rates and increasing longevity interact to produce structural imbalances in the dependency ratio — admits of no straightforward policy resolution, and those that claim otherwise do so, the evidence suggests, in service of political convenience rather than analytical rigour. Much as pronatalist fiscal incentives have demonstrated some capacity to influence fertility at the margins, the most thoroughly evaluated ones have produced effect sizes too modest to reverse structural trends without complementary immigration policy. Notwithstanding the political sensitivities that such a conclusion tends to generate, the longitudinal evidence would appear to suggest so — and countries that have declined to act on it have not, in general, found alternative instruments capable of substituting for what immigration-led demographic adjustment can provide. Were governments to integrate both approaches systematically, the compound effects might prove considerably more substantial than either can achieve alone.

② do so (C7 G1) — 'claim otherwise' без повтора VP

③ the most thoroughly evaluated ones (C7 G4) — nominal ellipsis: 'ones' заменяет 'incentives'

📖 READING 1 — Академический текст

Demographic Transition and the Limits of Policy

The relationship between economic development and fertility decline has been among the most extensively studied in demography, yet it remains one of the least well understood. The empirical regularity — that societies consistently move toward below-replacement fertility as they achieve higher levels of economic development — is robust; the mechanisms producing it are not. Whether declining fertility represents a rational response to the rising opportunity costs of parenthood, a cultural shift in life-course aspirations, or a structural consequence of inadequate institutional support for family formation is, the evidence suggests, not a question admitting of a single satisfying answer, and demographers who claim so are almost certainly overstating the parsimony of their preferred theoretical framework.

Policy responses to below-replacement fertility have taken two broad forms: pronatalist, designed to increase indigenous birth rates through fiscal and institutional incentives, and immigration-based, intended to offset demographic decline through managed population inflows. The former has, across the range of comparable national contexts in which such policies have been systematically evaluated, produced effects considerably more modest than their advocates anticipated; the latter has demonstrated greater short-term efficacy in sustaining labour force participation but has generated its own set of integration challenges — ones that, in several European contexts, have proven considerably more persistent than early optimism suggested.

Notwithstanding the considerable scholarly attention devoted to optimising each approach in isolation, it is perhaps the combination of the two — calibrated to the specific institutional and demographic conditions of each national context — that offers the most promising avenue for genuinely sustainable demographic adjustment. Whether governments will do so, given the political pressures that both approaches generate, remains to be seen; the demographic evidence, however, does not suggest that continued inaction represents a viable long-term option for any advanced economy currently operating at significantly below-replacement fertility.

📖 READING 2 — Академический текст

The Rhetoric of Demographic Anxiety

Demographic discourse has long been susceptible to a particular form of rhetorical inflation: the projection of current trends into futures sufficiently distant to generate alarm while remaining sufficiently plausible to resist dismissal. Population bomb narratives, greying society panics, and immigration crisis framings have in common that they select and amplify certain demographic signals while suppressing others — and, crucially, that those who construct such narratives frequently do so in service of policy prescriptions they would advocate on other grounds regardless.

It is worth asking, in this context, what the evidence actually suggests — and what it does not. The empirical regularities of demographic transition are, as noted, robust; the policy implications one draws from them are considerably less so. Two demographers examining identical data may arrive at radically different conclusions: one emphasising the fiscal unsustainability of current pension commitments, the other the historical resilience of advanced economies in adapting to structural demographic shifts. Both readings are defensible; neither is uniquely compelled by the data. The question is not which is correct, but which policy orientation each serves — and whether those orientations are acknowledged or concealed.

Such observations do not, of course, resolve the substantive policy questions raised by demographic change. They do, however, counsel a certain epistemic humility in the face of demographic projections presented with unqualified confidence — particularly those which claim to identify a single optimal policy response to a phenomenon as complex and multidimensional as population ageing. Granted that some degree of policy urgency is warranted, the case for any specific demographic intervention is considerably stronger when it acknowledges what the evidence does not establish, as well as what it does.

💡 Central Question — ответ:

Когда убираешь всё лишнее, остаётся структура мысли. Ellipsis и substitution — это не упрощение: это сигнал, что автор достаточно уверен в своём аргументе, чтобы не повторять его снова и снова. Экзаменатор видит именно это: не краткость ради краткости, а precision — способность сказать ровно столько, сколько нужно, и ни словом больше.
