[!] СВЯЗЬ С УРОВНЕМ A: В уровне A (урок A18) ты изучил базовый Reported Speech — сдвиг said/told/asked и простые трансформации времён. [G] Grammar 1 — Сдвиг времён в Reported Speech: полная система

Когда мы передаём чужую речь в прошедшем времени, времена сдвигаются назад на одну ступень. Это обязательное правило, которое журналисты и академические авторы применяют постоянно. Рассмотрим все 6 трансформаций с примерами из медиа.

Прямая речь (Direct) Reported Speech (Reported) Правило
"Emissions are rising." He said emissions were rising. Present Simple -> Past Simple
"Scientists are studying this." She reported scientists were studying this. Present Continuous -> Past Continuous
"Temperatures have increased." He noted temperatures had increased. Present Perfect -> Past Perfect
"The summit was held in Paris." She stated the summit had been held in Paris. Past Simple -> Past Perfect
"We will act immediately." He promised they would act immediately. will -> would
"Governments can do more." She argued governments could do more. can -> could

Исключения — сдвиг НЕ применяется: 1) Факты и универсальные истины: He said the Earth revolves around the Sun. 2) Если reporting verb в Present: She says that inflation is high. 3) Модальные глаголы would/could/might/should/ought to — не сдвигаются.

Исключение Пример Почему нет сдвига?
Научный факт He confirmed that CO2 absorbs heat. Постоянная научная истина
Reporting verb в Present The report states that emissions are increasing. Главный глагол — Present Simple
Модальный would/could She said the plan could succeed. Уже прошедшее — не сдвигается
Кавычки (direct quote) The minister stated: "We will not compromise." Прямая цитата — сдвига нет
[X] НЕПРАВИЛЬНО [OK] ПРАВИЛЬНО
She said that the results are published yesterday. (ОШИБКА: 'are published' нужно сдвинуть; 'yesterday' несовместимо с Present) She said that the results had been published the previous day. (Past Perfect для сдвига Past Simple + 'yesterday' -> 'the previous day')
[!] ЛАЙФХАК — СДВИГ ВРЕМЕНИ НАРЕЧИЙ: При переходе в Reported Speech меняются и наречия: now -> then, today -> that day, yesterday -> the previous day, tomorrow -> the following day, last week -> the previous week, here -> there, this -> that. Забытый сдвиг наречий — типичная ошибка Band 5!
[I] IELTS: В IELTS Task 1 (описание графиков) Reported Speech используется для передачи данных отчётов: 'The survey indicated that respondents believed living standards had declined.' Правильный сдвиг времён показывает точность и академичность.
[T] ЗАДАНИЕ 1: Трансформируй предложения в Reported Speech
> "Deforestation is accelerating in tropical regions." — The researcher announced...
> "We have discovered a new climate pattern." — Scientists reported...
> "The government will introduce new regulations." — Officials stated...
> "Media coverage can influence public opinion." — The professor argued...
> "Journalists were investigating the scandal yesterday." — Sources revealed...
> Не забудь сдвинуть наречия времени там, где они есть!

[G] Grammar 2 — Reporting Verbs: структуры, значения и оттенки

Reporting verbs — это глаголы, заменяющие said/told и передающие точный оттенок высказывания: обещание, предупреждение, отрицание, рекомендацию. В академических текстах и журналистике они критически важны для точности и разнообразия стиля.

Структура Глаголы Пример
verb + that claim, argue, confirm, deny, insist, reveal, suggest, warn, note, report She claimed that the data had been falsified.
verb + to-inf agree, demand, offer, promise, refuse, threaten, urge, advise The editor agreed to publish the correction.
verb + obj + to-inf advise, encourage, invite, persuade, remind, tell, urge, warn He urged journalists to verify their sources.
verb + -ing admit, deny, recommend, suggest, regret, acknowledge The minister denied leaking the information.
verb + obj + prep + -ing accuse (of), blame (for), congratulate (on) Critics accused the channel of misleading viewers.
Глагол Значение/оттенок Пример в контексте СМИ
claim утверждать (спорно) The tabloid claimed the minister had resigned.
reveal раскрывать (сенсация) The investigation revealed widespread corruption.
acknowledge признавать (неохотно) The CEO acknowledged the company had misled investors.
insist настаивать The spokesperson insisted no laws had been broken.
warn предупреждать Experts warned that misinformation was spreading rapidly.
deny отрицать The official denied any involvement in the scandal.
urge настоятельно призывать The editorial urged readers to question media bias.
concede уступать, признавать The politician conceded the policy had failed.
[X] НЕПРАВИЛЬНО [OK] ПРАВИЛЬНО
He suggested to improve the editorial standards. (ОШИБКА: suggest не принимает to-infinitive!) He suggested improving the editorial standards. (suggest + -ing) ИЛИ: He suggested that standards should be improved. (suggest + that)
[!] ЛАЙФХАК — КАК ВЫБРАТЬ REPORTING VERB: Спроси себя: 1) Каков оттенок? (критика -> accuse/blame, обещание -> promise/pledge, сомнение -> question/dispute). 2) Что после глагола? (that-clause, to-inf, -ing). Составь мини-таблицу из 10 любимых verbs — это ускорит академическое письмо в 2 раза.
[T] ЗАДАНИЕ 2: Замени said/told на подходящий reporting verb
> He said (= warned) that fake news was undermining democracy.
> She said (= admitted) that the headline had been exaggerated.
> The editor said (= urged) journalists to double-check facts.
> The politician said (= denied) any connection to the leaked documents.
> Critics said (= accused) the broadcaster of sensationalising the story.
> Для каждого предложения укажи структуру: verb+that / verb+to-inf / verb+-ing

[G] Grammar 3 — Reported Questions и репортажный стиль в академическом тексте

Reported Questions имеют особую структуру: порядок слов прямой (как в утвердительном предложении), без вопросительного знака, с сдвигом времён. В академических текстах репортажный стиль используется для ссылок на исследования.

Тип вопроса Прямой вопрос Reported Question Структура
Yes/No вопрос "Is the press free?" She asked whether the press was free. asked + whether/if + S + V
Wh- вопрос "What drives media bias?" He wondered what drove media bias. asked/wondered + wh- + S + V
Wh- с do/did "Where did the leak originate?" They investigated where the leak had originated. wh- + S + had + V3
Yes/No с aux "Has the story been verified?" Editors questioned whether the story had been verified. whether + S + had + V3

Ключевое правило: в Reported Questions вспомогательный глагол do/does/did исчезает, а глагол сдвигается: 'Where did he go?' -> She asked where he had gone. Порядок слов: Wh- + Subject + Verb (не Verb + Subject!).

Репортажная фраза Использование Пример
According to + источник Ссылка на источник According to the Reuters report, inflation had risen.
It is reported that Нейтральная передача It is reported that media ownership is increasingly concentrated.
The study suggests that Ссылка на исследование The study suggests that online news consumption has doubled.
Critics have argued that Передача критики Critics have argued that press freedom has declined.
The data indicate that Передача данных The data indicate that viewer trust has fallen sharply.
[X] НЕПРАВИЛЬНО [OK] ПРАВИЛЬНО
She asked where did the journalist find the documents. (ОШИБКА: прямой порядок слов в Reported Question — вспомогат. глагол исчезает!) She asked where the journalist had found the documents. (where + S + had + V3 — без do/did, с прямым порядком слов)
[!] ЛАЙФХАК — REPORTED vs ACADEMIC: В IELTS Writing Task 2 не используй 'He said that...' — это разговорно. Используй академические формулы: 'Research indicates that...', 'It has been suggested that...', 'Scholars have argued that...'. Это Lexical Resource Band 7, а не Band 5.
[I] IELTS: В IELTS Task 2 при написании аргументов используй: 'Proponents of this view contend that digital media has fundamentally altered journalistic standards.' Здесь contend + that-clause = академический Reported Speech без конкретного говорящего. Это более профессионально, чем 'People say that...'
[T] ЗАДАНИЕ 3: Трансформируй вопросы и реплики в академический стиль
> Reported Q: "Why do tabloids exaggerate headlines?" -> Researchers wondered...
> Reported Q: "Have social media platforms replaced traditional journalism?" -> The panel debated...
> Direct -> Academic: 'Many people say fake news is dangerous.' -> Scholars have argued that...
> Direct -> Academic: 'A new study says trust in media is falling.' -> According to a recent study...
> Составь 2 предложения для IELTS Task 2 о СМИ, используя It is reported / The data indicate

[G] Grammar 4 — Academic Language: Reporting в IELTS Task 1 и Task 2

Grammar 4 посвящён применению Reported Speech в обоих заданиях IELTS Writing. В Task 1 — передача данных и тенденций; в Task 2 — ссылки на мнения и исследования. Оба контекста требуют разных формул.

Контекст IELTS Формула Пример Band 7
Task 1 — данные графика The chart/data shows/indicates that... The data indicate that viewership declined steadily between 2010 and 2020.
Task 1 — тенденции It can be observed that... It can be observed that online news consumption has risen considerably.
Task 2 — чужое мнение Proponents argue / Critics contend that... Critics contend that social media has eroded journalistic integrity.
Task 2 — исследование Research/Studies suggest that... Studies suggest that media literacy programmes reduce susceptibility to misinformation.
Task 2 — общее мнение It is widely believed / generally acknowledged that... It is widely acknowledged that press freedom underpins democratic governance.
Task 2 — тезис It has been argued that... / This essay contends that... It has been argued that deregulation of media ownership poses significant risks.
[B] Слабый репортаж (Band 5) [P] Сильный репортаж (Band 7)
Many people say... It is widely contended that...
The news said that... According to media reports...
Experts told us that... Scholars have argued that...
The graph shows it went up. The data indicate a marked upward trend.

Образец Task 2 абзаца с Reported Speech (Band 7):

The relationship between media ownership and editorial independence has attracted considerable academic scrutiny. Scholars have argued that concentrated media ownership inevitably distorts news coverage, as proprietors are likely to favour reporting that aligns with their commercial and political interests. A landmark study by McChesney and Nichols contended that the consolidation of media corporations had fundamentally undermined the public interest function of journalism. Critics of this position, however, insist that market competition provides sufficient safeguards against bias. Nevertheless, it is widely acknowledged that where regulatory frameworks are weak, the risk of editorial interference remains substantial. This essay contends that stronger independent regulation is essential to preserve journalistic integrity in the digital age.

~100 слов — образец академического абзаца
[!] ЛАЙФХАК — РАЗНООБРАЗИЕ REPORTING VERBS в одном абзаце: Используй не более одного повтора одного и того же глагола. Схема для абзаца Task 2: argue -> contend -> insist -> acknowledge -> suggest. Пять разных verbs в одном абзаце = максимальный балл за Lexical Resource.
[I] IELTS: ACADEMIC UPGRADE: Сочетай Reported Speech с Passive для анонимности источника: 'It has been suggested that...' / 'It is reported that...' / 'It has been acknowledged that...' Это стандарт академического письма, который позволяет делать утверждения, не называя конкретного автора — полезно когда источник неизвестен или для обобщения.
[T] ЗАДАНИЕ 4: Academic Writing — репортажный стиль в IELTS
> Перепиши фрагмент, заменив все 'people say/think' на академические reported phrases:
> 'People say social media is changing journalism. People think fake news is a big problem.'
> Напиши 3-4 предложения о свободе прессы, используя: argue / contend / acknowledge / suggest
> Task 1 практика: опиши тенденцию (выбери любую) используя: indicate / reveal / demonstrate
> Самопроверка: нет ли у тебя двух одинаковых reporting verbs в одном абзаце?

[R] Reading 1 — The Changing Landscape of Journalism

Прочитай текст. Задание: а) выпиши все reporting verbs (не только said/told); б) определи структуру каждого (verb+that / verb+to-inf / verb+-ing); в) найди все случаи сдвига времён и определи исходное время.

Media analysts have long argued that the digital revolution has fundamentally transformed journalistic practice. A landmark Reuters Institute report revealed that, by 2022, more than half of adults in developed nations were accessing news primarily through social media platforms rather than traditional outlets. Critics contended that this shift had eroded the editorial standards that professional journalism had historically upheld. The report further suggested that news organisations were struggling to maintain financial viability as advertising revenue migrated to technology platforms. Several editors acknowledged that commercial pressures had influenced editorial decisions in ways that compromised independence. Meanwhile, press freedom advocates warned that governments in certain regions were exploiting the fragmentation of media to suppress dissenting voices. Researchers have recommended that media literacy be incorporated into school curricula, arguing that an informed public represents the most effective defence against misinformation. The evidence indicates that where such programmes have been implemented, susceptibility to false narratives has declined measurably.

[R] Reading 2 — Media Bias and the Question of Objectivity

Прочитай текст. Задание: а) найди 5 примеров академических reported phrases (It is argued / According to / Studies suggest); б) перепиши 3 любых предложения, заменив reporting verb на другой с тем же значением.

The concept of journalistic objectivity has been extensively debated within media studies. Scholars have long contended that complete impartiality is unattainable, given that every editorial decision — from story selection to framing — reflects underlying assumptions and institutional interests. According to leading media theorist Stuart Hall, news does not simply report reality but actively constructs it through the selection and arrangement of information. Research conducted across multiple news markets suggests that audiences increasingly seek out sources that confirm existing beliefs, a phenomenon widely described as confirmation bias. Studies have indicated that algorithmic content curation on digital platforms has intensified this tendency, creating what analysts have termed 'filter bubbles'. Critics have argued that this development poses a serious threat to the informed public debate upon which democratic society depends. It is widely acknowledged that addressing media bias requires both structural reforms — including ownership regulation — and individual efforts to cultivate critical media consumption habits.
