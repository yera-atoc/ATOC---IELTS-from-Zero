Ellipsis & Substitution

Как избегать повторения, не теряя точности?

Тематика: Глобализация и экономическое неравенство · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Cause & Effect Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Прочитай эти два абзаца об экономическом неравенстве:

Band 6 (с повторением): Globalisation has increased inequality. Globalisation has benefited some countries but not other countries. Some countries have developed quickly. Other countries have not developed quickly. Developed countries support globalisation. Developing countries do not always support globalisation.

Band 8 (с ellipsis & substitution): Globalisation has increased inequality, benefiting some economies considerably while leaving others largely behind. Developed nations have tended to support the liberalisation agenda; developing ones have not — or have done so only selectively, and with good reason.

Band 6 повторяет каждое слово, потому что боится быть непонятым. Band 8 доверяет читателю — и использует пропуски и замены, которые делают текст не короче, а плотнее, быстрее, и умнее. Именно это и оценивает IELTS как Cohesion & Coherence.

📘 Grammar 1 · Verbal Ellipsis: пропуск глагольной группы

Auxiliary ellipsis · do/does/did · So/Neither · Comparative ellipsis
🟣

В уровне A ты знал: 'Can you speak French? — Yes, I can.' (пропуск глагола после auxiliary)

В уровне B ты расширил: 'She works harder than he does.'

В уровне C мы изучаем глагольную эллипсис в академическом тексте — как она создаёт плотность и параллелизм без потери точности.
Тип эллипсиса Структура Пример без эллипсиса С эллипсисом Функция
Auxiliary ellipsis S + aux (+ not) Some nations have adopted carbon pricing; other nations have not adopted carbon pricing. Some nations have adopted carbon pricing; others have not. Избегает повторения VP; 'have not' несёт всю нагрузку
Do-substitution do / does / did Wealthy countries consume more resources than poor countries consume. Wealthy countries consume more resources than poor ones do. 'do' замещает весь предикат
So / Neither inversion So + aux + S / Neither + aux + S Globalisation has increased productivity. Globalisation has also reduced labour costs. Globalisation has increased productivity — and so, in many contexts, has it reduced labour costs. 'So' = подтверждение того же предиката; инверсия сигнализирует о formal register
Comparative ellipsis more/less/as + adj + as The economic benefits of trade liberalisation are less evenly distributed than the economic costs of trade liberalisation. The economic benefits of trade liberalisation are less evenly distributed than the costs. Полный VP пропускается после точки сравнения
Parallel clause ellipsis Shared predicate omitted Some regions have benefited from globalisation; some regions have not benefited from globalisation. Some regions have benefited from globalisation; others have not — and the divergence is widening. Эллипсис + добавление нового элемента в одной структуре

⚡ So/Neither inversion в академическом тексте

Стандартное: 'Trade liberalisation has increased output. It has also widened inequality.'

С 'so' + инверсией: 'Trade liberalisation has increased aggregate output — and so, it would appear, has it systematically widened the distribution of that output's benefits.'

Ключевое правило: 'so' стоит после тире или точки с запятой, затем — инверсия aux + subject. Sentence adverbs из C13 ('it would appear') можно встраивать между 'so' и инверсией.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

High-income economies have largely succeeded in exporting the environmental costs of manufacturing to lower-income ones — and so, it would appear, have they exported a commensurate share of the employment and technological development that might otherwise have accrued to their own populations.

① have largely succeeded in exporting — verbal + gerund: 'exporting' carries the semantic weight

② lower-income ones — nominal substitution: 'ones' replaces 'economies' (Grammar 2 preview)

③ and so, it would appear, have they — so-inversion + parenthetical hedging (C13): formal, high-density

④ a commensurate share of the employment and technological development that — complex NP object with defining relative: only ellipted what can be recovered
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Some countries have benefited and others countries have not benefited.

Правильно: Some countries have benefited from trade liberalisation; others have not — or have done so only under highly specific conditions.

Почему: 'Others countries' — неправильно: 'others' уже является местоимением, не требует существительного. 'Others have not' — полный эллипсис, правильный

Ошибка: The costs are higher than the benefits are high.

Правильно: The costs are considerably higher than the benefits — a disparity that becomes more pronounced at the household level.

Почему: В сравнительном эллипсисе повторять прилагательное после точки сравнения не нужно: 'higher than the benefits' достаточно. Добавление 'are high' — избыточное, не эллипсис
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Verbal Ellipsis

✔ Auxiliary ellipsis в параллельных структурах → CC Band 7–8: текст связен без механического повторения

✔ 'So + inversion' в академическом тексте → редкая, заметная структура; GRA Band 8

✔ Comparative ellipsis ('more than the costs') → плотность + точность одновременно (LR + GRA)
⚠ Снижает балл: Эллипсис без параллелизма — нечитаемо; убедись, что опущенный элемент однозначно восстанавливается из контекста

📙 Grammar 2 · Nominal Substitution: замена именных групп

One/ones · That/those · The former/the latter · Such · The same
🟣

Nominal substitution — это замена существительного или NP на pro-form.

В уровне C мы изучаем не только базовые замены, но и то, какой именно pro-form выбрать в зависимости от регистра и семантической точности.
Pro-form Что замещает Регистр Пример Ограничение
one / ones Count noun (единственное / множественное) Neutral–formal The economies most severely affected are the smaller ones — those that lack the institutional capacity to manage rapid capital flows. Только countable nouns; нельзя для uncountable
that / those NP с of-postmodifier Formal The inequality of emerging markets differs substantially from that of post-industrial economies in both its structural causes and its political consequences. 'That' замещает singular NP, 'those' — plural; требует post-modifier после
the former / the latter Два упомянутых NP Formal–academic Trade liberalisation and capital mobility are frequently conflated in policy discourse, yet the former tends to generate diffuse gains while the latter concentrates them. Только для двух элементов; порядок строго соблюдается
such Целая категория / тип Formal The liberalisation of capital markets has produced profound distributional consequences — and such consequences are rarely anticipated in the models used to justify liberalisation. 'Such' = 'consequences of this type'; implies category, not just reference
the same Идентичный элемент / действие Neutral–formal The regulatory framework applied to financial institutions should, I would contend, be applied in the same way to the digital platforms that have assumed comparable systemic importance. Подчёркивает идентичность, не только сходство
do so VP с to-infinitive или that-clause Formal Many governments have sought to attract foreign investment; others have declined to do so, prioritising domestic capital accumulation instead. 'Do so' замещает полный VP; более формальный, чем 'do it'

⚡ The former / the latter: точность при двух референтах

Правило: 'the former' = первый упомянутый; 'the latter' = второй упомянутый. Используется только когда оба референта чётко названы в предыдущем предложении.

Пример: 'Economic growth and income equality are often treated as competing objectives; the former has been prioritised in most mainstream development models, while the latter has been systematically subordinated to it.'

Когда НЕ использовать: если в предыдущем предложении упомянуто более двух референтов — 'the former/latter' создаёт неоднозначность. Используй повторение или 'that of / those of'.

📐 'Such' как академическая анафора: тонкость использования

'Such' в начале предложения указывает на целую категорию, не просто на предыдущее существительное. Это делает его мощнее, чем 'this' или 'these', — он обобщает и классифицирует:

Слабее (this/these): 'Capital flows have destabilised developing economies. These problems are well documented.'

Сильнее (such): 'Unregulated capital flows have repeatedly destabilised developing economies. Such destabilisation tends to follow a predictable pattern: currency appreciation, asset bubbles, sudden reversal.'

'Such destabilisation' — не просто 'this' = указание на предыдущее. Это классификация: 'destabilisation of this type'. Тонкая, но важная разница.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The distributional consequences of globalisation differ markedly between those of the initial post-war liberalisation era and those of the more recent phase of financialised global integration — the former having generated broadly shared gains across the income distribution, the latter having concentrated the overwhelming majority of its benefits within the uppermost decile.

① those of the initial post-war liberalisation era... and those of — 'those' substitutes 'distributional consequences': formal NP substitution with of-postmodifier

② the former / the latter — two clear referents (two eras): classic formal nominal substitution

③ having generated / having concentrated — nominative absolute style participial clauses: ellipsis of subject + auxiliary

④ within the uppermost decile — formal NP: 'decile' signals economic register; avoids 'the top 10%'
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Trade and investment are important. The former and the latter are both increasing.

Правильно: Trade and investment are both increasing, though the former has grown more steadily while the latter has proved more volatile.

Почему: 'The former and the latter' вместе в одном предложении — неуклюже. Используй их в разных клаузах для создания контраста, не просто как замену 'both'

Ошибка: Capital flows have caused problems. Such is happening in many countries.

Правильно: Unregulated capital flows have generated significant macroeconomic instability. Such instability has proved particularly acute in economies with underdeveloped financial regulatory frameworks.

Почему: 'Such' должно стоять перед существительным ('such instability', 'such consequences') — не отдельно как подлежащее без существительного ('such is')
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Nominal Substitution

✔ 'Those of' + NP вместо повторения → LR Band 7+: студенты Band 6 почти никогда не используют эту конструкцию

✔ 'The former / the latter' точно применённые → сигнал академической точности (CC + LR)

✔ 'Such + noun' вместо 'this' → категориальная анафора = более высокий уровень обобщения (TA + LR)
⚠ Снижает балл: 'Such' без существительного ('Such is the case') — только фиксированная идиома; в остальных случаях 'such' требует noun после него

📗 Grammar 3 · Clausal Substitution & Textual Ellipsis

So / Not · Do so · As such · As is the case · The above / The following
🟣

Мы изучили пропуск глагольной группы (Grammar 1) и замену именных групп (Grammar 2).

В Grammar 3 мы рассматриваем замену и пропуск целых клауз — самый сложный уровень когезии, который отличает Band 7 от Band 8.
Конструкция Что замещает Функция Пример
so (clausal substitute) Целую that-clause Замещает утвердительное содержание предыдущей клаузы Global inequality has widened, as the data clearly indicate — and economists have long predicted that it would do so under current policy frameworks.
not (clausal substitute) Целую that-clause (отрицание) Замещает отрицательное содержание Whether globalisation ultimately reduces inequality remains contested; the empirical record suggests that, in the short term at least, it does not.
do so VP с to-infinitive Заменяет полный инфинитивный VP Many economists have called for redistributive taxation; governments have, however, proved reluctant to do so.
as such Анафорическая ссылка на категорию 'Будучи таковым'; следствие из предыдущей классификации Globalisation is, at its core, a political project rather than a purely economic phenomenon; as such, its distributional outcomes are not natural but are the product of deliberate regulatory choices.
as is the case with / as is often the case Сравнительная анафора Указывает на параллель с упомянутым ранее The tax structures of developing economies tend to be regressive — as is often the case with fiscal systems that depend heavily upon consumption levies.
the above / the following Textual reference Указывает на предшествующий или последующий текст The above analysis suggests that redistribution, rather than growth, must be the primary instrument of poverty reduction. The following section examines the available policy instruments.

⚡ 'As such' — самая часто неправильно используемая конструкция

Правильное использование: 'As such' = 'being what it is / given this classification'. Оно следует из предыдущей классификации или определения:

✔ 'Inequality is a political choice, not an economic inevitability; as such, it can be addressed through deliberate policy intervention.'

Неправильное использование: 'The problem is very serious. As such, we need to act.' ← Здесь нет классификации, из которой следует вывод. Правильно: 'Therefore' или 'Consequently'.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

It has been contended, with considerable empirical support, that the liberalisation of capital markets systematically advantages holders of financial assets over those of labour — and, if so, that the distributional consequences of such liberalisation are not incidental but constitutive of the model itself; as such, they cannot be remedied through redistributive measures alone but require a fundamental rethinking of the regulatory architecture within which capital is permitted to move.

① those of labour — 'those' substitutes 'holders': nominal substitution (Grammar 2)

② and, if so — clausal substitute: 'if [what has just been contended is true]'; compact conditional

③ such liberalisation — 'such' as categorical anaphora: 'liberalisation of this type' (Grammar 2)

④ as such, they cannot be remedied — clausal substitute leading to logical consequence: 'being this kind of structural outcome'
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: The government failed to act. As such, it was a big problem.

Правильно: The government's failure to act constituted a structural abdication of regulatory responsibility; as such, it cannot be characterised as an oversight but as a deliberate policy choice.

Почему: 'As such' требует предшествующей классификации или определения. 'It was a big problem' — оценка, не классификация. 'As such' здесь следует заменить на 'consequently' или 'as a result'

Ошибка: Many economists have argued so.

Правильно: Many economists have argued that redistributive taxation is preferable to growth-led poverty reduction — and a growing body of empirical evidence suggests that they are correct to do so.

Почему: 'Argued so' — неправильно; 'so' замещает только be/seem/think/expect/etc. + that-clause. После 'argue' нужно 'to do so' или повтор с that-clause
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Clausal Substitution

✔ 'And, if so...' в середине аргумента → сигнализирует о сложном условном рассуждении (GRA + TA)

✔ 'As such' с правильной классификацией-предпосылкой → показывает логическую цепочку (CC Band 8)

✔ 'Do so' вместо 'do it' → LR: формальный замена; 'it' воспринимается как менее точный референт
⚠ Снижает балл: Неправильный 'as such' (без классификации-предпосылки) — очень частая ошибка Band 6–7; экзаменатор её видит

📕 Grammar 4 · Cohesion System & Band 8 Upgrade

Интеграция: как эллипсис и субституция создают связность без повторения
🟣

Эллипсис и субституция — это не просто способ избежать повторения.

В Grammar 4 мы видим, как они создают систему когезии: каждое предложение тянет за собой следующее через невидимые нити пропущенных и замещённых элементов.
Когезионный механизм Уровень Что связывает Пример из темы глобализации
Verbal ellipsis (aux) Предложение Параллельные клаузы с тем же VP Developed nations have benefited from trade liberalisation; developing ones have not — at least not consistently.
Nominal substitution (that of / those of) Предложение / абзац NP с одинаковой головой, но разными постмодификаторами The wage inequality of post-industrial economies differs markedly from that of resource-dependent ones.
The former / the latter Абзац Два чётко обозначенных референта Trade openness and capital mobility were presented as complementary; the former has proved broadly beneficial, the latter considerably more ambiguous.
Such + noun Абзац Категория из предыдущего предложения Supply chain offshoring has concentrated corporate profits. Such concentration undermines the tax base upon which social provision depends.
As such Абзац Логическое следствие из классификации Globalisation is a governance choice, not an economic law; as such, its distributional outcomes are subject to political revision.
Do so / if so / not Абзац / текст VP или целая клауза Redistributive taxation can mitigate the effects of inequality — but governments have, thus far, proved reluctant to do so at the scale required.

📐 Band 8 Paragraph: система когезии в действии

Предложение 1 (тезис + тема): The distributional consequences of globalisation have been profoundly asymmetric, disproportionately benefiting holders of capital relative to those of labour.

Предложение 2 (such + development): Such asymmetry is not incidental to the liberalisation model but constitutive of it — embedded in the structural incentives that govern the behaviour of mobile capital in a world of immobile labour.

Предложение 3 (as such + consequence): As such, it cannot be addressed through growth-maximising strategies alone; redistributive mechanisms of a scale and ambition that few governments have thus far proved willing to implement are, I would contend, a necessary complement.

Предложение 4 (the former/latter + ellipsis): The distinction between adequate and transformative redistribution matters enormously; the former stabilises existing inequalities while the latter restructures them — and only the latter, I would submit, is proportionate to the challenge.
🚀 Band 8 Upgrade

Band 6: Globalisation has increased inequality. Rich countries benefit more than poor countries benefit. This inequality is a problem. Governments should do something about this inequality but they don't do something about it.

Band 7: Globalisation has increased inequality, benefiting wealthy nations considerably more than developing ones. This constitutes a significant policy failure, yet governments have proved reluctant to address it through redistributive means.

Band 8: The distributional consequences of globalisation have been asymmetric to a degree that standard liberalisation theory did not predict and has subsequently struggled to explain — those accruing to capital holders having been substantially greater than those accruing to labour, and those concentrated in high-income economies greater still than those reaching the developing world. Such asymmetry is, as the foregoing analysis has sought to demonstrate, neither accidental nor correctable through marginal policy adjustment; as such, it demands a fundamental reconsideration of the governance frameworks through which global economic integration is structured and legitimated.
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Full Cohesion System

✔ Комбинация verbal ellipsis + nominal substitution + 'as such' в одном абзаце → CC Band 8: высшая когезия

✔ Текст без повторений слов, но с полной смысловой ясностью → LR + CC: именно это измеряет экзамен

✔ Каждая замена однозначно восстанавливается → признак зрелого академического автора (все 4 критерия)
⚠ Снижает балл: Чрезмерный эллипсис — когда опущенный элемент восстанавливается неоднозначно — хуже повторения; CC снижается

📖 Reading 1 · Globalisation and the Inequality Paradox

Академическое чтение: эллипсис и субституция в реальном экономическом дискурсе
📖 Globalisation and the Inequality Paradox: Why Integration Has Not Delivered Convergence

The relationship between economic globalisation and income inequality is, it would appear, considerably more complex than either its most ardent proponents or its most vocal critics have typically acknowledged. The former have tended to invoke aggregate measures of global poverty reduction as evidence of globalisation's broadly beneficial character, while the latter have pointed to within-country inequality — which has risen sharply in many of the world's largest economies — as evidence of its fundamentally regressive distributional logic. Both sets of observations are, in their own terms, empirically defensible; as such, any adequate account of globalisation's distributional consequences must be capable of holding them simultaneously.

More precisely, the relevant distinction is between the effects of trade integration on the one hand and those of financial liberalisation on the other — the former having contributed, over the past three decades, to a measurable convergence of living standards between countries, while the latter has proved considerably more ambiguous in its distributional effects and, in several well-documented cases, has generated the kind of macroeconomic instability that disproportionately burdens lower-income households. Such instability — manifesting as currency crises, sudden capital reversals, and asset-price volatility — has proved particularly acute in economies that liberalised their financial sectors rapidly and without adequate regulatory infrastructure.

What the evidence therefore suggests is not that globalisation is inherently regressive but that the distributional outcomes of global economic integration are determined less by the fact of integration than by the governance frameworks within which it occurs — and that those frameworks have, thus far, been designed primarily to facilitate the mobility of capital rather than to ensure that the gains from such mobility are equitably shared. As such, the appropriate policy response is not deglobalisation but the construction of more robust and equitable international regulatory institutions: a task that is, admittedly, considerably easier to prescribe than to achieve.

📖 Reading 2 · The Political Economy of Redistribution

Субституция и эллипсис в нормативном аргументе
📖 The Political Economy of Redistribution: Why Governments Know What to Do and Fail to Do So

It is widely acknowledged that the tools available to governments for addressing the distributional consequences of globalisation are, at least in principle, well understood: progressive taxation, investment in public goods, portable social insurance systems, and active labour market policies have all been demonstrated, in various national contexts, to do so effectively. The question is, therefore, not primarily one of technical knowledge but of political feasibility — specifically, why governments that possess both the analytical capacity to identify the problem and the institutional capacity to address it have consistently proved unwilling to do so at the scale that the severity of the challenge demands.

The former capacity — analytical understanding — has, if anything, increased in recent decades, as the distributional consequences of liberalisation have become increasingly visible and empirically documented. The latter — institutional capacity — is more unevenly distributed, particularly among developing economies whose fiscal infrastructure remains insufficient to support ambitious redistributive programmes. As such, the policy prescription appropriate for a high-income OECD economy with a mature tax administration is not necessarily the same as that appropriate for a middle-income country with a large informal sector and limited fiscal reach — and treating them as if they were the same constitutes one of the most persistent failures of international development advice.

What is required, then, is not a universal redistributive template but a context-sensitive analytical framework capable of identifying the specific political and institutional constraints that render redistribution difficult in any given national context and of designing interventions calibrated to those constraints. The above analysis suggests that such a framework must incorporate not only the technical dimensions of fiscal policy design but also the political economy of reform — specifically, the conditions under which coalitions capable of sustaining redistributive policy can be constructed and maintained. Admittedly, this is considerably more difficult than standard policy advice implies; do so, however, is what the situation demands.

✍️ Writing Task 1 · Pie Charts (160 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

The pie charts below show the distribution of national income across five income groups (lowest 20%, second 20%, middle 20%, fourth 20%, and highest 20%) in two countries — Country A (a high-income OECD economy) and Country B (a middle-income emerging economy) — in 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

The distribution of national income in Country B is markedly more unequal than that of Country A — the upper quintile capturing over 55% of total income in the former and approximately 38% in the latter, while the lowest quintile accounts for less than 4% of national income in Country B and nearly twice that in Country A.

① than that of Country A — nominal substitute 'that' replaces 'distribution of national income': formal and compact

② in the former... in the latter — 'the former' = Country B (first mentioned); 'the latter' = Country A

③ nearly twice that — ellipsis + nominal substitute: 'twice that [proportion/share]'

④ the upper quintile capturing / the lowest quintile accounts — parallel structure: participial + finite; creates rhythm

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Главный паттерн (inequality contrast) + конкретные данные по quintiles

CC (7–8): 'that of' + 'the former/latter' + 'twice that' в одном предложении: максимальная когезия

LR (7–8): quintile, capturing, accounts for, approximately — точная академическая лексика

GRA (7–8): Parallel structure с ellipsis + nominal substitution в сложном предложении

📐 Структура эссе:

Overview (2 пр.): 'that of' для главного сравнения. 'Such inequality / such a disparity' для введения паттерна.

Body 1 (3–4 пр.): 'The former / the latter' для стран. Verbal ellipsis: 'Country A accounts for X%; Country B, Y%'.

Body 2 (3–4 пр.): 'As such, the middle quintiles...' для логического следствия. 'Do so' для предикатной замены.

✍️ Writing Task 2 · Cause & Effect Essay

Когезия через эллипсис и субституцию в причинно-следственном аргументе
✍️ Writing Task 2 · Cause & Effect Essay (265 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

Economic inequality has increased in many countries in recent decades. What are the main causes of this increase, and what effects does rising inequality have on society? Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

The acceleration of economic inequality across much of the developed and developing world is attributable to a convergent set of structural forces — among them the liberalisation of capital markets, the erosion of collective bargaining, and the skill-biased nature of technological change — each of which has, to a greater or lesser degree, increased the returns to capital and to high-skill labour relative to those of low-skill labour and, as such, has shifted the distribution of productivity gains away from those who arguably need them most.

① those of low-skill labour — nominal substitute 'those' replaces 'returns': formal NP substitution (Grammar 2)

② as such, has shifted — clausal substitute: 'being the kind of process just described'; logical consequence

③ those who arguably need them most — 'those' = 'those [people]': nominal ellipsis of head noun; sentence adverb 'arguably'

④ each of which has, to a greater or lesser degree — non-defining relative with quantification; hedged degree adverbial

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Causes и effects чётко разграничены; позиция через 'as such' и 'do so' встроена без 'I think'

CC (7–8): Nominal substitution + as such + verbal ellipsis: три механизма когезии в intro

LR (7–8): liberalisation, collective bargaining, skill-biased, productivity gains — C2 экономическая лексика

GRA (7–8): 'Each of which' + relative + degree adverbial + nominal substitute в одном предложении

📐 Структура эссе:

Intro (2 пр.): Complex NP для причин + 'as such' для перехода к следствиям.

Causes (3–4 пр.): 'Such processes' → 'the former / the latter' для двух главных причин → verbal ellipsis в сравнениях.

Effects (3–4 пр.): 'Such inequality' → 'do so' для политических следствий → 'as such' для логических выводов.

Conclusion (2 пр.): 'The above analysis' + 'if so' + 'it is submitted' + 'as such'.

🎓 Урок C16 завершён!

✅ Verbal ellipsis: auxiliary ellipsis / do-substitution / so-inversion / comparative ellipsis — плотность без повторения

✅ Nominal substitution: one/ones · that/those · the former/the latter · such + noun · do so — замена NP и VP с регистровой точностью

✅ Clausal substitution: so / not / do so / as such / as is the case — замена целых клауз и логических следствий

✅ Cohesion system: шесть механизмов в одном абзаце — не набор приёмов, а связная система

✅ Band 8 Cohesion = каждая замена однозначна + повышает плотность + соответствует регистру

💡 Central Question — Ответ:

Эллипсис и субституция — это инструменты доверия читателю. Когда автор пишет 'as such' вместо повторения всего аргумента, или 'the latter' вместо повторного называния термина, он говорит читателю: ты достаточно умён, чтобы восстановить пропущенное. Именно это и создаёт ощущение плотного, уважающего читателя академического текста. Повторение — это сомнение в читателе. Эллипсис — это доверие.
