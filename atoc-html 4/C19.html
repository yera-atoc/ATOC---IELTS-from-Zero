Урок C19 · Upper-Intermediate / Advanced

Advanced Concession & Refutation

Как признание слабости аргумента делает его сильнее?

Тематика: Здравоохранение и биоэтика · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Opinion + Discussion Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Два ответа на возражение: 'Expensive treatments drain public health budgets.'

Band 6: Yes, expensive treatments are a problem. But they also save lives. So we should still fund them.

Band 8: Admittedly, the fiscal pressure exerted by high-cost treatments on public health systems is real and ought not to be minimised; yet this observation, while accurate in isolation, loses much of its force when set against the countervailing consideration that the economic cost of preventable mortality and chronic morbidity — in lost productivity, in foregone tax revenue, in the downstream burden upon social care systems — routinely exceeds the upfront cost of the interventions that would have prevented them.

Band 6 признаёт возражение — но только чтобы его отмахнуться. Band 8 проводит его через четыре операции: признание → точная оценка масштаба → контрвес → разворот через конкретный аргумент. Это и есть advanced concession & refutation.

📘 Grammar 1 · The Concession-Refutation Architecture

Admittedly · Granted · It must be acknowledged · While X, Y · Concede → Qualify → Reframe → Counter
🟣

Студенты уровня B знают: 'However, on the other hand, despite this'.

Студенты уровня C понимают: продвинутая уступка — это не просто переход. Это четырёхшаговая аргументативная операция, в которой каждый шаг выполняет отдельную функцию.

📐 Четыре шага продвинутой уступки

Шаг 1 — CONCEDE (признание): признай силу возражения честно и конкретно. Не преуменьшай.

'Admittedly, the fiscal burden of high-cost therapies is considerable and ought not to be dismissed as a secondary concern.'

Шаг 2 — QUALIFY (ограничение): огради уступку — укажи условия, при которых она верна, и что она не доказывает.

'This observation, however accurate in the short term, does not, in itself, constitute a decisive argument against funding.'

Шаг 3 — REFRAME (переосмысление): предложи иной аналитический угол, при котором уступленное становится менее значимым.

'When evaluated across the full economic and social cost horizon rather than the immediate budgetary cycle, the calculus changes substantially.'

Шаг 4 — COUNTER (контраргумент): приведи конкретный аргумент, который перевешивает уступленное.

'The economic cost of preventable mortality routinely exceeds the upfront cost of the interventions that would have prevented it.'
Маркер уступки Сила уступки Синтаксическая форма Функция
Admittedly Полное признание Sentence adverb в начале или середине (C13) Показывает академическую честность; самый прямой маркер
Granted (that) Полное признание + условность Granted + NP / Granted that + clause Признаёт конкретный факт как данность перед опровержением
It must be acknowledged (that) Формальное, дистанцированное признание Impersonal it-clause (C13) Максимальная прагматическая дистанция; самый формальный
While X, Y / Whilst X, Y Одновременное признание и контр While/Whilst + clause, main clause Уступка и ответ в одном предложении; плотно, эффективно
It is true that X; however, Y Двухшаговая структура 'It is true that' + assertion; sentence break; counter Явная разметка уступки и ответа; структурно прозрачная
Notwithstanding X, Y Формальная уступка в NP Notwithstanding + NP, main clause Очень формально; X признаётся без развёрнутого обсуждения
For all that X Риторически сильная уступка For all that + noun/clause, Y remains 'Несмотря на всё это' — признаёт полную силу возражения
Be that as it may Закрытие уступки, переход Отдельное предложение-переход Закрывает уступку и маркирует возврат к главному аргументу

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Admittedly, the argument that rationing access to high-cost treatments on grounds of cost-effectiveness represents a form of discrimination against the chronically ill carries genuine moral weight — and it would be intellectually dishonest to pretend otherwise. It must be acknowledged, however, that the alternative — unlimited funding of all clinically indicated treatments regardless of cost — is, for any health system operating under finite budgetary constraints, simply not a coherent policy option; the question is therefore not whether to ration but how to do so transparently, equitably, and in accordance with publicly defensible criteria.

① Admittedly... carries genuine moral weight — full concession: 'genuine' means the author takes it seriously

② it would be intellectually dishonest to pretend otherwise — double concession: author acknowledges they cannot dismiss it

③ It must be acknowledged, however — formal pivot (C13): 'however' signals the counter is coming

④ not whether to ration but how — 'not X but Y' reframe (C17): the question itself is reframed, not just answered
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Some people say that expensive treatments waste money. But I disagree. Treatments are important.

Правильно: Admittedly, the allocation of substantial public resources to high-cost treatments raises legitimate questions of opportunity cost. This concern, however, loses considerable force when one considers that untreated chronic conditions generate far greater long-term expenditure than the preventive interventions that would have averted them.

Почему: 'But I disagree' — прямое, неаргументированное отрицание. Продвинутая уступка признаёт конкретный факт возражения и отвечает конкретным контраргументом, а не общим несогласием

Ошибка: While it is true that rationing is necessary, it is also true that we should fund treatments.

Правильно: While the necessity of some form of resource rationing within health systems is, I would contend, beyond reasonable dispute, the criteria by which such rationing is conducted — and the transparency of those criteria to public scrutiny — remain deeply consequential questions that purely budgetary frameworks are ill-equipped to address.

Почему: 'It is also true that we should fund treatments' — расплывчатый контраргумент. Продвинутый ответ на уступку должен быть конкретным, содержательным и аналитически сильнее, чем уступленное утверждение
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Concession-Refutation Architecture

✔ 'Admittedly... it must be acknowledged, however' — двойная уступка + формальный разворот → Band 8 CC и TA одновременно

✔ Qualify + Reframe перед Counter → структура аргумента видна экзаменатору; CC Band 8

✔ 'Not whether X but how' как реформулировка после уступки → TA: автор поднимает дискуссию на более высокий уровень
⚠ Снижает балл: Уступка без ответа — это не уступка, это провал аргумента; Counter должен быть сильнее уступленного утверждения

📙 Grammar 2 · Refutation Strategies: пять способов опровергнуть аргумент

Reductio · Counterexample · Scope restriction · Burden shift · Reframe the question
🟣

Уступка — первая половина операции. Опровержение — вторая.

В уровне C мы изучаем пять разных стратегий опровержения — каждая подходит для разного типа аргумента противника.
Стратегия Логика Синтаксическая форма Пример из биоэтики
Reductio ad absurdum Показывает, что принцип оппонента ведёт к неприемлемым следствиям 'If X were true, it would follow that Y — which is clearly unacceptable.' / 'Taken to its logical conclusion, this argument implies...' If cost-effectiveness alone were to determine treatment eligibility, it would follow that elderly patients and those with rare conditions — who offer the least return per unit of expenditure — should be systematically deprioritised; a conclusion that few would endorse openly yet that lies just beneath the surface of purely utilitarian healthcare rationing.
Counterexample Один конкретный пример, опровергающий общее утверждение 'This claim does not, however, withstand empirical scrutiny. Consider the case of...' / 'The evidence from X suggests otherwise.' The claim that high drug prices are a necessary incentive for pharmaceutical innovation does not withstand scrutiny: the case of publicly funded research that generated numerous breakthrough therapies — including vaccines for which no patent protection was pursued — demonstrates that innovation is not contingent upon the price-maximisation model.
Scope restriction Ограничивает применимость аргумента: он верен в одних условиях, но не в других 'This argument holds, at most, in [limited context]; it does not extend to...' / 'The evidence for X is confined to...' The argument that market competition drives pharmaceutical innovation holds, at most, for conditions affecting large patient populations in high-income markets; it fails entirely to account for the near-total absence of private sector investment in treatments for neglected tropical diseases affecting hundreds of millions in low-income countries.
Burden shift Перекладывает бремя доказательства: оппонент должен обосновать, а не просто утверждать 'This position assumes X without establishing it.' / 'Before accepting this claim, it must be demonstrated that...' / 'The burden of proof lies with those who assert...' The assertion that patients themselves bear primary responsibility for lifestyle-related health conditions assumes, without demonstrating, that the determinants of health behaviour are primarily individual rather than structural — an assumption that the social epidemiology literature, taken as a whole, does not support.
Reframe the question Показывает, что вопрос задан неверно — и что правильный вопрос открывает другой ответ 'The question is not whether X but whether Y.' / 'This framing obscures the more fundamental issue of...' / 'The more productive question is...' The question is not whether genetic testing creates anxiety — it demonstrably does, in some patients, under some conditions — but whether the clinical and preventive benefits of early detection justify the management of that anxiety as a distinct and addressable component of the diagnostic pathway.

⚡ Какую стратегию выбрать?

Reductio: когда аргумент оппонента основан на принципе с неприемлемыми следствиями. Применяй осторожно — reductio легко выглядит как атака соломенного чучела.

Counterexample: когда утверждение сформулировано как всеобщее ('always', 'never', 'all'). Один хорошо подобранный пример разрушает универсальное обобщение.

Scope restriction: когда аргумент верен в узком контексте, но его незаконно расширяют. Лучший выбор для сложных, многосторонних вопросов.

Burden shift: когда оппонент делает утверждение без доказательств и рассчитывает, что ты будешь его опровергать. Вместо этого — требуй доказательства.

Reframe: когда сам вопрос сформулирован таким образом, что предрасполагает к ошибочному ответу. Самый высокий уровень опровержения.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The contention that genetic screening programmes should be curtailed because they generate anxiety in patients who receive uncertain results does not, on closer examination, constitute a decisive argument against such programmes — it constitutes, rather, an argument for their more thoughtful implementation. The question is not whether uncertainty is distressing — it self-evidently is — but whether the clinical system surrounding genetic disclosure is adequately designed to manage that distress; and if it is not, the appropriate response is to improve the system, not to deprive patients of information that may, for many, prove clinically actionable.

① does not... constitute a decisive argument against X — it constitutes, rather, an argument for Y — reframe strategy

② The question is not whether X — it self-evidently is — but whether Y — scope restriction + parenthetical concession inside the reframe

③ and if it is not, the appropriate response is to improve the system, not to deprive — not X but Y (C17) inside counter

④ that may, for many, prove clinically actionable — hedging (C13) within the counter-argument; intellectually honest
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: If we follow this logic, then we should kill all sick people to save money — which is ridiculous.

Правильно: Taken to its logical conclusion, a purely utilitarian approach to healthcare rationing would systematically deprioritise patients with low quality-adjusted life year projections — a consequence that proponents of this view rarely articulate openly, but that is nonetheless entailed by their position.

Почему: Reductio должен точно отражать логику позиции оппонента, не карикатурить её. 'Kill all sick people' — strawman. Честный reductio показывает реальные, незамечаемые следствия принципа

Ошибка: This argument is wrong because there are some countries where it doesn't work.

Правильно: The claim that market mechanisms reliably deliver pharmaceutical innovation does not hold universally: the near-total absence of private investment in treatments for neglected diseases — conditions affecting over one billion people — constitutes a counterexample whose scale is difficult to reconcile with the market-optimist thesis.

Почему: 'Some countries where it doesn't work' — слабый counterexample без конкретики. Сильный counterexample: конкретный случай + масштаб + почему он опровергает общее утверждение
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Refutation Strategies

✔ Reframe strategy ('not whether X but how') → TA Band 8: автор демонстрирует более высокий уровень анализа, чем оппонент

✔ Scope restriction с конкретными условиями → показывает, что автор понимает нюансы, а не отрицает всё огулом

✔ Burden shift ('this assumes X without demonstrating it') → сильнейший академический ход против необоснованных утверждений
⚠ Снижает балл: Reductio ad absurdum, направленный против карикатурной версии позиции оппонента, — это strawman fallacy; опровергай реальный аргумент

📗 Grammar 3 · Steel-Manning & Intellectual Honesty

Как представить позицию оппонента сильнее, чем он сам — и почему это тебе выгодно
🟣

Straw-manning — ослаблять позицию оппонента перед опровержением. Это аргументативная нечестность.

Steel-manning — усиливать позицию оппонента до её лучшей версии перед опровержением. Это не слабость — это высший уровень аргументации, который демонстрирует TA Band 8.
Уровень Подход Синтаксическая форма Что это сигнализирует экзаменатору
Band 5–6 (strawman) Упрощённая или карикатурная версия позиции оппонента 'Some people think X is bad. But this is too simple.' Нет понимания нюансов; TA снижается
Band 6–7 (fair representation) Точное воспроизведение позиции оппонента 'Proponents of X argue that...' / 'Those who support X contend that...' Честно, но не особенно сильно
Band 7–8 (steel-manning) Лучшая версия позиции оппонента — сильнее, чем она обычно формулируется 'The strongest version of this argument holds that...' / 'Put at its most compelling, this position maintains...' / 'The most defensible form of this view is...' Высший академический стандарт; TA Band 8
Band 8 (steel-man + refutation) Предложить наилучшую версию — и затем опровергнуть её Steel-man → 'And yet, even in this formulation...' / 'The most sympathetic reading of this position, however, cannot entirely evade...' Показывает, что автор понимает все версии — и всё равно не согласен; максимальная убедительность

📐 Steel-manning в действии: пример из биоэтики

Тема: должны ли пациенты с самоиндуцированными заболеваниями иметь меньший приоритет?

Strawman: 'Some people think smokers don't deserve treatment. This is cruel and wrong.'

Fair representation: 'Proponents of lifestyle-adjusted prioritisation argue that those whose conditions are partly self-induced should be deprioritised in favour of patients who had no choice in their condition.'

Steel-man: 'The strongest version of the lifestyle-responsibility argument does not depend upon blame or desert but upon the coherent principle that publicly funded health systems must make resource allocation decisions, and that individual choices which foreseeably increase resource demand are a legitimate consideration in those decisions — provided that the attribution of causal responsibility is accurate and the conditions of genuine autonomous choice were present.'

Steel-man + refutation: 'And yet, even in this formulation, the argument fails at precisely the point it most requires to succeed: the social determinants of health — income, housing, education, exposure to advertising, access to healthy food — systematically undermine the assumption of genuine autonomous choice upon which the entire edifice of lifestyle responsibility rests.'

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The strongest version of the argument for voluntary euthanasia does not rest upon a simple appeal to individual autonomy — a formulation that, taken in isolation, might be countered by noting that autonomy is rarely exercised in conditions of full information and freedom from coercive pressure. Put at its most compelling, this position maintains that the alternative — compelling terminally ill patients to endure suffering they have clearly and repeatedly expressed a wish to end — constitutes a form of state-enforced cruelty that no plausible account of human dignity can justify. And yet, even in this formulation, a residual concern remains: the difficulty of reliably distinguishing a settled, autonomous decision from one distorted by depression, inadequate palliative care, or the internalised belief that one has become a burden.

① The strongest version of the argument... does not rest upon X — explicitly marks this as steel-manning, not strawman

② a formulation that, might be countered — parenthetical concession (C13) within the steel-man: shows the author knows the weak version

③ Put at its most compelling — formal marker of steel-manning: 'I am now giving you the best version'

④ And yet, even in this formulation, a residual concern remains — counter after steel-man: the concern that even the best version cannot answer
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: People who support euthanasia just want to kill old people. This is obviously wrong.

Правильно: The strongest version of the case for assisted dying rests not on indifference to vulnerable patients but on the contention that the current legal prohibition forces terminally ill individuals to endure suffering they have explicitly and repeatedly chosen to end — a form of compelled suffering that even those who oppose euthanasia must acknowledge as morally troubling.

Почему: Первое предложение — strawman (карикатура). Сильный академический аргумент представляет позицию оппонента честно и в её лучшей форме — а затем опровергает именно её

Ошибка: Granted the argument has some good points, but overall it is wrong and not convincing.

Правильно: Granted that the autonomy-based case for voluntary euthanasia has genuine moral force — and intellectual honesty demands that this be acknowledged — the difficulty of operationalising meaningful consent in conditions of terminal illness and psychological distress remains, I would contend, a serious and unresolved objection.

Почему: 'Has some good points but overall wrong' — расплывчато. Продвинутая уступка должна конкретно назвать, что именно признаётся, и конкретно сформулировать оставшееся возражение
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Steel-Manning

✔ 'The strongest version of this argument...' → TA Band 8: мгновенный сигнал аналитической зрелости

✔ 'Put at its most compelling...' → показывает, что автор понимает все версии позиции, не только слабые

✔ 'And yet, even in this formulation...' → опровержение лучшей версии убедительнее, чем опровержение карикатуры
⚠ Снижает балл: Steel-man без последующего refutation — это не аргумент, это капитуляция; всегда показывай, что даже лучшая версия позиции оппонента не устоит

📕 Grammar 4 · Integration: The Full Dialectical Paragraph

Тезис → Steel-man → Concede → Qualify → Reframe → Counter → Recover
🟣

Всё, что мы изучили в C19, складывается в одну архитектуру.

Диалектический абзац — это абзац, который ведёт внутреннюю дискуссию: автор представляет лучшую версию противоположной позиции, честно её признаёт, ограничивает, переосмысливает — и в итоге убедительнее, чем если бы он просто игнорировал возражения.
Шаг Функция Маркер Пример (тема: принцип информированного согласия)
Тезис Основной аргумент абзаца Declarative statement The principle of informed consent — the requirement that patients fully understand and freely endorse any medical intervention — constitutes one of the foundational commitments of contemporary bioethics.
Steel-man Сильнейшая версия контрпозиции 'The strongest objection to this principle holds that...' / 'Critics who are most difficult to dismiss argue...' The strongest objection to this principle, however, holds not that patients should be deceived but that the cognitive and emotional conditions under which consent is typically sought — serious illness, time pressure, information asymmetry between clinician and patient — systematically undermine the very autonomy that the principle is designed to protect.
Concede Честное признание силы возражения 'Admittedly...' / 'This concern is not without foundation.' Admittedly, this concern is not without foundation: the empirical literature on medical decision-making suggests that many patients, even after formal consent procedures, retain only limited understanding of the interventions to which they have agreed.
Qualify Ограничение уступки 'This observation, however, does not...' / 'Accurate as this is, it does not follow that...' This observation, however, does not constitute an argument against informed consent; it constitutes an argument for better-designed consent processes.
Reframe Переосмысление вопроса 'The question is not whether X, but how...' / 'The more productive framing is...' The question is not whether genuine autonomy is achievable in clinical settings — it is, under the right conditions — but what those conditions require, and whether health systems are designed to produce them.
Counter Конкретный контраргумент 'The evidence, moreover, suggests...' / 'More fundamentally...' More fundamentally, the imperfect realisation of a principle does not, in itself, provide grounds for its abandonment; it provides grounds for its more rigorous implementation — and the history of bioethical reform is substantially the history of precisely this kind of iterative improvement.

📐 Band 8 Dialectical Paragraph: полная версия

Тема: справедлива ли приоритизация органов трансплантации по образу жизни пациента?

The proposition that lifestyle choices should influence organ transplant eligibility represents, on the surface, a straightforward application of the principle that scarce public resources should be allocated to those most likely to benefit from them. The strongest version of this position does not appeal to desert or blame but to the coherent claim that transplant systems operating under conditions of severe scarcity are obliged to maximise the utility of the organs available — and that this may legitimately include consideration of behavioural factors that affect post-transplant outcomes. Admittedly, this argument has genuine empirical force: there is credible evidence that continued alcohol consumption following liver transplant is associated with worse outcomes in a meaningful subset of recipients. This evidence, however, must be evaluated against the considerably more powerful social determinants literature, which demonstrates that 'lifestyle choices' are rarely the product of unconstrained individual agency and are, in a substantial proportion of cases, the downstream consequence of socioeconomic deprivation, addiction disorders with recognised neurological substrates, and limited access to preventive care. Granted, therefore, that the utilitarian premise is sound, its application requires a standard of causal attribution that the current evidence base does not reliably support — and its adoption without that evidential foundation risks laundering structural inequality as individual moral failure.

Band 6: Some people think lifestyle choices should affect organ transplant priority. I disagree because it is not fair. People can't always control their lifestyle. So this policy is wrong.

📖 The Ethics of Healthcare Rationing: Scarcity, Priority, and the Limits of the Market Model

It is widely acknowledged that the allocation of scarce healthcare resources involves unavoidable trade-offs that no ethical framework has yet succeeded in resolving to universal satisfaction. The strongest version of the market-based approach to healthcare allocation does not rest upon indifference to the suffering of those who cannot afford treatment; it rests upon the empirical claim that market mechanisms, by generating incentives for innovation and efficiency, ultimately expand the total pool of resources available for healthcare — and that this expansion benefits, over time, even those who initially lack access. Put at its most compelling, this position holds that the alternative — a system of centralised allocation premised on the suppression of price signals — generates the kind of static inefficiency that, in the long run, produces greater aggregate suffering than the inequality it was designed to eliminate.

Admittedly, this argument has genuine force in certain domains: the historical record does suggest that competitive pharmaceutical markets have generated significant innovation in the treatment of conditions affecting large patient populations in high-income countries. This observation must, however, be qualified by the countervailing evidence that market mechanisms have produced near-total investment failure in the treatment of conditions primarily affecting low-income populations — neglected tropical diseases, for instance, affect over one billion people yet attract a minute fraction of global pharmaceutical research expenditure. The market model, in other words, is not merely imperfect but structurally incapable of addressing healthcare needs that cannot be monetised.

What the foregoing analysis suggests is that the question is not, as market advocates sometimes frame it, a choice between efficiency and equality — a framing that systematically privileges the former by treating the latter as a luxury. The more productive analytical question is how different institutional arrangements perform against a plurality of values simultaneously: efficiency, equity, quality, accessibility, and sustainability. Granted that no existing system performs optimally against all five criteria, the evidence broadly suggests that systems with stronger elements of public coordination and universal coverage perform better, on average, against four of these five than those relying primarily on market mechanisms — the exception, notably, being the dimension of innovation in commercially viable treatments.

📖 The Limits of Informed Consent: Autonomy, Cognitive Capacity, and the Boundaries of Patient Self-Determination

The principle of informed consent occupies a foundational position in contemporary medical ethics, and rightly so: the alternative — allowing clinicians to make decisions on behalf of patients without their knowledge or agreement — is, on most accounts, both paternalistic and inconsistent with the respect for persons that any adequate ethical framework must uphold. The strongest objection to the current formulation of this principle, however, does not challenge the value of autonomy itself but questions whether the procedural mechanisms through which consent is obtained reliably succeed in protecting it. Put at its most penetrating, this critique holds that the informed consent process, as currently practised, frequently produces the formal appearance of autonomous choice while the substance of genuine deliberation is systematically absent.

Admittedly, the evidence for this critique is, in certain respects, compelling: studies of patient comprehension following consent procedures consistently report that a substantial proportion of participants retain only limited understanding of the key risks and alternatives associated with their proposed treatment. For all that, however, the appropriate response to this evidence is not to abandon the consent requirement — as some critics have rashly suggested — but to redesign the consent process itself: to replace the current model of document-centric information transfer with one that prioritises genuine comprehension, supported decision-making, and the patient's own narrative understanding of what is at stake.

It is submitted, I would contend, that this distinction — between the procedural form of consent and its substantive achievement — is the most analytically productive reframing available to this debate. Granted that perfect autonomy is an ideal rather than an empirical reality, it does not follow that the regulatory apparatus designed to approximate that ideal should be dismantled; it follows, rather, that it should be reformed in ways that close the gap between aspiration and practice. The burden of proof lies firmly with those who would substitute clinical judgement for patient agency: not because clinicians are untrustworthy, but because the history of medical ethics offers ample evidence of what happens when that substitution is made without adequate safeguards.
