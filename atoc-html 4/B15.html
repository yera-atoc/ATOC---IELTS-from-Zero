📘 Grammar 1 — What-Cleft Sentences: «What...is/are»

🔗 Связь с уровнем A: В A19 ты изучал Linking Words и структуру аргументов. Cleft Sentences — это следующий уровень: они помогают расставить акцент на нужной части предложения без добавления новых слов, только меняя структуру.

Cleft Sentences (расщеплённые предложения) позволяют выделить любую часть предложения. Структура What + clause + is/are + [выделяемая часть] — один из самых мощных инструментов академического письма.

Обычное предложение What-cleft (выделяем...) Что выделяем
Social inequality causes conflict. What causes conflict is social inequality. подлежащее
Governments need to address poverty. What governments need to address is poverty. дополнение
Community support makes a difference. What makes a difference is community support. подлежащее
We lack clear data on this issue. What we lack is clear data on this issue. дополнение
Education transforms societies. What transforms societies is education. подлежащее

Варианты структуры What-cleft

Формула Пример Применение
What + S + V + is + NP What this research reveals is a growing divide. Выделение вывода/дополнения
What + V + S + is What concerns sociologists is rising inequality. Выделение подлежащего
What + S + need(s)/want(s) + is What policymakers need is reliable data. Рекомендация/решение
What + happened + was What happened was a fundamental shift in values. Описание события
What + is + adj + is (that) What is worrying is that the gap is widening. Выражение оценки
Неправильно Правильно
What is important are strong social institutions. What is important is strong social institutions. (единственное число)
💡 Согласование: глагол после «What...» согласуется с той частью, которую ты выделяешь. Если выделяемая часть — единственное число, используй is; множественное — are. 'What we need are better policies.' (policies = мн.ч.)

IELTS подсказка

В IELTS Writing Band 7 экзаменаторы ищут разнообразие синтаксических структур. What-cleft в начале абзаца — отличный способ сделать topic sentence более выразительным: 'What this essay argues is that social mobility depends on structural, not individual, factors.'

Задание 1

Преобразуй предложения в What-cleft, выделяя подчёркнутую часть:

• Strong communities reduce crime rates. → What...

• Society needs better mental health services. → What society...

• A lack of trust undermines social cohesion. → What undermines...

• Governments should prioritise affordable housing. → What governments...

• This study reveals a pattern of systemic exclusion. → What this study...

📘 Grammar 2 — It-Cleft Sentences: «It is/was...that/who/which»

Структура It is/was + [выделяемая часть] + that/who/which + остаток предложения позволяет поставить акцент на любом элементе: подлежащем, дополнении, обстоятельстве времени или места.

Исходное предложение It-cleft Акцент на...
Poverty drives social exclusion. It is poverty that drives social exclusion. подлежащее
Poverty drives social exclusion. It is social exclusion that poverty drives. дополнение
The reform failed in 2010. It was in 2010 that the reform failed. время
Institutions, not individuals, shape behaviour. It is institutions that shape behaviour. причина/агент
The lack of data caused the error. It was the lack of data that caused the error. подлежащее

It-cleft: выбор союза

Что выделяем Союз Пример
Человек / люди who It is researchers who bear responsibility for accuracy.
Предмет / явление that / which It is social media that has transformed public discourse.
Место that / where It was in urban areas that inequality was most visible.
Время that / when It was during the 1990s that globalisation accelerated.
Причина that It is the absence of trust that undermines cooperation.
Неправильно Правильно
It is the government who should act. (правительство — не человек) It is the government that should act.
💡 Тест на правильность It-cleft: убери «It is/was...that» и восстанови оригинальное предложение — оно должно быть грамматически корректным. Если нет — структура неверна.

Задание 2

Перепиши предложения, используя It-cleft для выделения жирной части:

• URBANISATION changed social structures in the 20th century. → It was...

• Sociologists study PATTERNS OF INEQUALITY. → It is patterns...

• The policy failed BECAUSE OF POOR IMPLEMENTATION. → It was poor implementation that...

• COMMUNITY BONDS, not wealth, predict happiness. → It is community bonds...

• The data was collected IN DEVELOPING COUNTRIES. → It was in developing countries...

📘 Grammar 3 — Расширенные Cleft-структуры и сравнение типов

В академическом тексте встречаются и другие cleft-структуры. Важно уметь различать их и выбирать нужную в зависимости от того, что ты хочешь выделить.

Дополнительные Cleft-конструкции

Тип Структура Пример
All-cleft All + that + S + V + is/are + ... All that is needed is political will.
The reason-cleft The reason (why)...is (that)... The reason why cohesion fails is that trust has eroded.
The place-cleft The place where...is... The place where inequality is most acute is in cities.
The time-cleft The time when...is... The time when change is most needed is now.
Pseudo-cleft (reversed) [Выделяемая часть] + is + what + S + V A fairer distribution is what society demands.

What-cleft vs It-cleft: когда что использовать?

Критерий What-cleft It-cleft
Что выделяем Действие или идею (verb/NP) Конкретный элемент (noun/adverb)
Где стоит акцент В конце предложения В середине (сразу после It is)
Типичный контекст Вывод / рекомендация Уточнение / опровержение
Пример What society needs is structural change. It is structural change that society needs.
Ощущение Более философское, широкое Более точное, фактическое
Неправильно Правильно
What it is important is education. (двойная структура — ошибка!) What is important is education. / It is education that is important.
💡 Не смешивай What-cleft и It-cleft в одной конструкции! Это частая ошибка продвинутых студентов. Выбери одну структуру и строй предложение по её формуле.

IELTS подсказка

Для IELTS Writing Band 7–8 используй cleft-предложения стратегически: в topic sentence абзаца для акцента на ключевом аргументе, и в заключении для финального вывода. Не используй их в каждом предложении — это звучит искусственно.

• Society mainly needs greater social cohesion. → All that...

📘 Grammar 4 — Academic Language: Cleft Sentences в IELTS Writing

🔗 Связь с уровнем A: В A15 (Second Conditional) и A19 (Linking Words) ты строил сложные аргументы. Cleft Sentences — следующий шаг: теперь ты управляешь фокусом читателя, выделяя именно тот элемент, который усиливает твой аргумент.

Cleft Sentences в структуре IELTS-эссе

Часть эссе Функция cleft Пример
Introduction Акцент на теме It is the question of social inequality that this essay addresses.
Topic Sentence Акцент на аргументе What drives crime is not poverty alone, but a lack of opportunity.
Supporting sentence Акцент на факторе It is the absence of community networks that leaves individuals vulnerable.
Counter-argument Акцент на возражении What critics often overlook is the structural nature of disadvantage.
Conclusion Финальный акцент What this essay has demonstrated is that inequality is a systemic issue.

Academic Upgrade: Band 6 → Band 7

Band 5–6 Band 7 с Cleft Sentence
Social trust is very important for society. What holds societies together is a shared sense of social trust.
The government should change the policy. It is the policy framework, not individual behaviour, that requires urgent reform.
We need to look at structural factors. What this analysis reveals is the primacy of structural factors over individual agency.
Inequality grew because of globalisation. It was the unchecked expansion of globalisation that accelerated socioeconomic inequality.

IELTS подсказка

Academic Upgrade: Cleft Sentences сигнализируют экзаменатору об уровне Band 7+. Используй их для: (1) topic sentences в каждом абзаце; (2) введения counter-argument; (3) финального вывода в Conclusion. Старайся использовать оба типа (What + It is) в одном эссе — это демонстрирует синтаксическое разнообразие.

• Аргумент 1: Online interaction lacks depth → What online interaction lacks is...

• Аргумент 2: Algorithms promote division → It is algorithmic design that...

• Контраргумент: Critics say social media connects people → What critics argue is...

📄 Reading 1 — Социальная сплочённость

Прочитай текст. Найди и подчеркни все Cleft Sentences (What-cleft и It-cleft). Для каждого укажи тип и что именно выделяется.

Social cohesion — the degree to which members of a society feel connected and bound by shared values — has become a central concern for sociologists worldwide. What recent studies consistently show is that cohesion has declined significantly in many developed nations over the past three decades. It is not poverty alone that erodes social bonds; rather, it is the erosion of trust in institutions that appears to be the primary driver. What makes this trend particularly alarming is that communities with low social cohesion tend to exhibit higher rates of crime, mental illness, and political polarisation. Research indicates that what governments need to prioritise is not economic growth per se, but the rebuilding of civic infrastructure: community centres, local governance, and neighbourhood networks. It was during periods of economic instability that social trust declined most sharply, suggesting that the relationship between material conditions and social belonging is complex and bidirectional. What is clear is that no single policy intervention will suffice; a comprehensive, long-term strategy is required.

📄 Reading 2 — Социальная мобильность

Прочитай текст. Выпиши 5 Cleft Sentences. Для каждого: (1) определи тип; (2) назови выделяемый элемент; (3) перепиши то же предложение без cleft-структуры.

The concept of social mobility — the ability of individuals to move between social strata — lies at the heart of debates about justice and opportunity. What distinguishes highly mobile societies from rigid ones is not the absence of inequality, but the presence of accessible pathways to advancement. It is education, rather than inherited wealth, that most consistently predicts upward mobility in contemporary research. However, what critics of this view point out is that educational access itself is unequally distributed, meaning that the system reproduces rather than corrects social hierarchies. It was in Scandinavian countries that the strongest evidence for policy-driven mobility was found, largely due to universal childcare, comprehensive schooling, and robust social safety nets. What this suggests is that mobility is not a natural outcome of economic growth, but a deliberate achievement of institutional design. It is therefore the structure of opportunity, not individual effort alone, that determines social outcomes. What policymakers must acknowledge is that systemic change requires long-term political commitment.

Writing Task 1 — Cleft Sentence Transformation

Задание: Напиши Introduction + Body Paragraph 1

Тема:

"Some argue that social inequality is inevitable in any society. Others believe it can be significantly reduced through government policy."

Напиши только введение (2 предложения) и первый абзац (4–5 предложений) — ~80 слов.

Требования:

• Введение: используй It-cleft для акцента на теме

• Body 1 topic sentence: используй What-cleft

• Минимум один 'rather than' для контраста

• Академический словарь из Reading 1 и 2

Band Check: правильный cleft в topic sentence = Band 6.5+

Writing Task 2 — IELTS Essay (~190–200 слов)

IELTS Writing Task 2 — Discussion + Opinion Essay

Тема эссе:

"In many societies, social mobility is declining. What are the causes of this trend, and what measures can be taken to address it?"

Структура (~190–200 слов):

Introduction (2 предложения): It-cleft для акцента + thesis

Body 1 (4–5 предл.): Причины → What-cleft в topic sentence

Body 2 (4–5 предл.): Решения → It-cleft или All-cleft

Conclusion (2 предл.): What-cleft для финального аргумента

Обязательные конструкции:

• Минимум 2 What-cleft (в topic sentences или заключении)

• Минимум 1 It-cleft (во введении или body paragraph)

• Минимум 1 'rather than' или 'not...but' для контраста

• Академическая лексика: cohesion / mobility / structural / systemic

• Linking words: Furthermore / Nevertheless / What this suggests is

Band Check: 2+ cleft structures + academic lexis → Band 7
