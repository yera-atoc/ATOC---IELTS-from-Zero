Extended Metaphor & Academic Imagery

Как управляемая метафора делает абстрактный аргумент конкретным?

Тематика: Технологии и общество · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Opinion Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Три описания одного явления — алгоритмических рекомендательных систем:

Буквальное: Recommendation algorithms analyse user behaviour and show content that maximises engagement.

Одна метафора: Recommendation algorithms function as a mirror, reflecting users' existing preferences back at them.

Расширенная метафора: Recommendation algorithms function not as windows — through which users might encounter the unfamiliar — but as mirrors, reflecting existing preferences back with increasing precision; and like all mirrors, they offer not the world as it is but the world as the viewer has already decided it should appear.

Буквальное описание передаёт факт. Одна метафора добавляет образ. Расширенная метафора строит концептуальную модель: читатель не просто понимает алгоритм — он видит его в новом свете. Именно это и делает extended metaphor одним из самых мощных инструментов академической риторики.

📘 Grammar 1 · Метафора в академическом дискурсе: типология и функция

Dead metaphors · Conventional metaphors · Fresh metaphors · Conceptual metaphors
🟣

Студенты уровня B избегают метафор в IELTS — боясь, что это 'не академично'.

Студенты уровня C знают: академический текст насыщен метафорами — просто правильно управляемыми. Цель урока — научиться использовать их как аналитический инструмент, а не как украшение.
Тип метафоры Определение Академическая функция Пример в теме технологий
Dead metaphor Метафора, переставшая восприниматься как метафора Составляет большую часть академического словаря; невидима, но функциональна 'Address a problem', 'underpin an argument', 'erode trust', 'navigate regulations' — всё это мёртвые метафоры
Conventional metaphor Устойчивая метафора области знания Структурирует понимание концепции в данной дисциплине 'The information ecosystem', 'the digital marketplace', 'the attention economy' — метафоры, ставшие техническими терминами
Fresh / novel metaphor Новая метафора, создающая новый взгляд Делает абстрактное конкретным; запоминается; сигнализирует о концептуальной изощрённости 'Algorithms function not as windows but as mirrors' — свежая метафора для filter bubble
Conceptual / structural metaphor Метафора, через которую строится целая концепция Организует понимание всего явления: источник → цель TECHNOLOGY IS A TOOL (нейтральная) vs TECHNOLOGY IS AN ECOLOGY (органическая, взаимозависимая) vs TECHNOLOGY IS A MARKET (транзакционная, ценностная)
Extended metaphor Метафора, последовательно разворачиваемая через несколько предложений Строит концептуальную модель; позволяет исследовать все измерения явления Алгоритм как зеркало → зеркало искажает → зеркальный зал → эффект эхо-камеры

⚡ Почему 'technology is a tool' — слабее, чем 'technology is an ecology'?

Метафора инструмента: Technology is a tool — подразумевает пассивность, нейтральность, полный человеческий контроль. Инструмент делает то, что ему говорят. Этот взгляд не позволяет увидеть, что технология структурирует поведение, формирует ценности, создаёт зависимости.

Метафора экологии: Technology is an ecology — подразумевает взаимозависимость, эволюцию, неконтролируемые последствия, адаптацию. Экология не нейтральна: она формирует тех, кто в ней живёт.

Выбор концептуальной метафоры — это политический и аналитический выбор. Он предопределяет, какие вопросы кажутся разумными, а какие — абсурдными.
Концептуальная метафора Что она делает видимым Что она скрывает Академический контекст
Technology as TOOL Человеческий контроль, инструментальность Структурирующее воздействие на поведение, ценности, зависимости Дискурс нейтральности технологий; 'guns don't kill people'
Technology as ECOLOGY Взаимозависимость, эволюция, непредсказуемость Намеренное проектирование, корпоративная ответственность Дискурс сложных систем; теория сетей
Technology as ARCHITECTURE Дизайн среды формирует поведение Агентность пользователей, возможность сопротивления Выбор архитектуры (C9); «nudge» теория (C13)
Technology as MARKET Конкуренция, стимулы, рациональные агенты Власть, неравенство, монополизация, общественные блага Экономический детерминизм; дискурс регулирования
Technology as MIRROR Отражение, нарциссизм, искажение Активное формирование, а не пассивное отражение Критика filter bubble; epistemicharm
Technology as INFRASTRUCTURE Невидимость, встроенность, зависимость Сознательный выбор дизайна, возможность реконструкции Политэкономия платформ; digital commons

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The dominant metaphor through which digital platforms have been conceptualised — both in popular discourse and in much of the regulatory literature — is that of a marketplace: a neutral space in which buyers and sellers of information, attention, and goods freely exchange their respective surpluses. This framing, while rhetorically convenient, systematically obscures the degree to which the architecture of such platforms does not merely facilitate exchange but actively shapes the preferences, behaviours, and epistemic orientations of those who participate within it.

① The dominant metaphor through which — nominalisaion of 'people conceptualise X through metaphor'

② that of a marketplace — nominal substitute (C16): 'that' replaces 'the dominant metaphor'

③ a neutral space in which buyers and sellers... freely exchange their respective surpluses — extends the marketplace metaphor: each word activates a different dimension

④ This framing, while rhetorically convenient — comment clause (C13) + admission of rhetorical utility before critique
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Technology is like a double-edged sword which can be good or bad.

Правильно: Digital technology functions less as a neutral instrument — the double-edged sword of popular discourse — than as an architecture of incentives that systematically shapes the behaviours it purports merely to facilitate.

Почему: 'Double-edged sword' — мёртвая клише-метафора, не добавляет аналитической ценности. В академическом тексте: либо развивай метафору концептуально, либо не используй её

Ошибка: Social media is a monster that is eating our brains.

Правильно: Social media platforms are more accurately conceptualised as attentional architectures — systems specifically engineered to maximise the duration and intensity of cognitive engagement, regardless of its quality or consequences.

Почему: Эмоциональная метафора ('monster', 'eating our brains') — не академический регистр. Замени на концептуальную метафору с аналитической нагрузкой
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Metaphor in Academic Discourse

✔ Conventional metaphor ('attention economy', 'information ecosystem') — корректное использование дисциплинарного языка (LR Band 7+)

✔ Fresh metaphor с аналитическим развитием → демонстрирует концептуальную изощрённость (TA Band 8)

✔ Critique of another's metaphor ('this framing... systematically obscures') → показывает метауровень аналитического мышления
⚠ Снижает балл: Эмоциональная метафора без аналитической нагрузки ('is a monster', 'is destroying society') — нарушение регистра (C15) и снижение TA

📙 Grammar 2 · Building an Extended Metaphor: как разворачивать метафору

Source domain → Target domain mapping · Entailments · Limits of the metaphor
🟣

Одна метафора — это образ.

Extended metaphor — это концептуальная модель. Она разворачивается через несколько предложений, последовательно используя разные аспекты исходной области (source domain) для освещения целевой области (target domain).

📐 Структура extended metaphor: source → target mapping

Шаг 1 — Установи source domain: 'The digital public sphere functions, in many respects, as an ecosystem.'

Шаг 2 — Развивай entailments (следствия метафоры): 'Like any ecosystem, it is structured by competitive dynamics — some organisms [platforms] consuming the resources [attention] upon which others depend.'

Шаг 3 — Углубляй аналитическую ценность: 'The introduction of a dominant predator [monopolistic platform] into an ecosystem that previously supported diverse species [independent media] tends to reduce biodiversity — and, with it, the resilience of the system as a whole.'

Шаг 4 — Признай ограничения метафоры: 'The ecosystem metaphor, however, risks naturalising what is in fact a product of deliberate regulatory choices: ecosystems evolve; platforms are designed.'
Source domain Target domain Ключевые entailments Аналитическая ценность Ограничение
Ecosystem / ecology Digital media environment Biodiversity, predation, resilience, collapse Позволяет говорить о системных рисках, монополии, устойчивости Натурализует: экосистемы не проектируются людьми
Architecture / infrastructure Platform design Built environment shapes behaviour, invisible, hard to change 'Choice architecture' (C13); дизайн как политика; lock-in эффект Скрывает агентность пользователей
Mirror / reflection Personalisation algorithms Reflection vs distortion, narcissism, self-reinforcement Filter bubble, echo chamber, epistemic closure Показывает пассивность алгоритма, скрывает его активную роль
Marketplace / economy Attention and data Supply/demand, pricing, commodity, monopoly 'Attention economy' (Goldhaber); platform economics Натурализует commodification сознания
Public square / agora Social media Civic participation, voice, exclusion, cacophony Нормативное измерение; standards of civility Идеализирует Agora; классическая Agora была эксклюзивной
Nervous system Internet infrastructure Signal transmission, reflex, paralysis, vulnerability Уязвимость, критическая зависимость, кибербезопасность Органицистская: скрывает политическое измерение контроля

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

If the early internet was imagined as a public square — open, egalitarian, governed by the norms of civil discourse — then what has emerged in its place is something closer to a private shopping mall: a space that mimics the visual grammar of public life while remaining, in every legally and architecturally significant respect, the exclusive property of its owners, who reserve the right to determine who speaks, what is displayed, and under what conditions participation is permitted.

① imagined as a public square — establishes the first term of the extended metaphor; 'imagined' signals it was always a metaphor

② what has emerged in its place is something closer to a private shopping mall — contrast metaphor: same visual grammar, different ownership structure

③ mimics the visual grammar of public life — 'visual grammar' is itself a dead metaphor activated freshly here

④ who reserve the right to determine who speaks, what is displayed, and under what conditions — tricolon (C17) within the extended metaphor
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: The internet is like a jungle where only the strongest survive.

Правильно: The digital media landscape increasingly resembles an ecological monoculture — one in which a small number of dominant platform species have displaced the diverse and competitive environment that once characterised the early web.

Почему: 'Jungle where only the strongest survive' — Social Darwinism клише. Замени на конкретную экологическую аналогию ('monoculture', 'displacement', 'species diversity') с аналитической нагрузкой

Ошибка: Technology is a mirror but also it is a window and a door at the same time.

Правильно: Whether digital technology functions primarily as a mirror — reflecting and reinforcing existing dispositions — or as a window — opening perspectives that would otherwise remain inaccessible — depends, I would contend, less on the technology itself than on the regulatory and design choices that govern its deployment.

Почему: Смешение трёх метафор в одном предложении без структуры — хаотично. Extended metaphor требует последовательности: одна метафора за раз, с аналитическим развитием
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Extended Metaphor

✔ Source → target mapping с 3–4 entailments → TA Band 8: автор строит концептуальную модель, не просто описывает

✔ Признание ограничений метафоры ('the metaphor risks naturalising...') → показывает метааналитическое мышление

✔ Two contrasting metaphors (public square → shopping mall) → TA: демонстрирует изменение через образ
⚠ Снижает балл: Расширенная метафора без аналитической нагрузки — красивая пустота; каждый шаг метафоры должен освещать новое измерение аргумента

📗 Grammar 3 · Controlled Imagery in Formal Prose

Analogy · Thought experiment · Concrete illustration · Qualifying the image
🟣

Extended metaphor — это образная концептуальная модель.

В Grammar 3 мы изучаем более широкий арсенал образных инструментов, которые в академическом тексте делают абстрактное конкретным — без потери формального регистра.
Инструмент Функция Синтаксическая форма Пример
Analogy (explicit) Поясняет принцип через хорошо известную область 'X is analogous to Y in that...' / 'Like Y, X...' / 'In much the same way that Y, X...' In much the same way that a river constrained to a single channel flows faster and more destructively than one dispersed across a floodplain, attention concentrated by algorithmic recommendation produces more intense but less diverse epistemic outcomes.
Thought experiment Проверяет принцип через мысленный сценарий (C14) 'Imagine a context in which...' / 'Consider a scenario where...' / 'Suppose that...' Consider a scenario in which every newspaper reader received a personalised edition containing only articles likely to confirm their existing views — the epistemic consequences would be immediately obvious. This is, in essence, the situation that algorithmic content curation has created.
Concrete illustration Делает абстрактное ощутимым через конкретный пример 'Consider the case of...' / 'Take, for instance,...' / 'A striking example is afforded by...' A striking example is afforded by the documented tendency of recommendation algorithms to amplify emotionally provocative content: not because such content is more true or more important, but because it generates more engagement — and engagement, in the attention economy, is the currency through which platform value is measured.
Spatial / visual image Создаёт пространственную метафору для абстрактного явления Formal use of 'landscape', 'terrain', 'architecture', 'sphere' The information landscape that has emerged under algorithmic curation is not a flat plain of equally accessible content but a deeply contoured terrain in which certain ideas occupy commanding heights while others remain in permanent shadow.
Qualifying the image Признаёт ограничения образа, сохраняя его ценность 'The analogy is imperfect in one important respect...' / 'The metaphor has its limits...' / 'It would be misleading to press this analogy too far...' The analogy between platform monopoly and industrial monopoly is suggestive but imperfect: unlike steel or railways, the 'product' that digital platforms monopolise is not a physical commodity but the structure of human attention and the architecture of epistemic access.

⚡ Qualifying the image: обязательный шаг

В академическом тексте использование аналогии или метафоры без признания её ограничений — это риторически слабая позиция: читатель-эксперт сразу видит, где аналогия ломается, и может использовать это против аргумента.

Слабо: 'Algorithms are like addictive drugs — and therefore should be regulated like them.'

Сильно: 'The analogy between algorithmic engagement mechanics and addiction pharmacology is, it must be acknowledged, imprecise — yet it captures something important: both operate through reward circuitry in ways that can override deliberate choice. The analogy's utility lies not in its precision but in its illumination of a governance gap: we regulate substances that exploit reward circuitry; we do not yet systematically regulate software that does the same.'

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The information landscape that algorithmic curation has produced is not, as is sometimes suggested, a simple echo chamber — a metaphor that implies a static enclosure from which no external sound penetrates. It is, rather, something more dynamic and more troubling: a finely tuned acoustic environment in which certain frequencies are systematically amplified, others attenuated, and the listener is gradually habituated to the resulting soundscape as if it were the natural distribution of signal and noise.

① not, as is sometimes suggested, a simple echo chamber — acknowledges the common metaphor and immediately extends it

② a metaphor that implies a static enclosure — appositive NP critiquing the source domain of the existing metaphor

③ a finely tuned acoustic environment — new extended metaphor: moves from 'echo' to 'acoustic engineering'

④ amplified / attenuated / habituated — three verbs from acoustic/psychological domain: consistent metaphor extension
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Technology is destroying society, just like the Black Death destroyed medieval Europe.

Правильно: The disruption wrought by algorithmic media upon established information ecosystems is, in some respects, analogous to the disruption caused by the printing press — though the analogy should not be pressed too far, since the printing press, unlike algorithmic curation, did not actively select which ideas would be amplified.

Почему: Аналогия с Чёрной смертью эмоциональна и аналитически бесполезна. Продуктивная аналогия должна освещать механизм: printing press → algorithmic curation = disruption of information ecosystem, но с важным различием

Ошибка: If we think about it like a river, technology flows and sometimes floods.

Правильно: Technology diffusion can be usefully conceptualised as a hydrological process: new platforms spread through social networks along paths of least resistance, pooling in communities of shared interest and only subsequently — if at all — breaching the embankments of institutional constraint that regulation attempts to construct.

Почему: 'Flows and sometimes floods' — слишком поверхностное развитие. Extended metaphor требует развёртывания через конкретные entailments: 'paths of least resistance', 'pooling', 'embankments of constraint' — каждый аспект метафоры несёт аналитическую нагрузку
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Controlled Imagery

✔ Concrete illustration ('a striking example is afforded by') → TA: конкретный пример заземляет абстрактный аргумент

✔ Qualifying the image ('the analogy is imperfect in one respect') → TA + GRA: сложность мышления, а не упрощение

✔ Spatial image ('information landscape', 'commanding heights') → LR Band 8: мёртвые метафоры оживляются в системе
⚠ Снижает балл: Аналогия, которую можно легко опровергнуть, слабее, чем отсутствие аналогии; всегда признавай ограничения образа

📕 Grammar 4 · Integration & Band 8 Upgrade

Метафора как аргументативная архитектура: от образа к аналитическому выводу
🟣

Метафора в академическом тексте — не украшение и не иллюстрация.

В Grammar 4 мы видим, как она становится структурой аргумента: каждый шаг метафоры соответствует шагу аналитической мысли.
Аргументативный шаг Метафорический инструмент Синтаксическая форма Пример
Установить концептуальную рамку Введение source domain 'X can be usefully conceptualised as...' / 'The dominant framing treats X as...' Digital platforms have been conceptualised — in regulatory discourse, at least — as neutral conduits for user-generated content, analogous to telecommunications infrastructure.
Развернуть entailments Последовательное расширение метафоры Series of parallel structures within source domain (C17) Like telecommunications infrastructure, they carry content they do not create; like publishers, they profit from content they do not pay for; unlike either, they actively shape the ranking, visibility, and emotional framing of what they transmit.
Обнажить пределы конкурирующей метафоры Critique of the source domain of a rival metaphor 'This framing, however, systematically obscures...' / 'The metaphor breaks down at precisely the point where...' The conduit metaphor, however, breaks down at precisely the point where it would be most useful: a conduit transmits without selection; an algorithm curates, amplifies, and suppresses.
Предложить альтернативную концептуальную метафору Introduce rival source domain 'A more apt analogy would be...' / 'It is more illuminating to conceptualise X as...' A more analytically productive framing would treat digital platforms not as conduits but as curators — and, as any museum visitor knows, curation is never neutral.
Признать ограничение новой метафоры Qualify and close 'The curator metaphor, in turn, has its limits...' / 'It would be misleading to press this analogy too far...' The curator analogy, in turn, risks underplaying the scale and automation of algorithmic selection — no human curator operates at the speed of two billion decisions per second.

📐 Band 8 Metaphorical Argument: полная архитектура

Предложение 1 — Установить рамку: The regulatory framework governing digital platforms has, since its inception, been structured around a single foundational metaphor: the platform as a neutral conduit, carrying content without selecting it, transmitting without endorsing it.

Предложение 2 — Развернуть entailments: This framing carries with it a set of legally and politically consequential entailments: as conduits, platforms cannot be held responsible for what flows through them; as neutral infrastructure, they require no editorial oversight; as passive intermediaries, they deserve the liability protections that were designed for telephone exchanges.

Предложение 3 — Обнажить предел: The metaphor, however, collapses the moment one asks what a telephone exchange would look like if it decided, algorithmically, which calls to connect based on which conversations were most likely to generate additional calls.

Предложение 4 — Альтернатива + квалификация: If platforms must be compared to anything, it is not to telephone exchanges but to editors — active selectors of what an audience sees, hears, and, ultimately, believes — though even this analogy understates the scale at which algorithmic editorial decisions are made.
🚀 Band 8 Upgrade

Band 6: Social media companies are not responsible for what users post. They are just a platform like a telephone company. But they do control what people see, so maybe they should be regulated.

Band 7: Social media platforms operate under a legal framework that treats them as neutral intermediaries. However, since they actively curate content through algorithmic selection, this framework may be inadequate.

Band 8: The legal architecture governing digital platforms rests upon a foundational analogy that has ceased to describe the reality it purports to govern: the platform as neutral conduit. A telephone exchange transmits without selecting; an algorithm curates without disclosing. A publisher chooses what to amplify; so, at two billion decisions per second, does an algorithmic feed. The conduit metaphor, far from being a neutral description, is a regulatory choice — and one whose consequences, as the epistemic landscape of contemporary democracy attests, have been considerable.

📖 Reading 1 · The Architecture of Attention

Академическое чтение: extended metaphor в критическом анализе технологий
📖 The Architecture of Attention: Algorithmic Curation, Epistemic Inequality, and the Metaphorical Framing of Platform Power

The metaphors through which we conceptualise digital media are not merely rhetorical conveniences — they are, in a substantive sense, constitutive of the regulatory and political frameworks within which platforms operate. The dominant metaphor — the platform as a neutral conduit or marketplace for ideas — has, since the early 1990s, provided the conceptual infrastructure for a regulatory approach premised on minimal intervention, maximum autonomy, and the assumption that the free flow of information is, in itself, epistemically beneficial. This framing, while rhetorically powerful, rests upon an analogy that has become increasingly difficult to sustain: a marketplace in which the prices are set not by competitive dynamics but by engagement metrics engineered to maximise addiction is not a free market in any meaningful sense.

A more analytically productive metaphor — one that is gaining traction in both academic and regulatory discourse — conceptualises digital platforms not as neutral conduits but as curators of the information environment: active selectors, rankers, and amplifiers whose editorial choices, however automated, produce structural effects on the epistemic landscape that are no less consequential for being the product of an algorithm rather than a human editor. Crucially, curators are not neutral. Every museum exhibition, every curated playlist, every algorithmically generated feed embodies a set of values about what is important, what is legitimate, and what deserves attention. The question is not whether platforms curate but whether the values embedded in their curation should be subject to democratic accountability.

It would be misleading, however, to press the curator analogy too far. A human curator operates within institutional frameworks that impose constraints — ethical, legal, professional — that have no algorithmic equivalent. More precisely, the scale, speed, and opacity of algorithmic curation produce effects that differ not merely in degree but in kind from anything that human editorial practice has previously encompassed. The information landscape that results is best described, perhaps, not as a curated gallery but as an acoustic environment: one in which the amplification of certain frequencies — outrage, anxiety, tribal identity — has been optimised to maximise the duration of listener engagement, with consequences for democratic deliberation that are, at this stage, difficult to overstate.

📖 Reading 2 · Data as Resource: Metaphor and Policy

Концептуальная метафора в регуляторном дискурсе
📖 Data as Resource: The Politics of the 'Data as Oil' Metaphor and Its Alternatives

Few metaphors have been more influential in shaping contemporary debates about data governance than 'data is the new oil'. The analogy captures something genuine: like oil, data is a raw material that requires processing to yield value; like oil, its extraction generates both wealth and externalities; like oil, its ownership has become a source of geopolitical contestation and market concentration. Yet the metaphor is, in at least one crucial respect, systematically misleading: oil is a finite, rivalrous resource whose consumption depletes it; data, by contrast, is non-rivalrous and non-depleting — sharing data does not diminish it, and using it does not consume it.

A more analytically productive alternative — one that better captures the distinctive political economy of data — conceptualises data not as a resource to be extracted but as a public infrastructure to be governed. Like roads, telecommunications networks, and electrical grids, data infrastructure exhibits the characteristics of natural monopoly: the marginal cost of adding a new user approaches zero, while the social costs of exclusion from access are substantial. The implications of this framing are, as one might expect, quite different from those of the oil metaphor: where oil suggests a market in commodities, infrastructure suggests a public utility subject to democratic oversight and universal service obligations.

Neither metaphor, it should be noted, is wholly adequate — as it were, each illuminates one face of a complex object while casting another in shadow. The oil metaphor captures the competitive dynamics of data markets; the infrastructure metaphor captures the public goods dimension. What is, perhaps, most instructive about this debate is not which metaphor is correct but what it reveals about the function of metaphor in policy discourse: the choice between 'data as oil' and 'data as infrastructure' is not merely a choice of words but a choice of regulatory philosophy — and, by extension, a choice about the distribution of economic power in the digital economy.

💡 Central Question — Ответ:

Управляемая метафора делает абстрактный аргумент конкретным — не потому что упрощает его, а потому что она переносит читателя в область, где отношения между вещами уже интуитивно понятны. Когда ты говоришь 'платформа как куратор', читатель немедленно понимает: кураторы выбирают, кураторы несут ответственность, кураторы воплощают ценности. Метафора не заменяет аргумент — она конкретизирует его форму. И именно поэтому её выбор — всегда аналитический и политический акт.
