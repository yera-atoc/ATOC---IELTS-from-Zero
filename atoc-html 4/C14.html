Урок C14 · Upper-Intermediate / Advanced

Counterfactual & Hypothetical Thinking

Как «невозможные» предложения делают аргумент реальнее?

Тематика: ИИ и этика · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Advantages/Disadvantages Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Два аргумента об ИИ:

Band 6: AI is dangerous because it can make mistakes that hurt people.

Band 8: Were autonomous AI systems to be deployed in critical infrastructure without adequate oversight, the consequences of a single misaligned decision could, in a matter of hours, cascade across interconnected networks in ways that no human intervention would be sufficiently rapid to prevent — a scenario that, while hypothetical, is by no means beyond the bounds of current technical possibility.

Band 6 называет опасность. Band 8 разворачивает контрфактический сценарий: конкретный, детализированный, с временными и причинными цепочками. Читатель не просто понимает угрозу — он её представляет. Именно это и есть сила гипотетического мышления: оно превращает абстрактный риск в воображаемую реальность.

📘 Grammar 1 · Unreal Conditionals: настоящее и будущее

Were + subject · Should + subject · If + past subjunctive · Had it not been — Advanced Type 2 & Mixed
🟣

В уровне A ты знал Type 2: 'If I were rich, I would buy a house.'

В уровне B ты расширил до Type 3: 'If I had studied, I would have passed.'

В уровне C мы изучаем инвертированные формы, формальные subjunctive и смешанные условные — конструкции, которые в академическом тексте сигнализируют о профессиональном анализе.
Конструкция Формула Значение Академический контекст
Inverted Type 2 (formal) Were + subject + to-inf... , would/could/might + bare inf Гипотетическое настоящее/будущее в формальном стиле Were autonomous AI to be deployed without oversight, the risks would be considerable.
Inverted Type 2 (present state) Were + subject + adj/NP... , would/could/might... Нереальное условие о текущем состоянии Were the regulatory framework more robust, developers would face stronger accountability.
Should + inversion (formal) Should + subject + bare inf... , imperative/would Маловероятное, но мыслимое будущее условие Should a breakthrough in artificial general intelligence occur within the decade, existing governance structures would prove wholly inadequate.
Suppose / Supposing Suppose/Supposing (that) + past simple... , would/could/might Менее формально; приглашение рассмотреть сценарий Supposing that AI-generated content becomes indistinguishable from human-authored material, what mechanisms could preserve epistemic authenticity?
What if (analytical) What if + past simple / were to... Риторический вопрос как аргументативный приём What if the primary beneficiaries of AI automation were not consumers but capital owners — would that alter the ethical calculus?

⚡ Were to vs If... were: почему инверсия делает предложение академичнее

Стандартный Type 2: 'If AI were deployed without oversight, risks would increase.'

Инвертированный (академический): 'Were AI to be deployed without oversight, the attendant risks would increase substantially.'

Три отличия инвертированной формы:

1. Убирает 'If' — более лаконично и формально

2. 'Were... to be' — подчёркивает гипотетичность сильнее, чем 'If... were'

3. Освобождает начало предложения для fronting (C9) — 'Were autonomous AI systems...'

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Were artificial general intelligence to emerge within the next two decades — a prospect that leading researchers regard as, at minimum, plausible — the consequences for labour markets, epistemic autonomy, and democratic governance would, I would contend, be of a magnitude that existing institutional frameworks are wholly unprepared to address.

① Were artificial general intelligence to emerge — inverted Type 2: formal hypothetical; 'to emerge' подчёркивает дистанцию от факта

② a prospect that leading researchers regard as, at minimum, plausible — appositive NP + hedging: гипотеза обоснована, не фантастика

③ the consequences... would, I would contend, be — epistemic parenthetical из C13 внутри главной клаузы

④ of a magnitude that existing institutional frameworks are wholly unprepared to address — complex NP + defining relative: масштаб описан через неготовность, а не через прямую характеристику
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Were AI would be used in hospitals, it might make mistakes.

Правильно: Were AI to be deployed in clinical settings without adequate safeguards, the risk of consequential diagnostic errors would be non-trivial.

Почему: После 'Were + subject' следует to-infinitive, не 'would'. 'Would' стоит только в главной клаузе (consequence)

Ошибка: Should AI becomes more powerful, we need new laws.

Правильно: Should AI systems become substantially more capable within the current decade, existing regulatory frameworks would require fundamental redesign.

Почему: После 'Should' в инвертированном условном — bare infinitive, не 'becomes'. Главная клауза требует 'would/could/might + bare infinitive'
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Unreal Present/Future Conditionals

✔ 'Were + subject + to-inf' → немедленный сигнал GRA Band 7–8: редкая инвертированная форма

✔ 'Should + inversion' для маловероятного сценария → точность гипотетического мышления (TA)

✔ Inverted conditional + appositive NP-хеджинг → комбинация C14 + C12 = Band 8 GRA
⚠ Снижает балл: Inverted conditional в условной клаузе + 'would' вместо to-inf ('Were AI would') — грубая ошибка, немедленно снижает GRA

📙 Grammar 2 · Counterfactual Past: продвинутый Type 3

Had it not been for · Had + subject + past participle · But for · Only if · Even if
🟣

Type 3 в базовом варианте: 'If they had regulated AI earlier, fewer harms would have occurred.'

В уровне C мы изучаем инвертированные и сокращённые формы прошедшего контрфактического, а также специальные конструкции, которые выражают контрфактические отношения без явного 'if'.
Конструкция Формула Значение Пример
Inverted Type 3 Had + subject + past participle... , would/could/might + have + pp Гипотетическое прошлое — формальный вариант Had the algorithmic bias been identified at the design stage, the discriminatory outcomes could have been prevented.
Had it not been for Had it not been for + NP... , would/could/might + have + pp Без X — Y бы не произошло Had it not been for the whistleblower's disclosure, the extent of the data misuse would have remained concealed from the public.
But for But for + NP... , would/could/might + (have) + pp Формальный эквивалент 'Had it not been for' But for the intervention of independent researchers, the flawed training data would have continued to produce biased outputs indefinitely.
Even if (counterfactual) Even if + past perfect... , would still + have + pp Контрфактическое уступительное: Y верно несмотря на X Even if the developers had implemented standard bias-detection protocols, the underlying data distribution would still have produced systematically skewed results.
Only if (counterfactual) Only if + past perfect... , would + have + pp (inverted) Единственное достаточное условие в прошлом Only if mandatory algorithmic audits had been introduced before deployment would the scale of the problem have become apparent.

📐 Контрфактическое рассуждение как аргументативный метод

Контрфактическое предложение — это не просто грамматика. Это аналитический инструмент, который позволяет:

1. Установить причинность: 'Had X not occurred, Y would not have followed' → X была необходимой причиной Y.

2. Оценить альтернативы: 'Had policy P been adopted, outcome O would have been avoided' → P была жизнеспособной альтернативой.

3. Показать необратимость: 'Even if X had been done, Y would still have occurred' → X не была достаточной мерой.

4. Выделить единственный выход: 'Only if X had happened would Y have been prevented' → X была единственным достаточным условием.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Had the major technology companies operated under a binding international regulatory framework from the outset — rather than in the largely ungoverned digital commons that actually prevailed — the accumulation of monopolistic data advantage that now renders meaningful competition structurally impossible would, in all likelihood, have been substantially curtailed.

① Had the major technology companies operated under — inverted Type 3: formal counterfactual; no 'if' required

② rather than in the largely ungoverned digital commons that actually prevailed — embedded contrast: 'actually prevailed' подчёркивает реальность как контраст к гипотезе

③ in all likelihood — epistemic adverbial phrase: хеджинг counterfactual consequence

④ would... have been substantially curtailed — passive perfect in consequence clause + degree adverb
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Had they would have regulated AI, problems wouldn't happen.

Правильно: Had they implemented robust AI regulation at the outset, many of the harms that have since materialised would have been preventable.

Почему: Никогда не используй 'would have' в if/had-клаузе. 'Had' — это уже прошедший subjunctive; consequence = 'would + have + pp'

Ошибка: But for the lack of regulation, AI became dangerous.

Правильно: But for the absence of adequate regulatory oversight, the current landscape of unchecked algorithmic deployment would not have emerged.

Почему: 'But for' требует условного следствия ('would have'), а не простого прошедшего
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Counterfactual Past

✔ 'Had it not been for + NP' → элегантная замена длинному 'If it had not been for'; LR Band 8

✔ 'But for' в академическом анализе причинности → редкая конструкция, сигнал зрелого письма (GRA)

✔ 'Even if + past perfect... would still have' → логика необходимости vs достаточности = сильный аналитический сигнал (TA)
⚠ Снижает балл: 'Would have' в if/had-клаузе — самая частая ошибка в counterfactual; немедленно снижает GRA

📗 Grammar 3 · As if / As though / As were · Imagining a world where

Сравнительные и гипотетические конструкции для аналитического воображения
🟣

'As if' и 'as though' — это не просто сравнение.

В уровне C они становятся инструментами риторического и аналитического воображения: автор создаёт иллюзию реальности для сценария, который ещё не существует — или никогда не существовал.
Конструкция Временная логика Академическая функция Пример
as if / as though + past simple Нереальное настоящее Описание текущей ситуации как будто что-то нереальное истинно Technology companies currently operate as if the social consequences of their products were entirely external to their domain of responsibility.
as if / as though + past perfect Нереальное прошлое Критика текущего состояния через прошлое нереальное Regulators have responded to algorithmic harm as if the framework developed for broadcast media were directly applicable to decentralised digital platforms.
as it were Метафорическая оговорка Смягчает нестандартное употребление слова или метафору The algorithm functions as a hidden mediator, as it were, between producer intent and consumer choice.
Imagine / Imagine a world where Риторический приём Создаёт гипотетический сценарий для проверки принципа Imagine a world in which every decision affecting a citizen's access to credit, healthcare, or employment were made by an opaque algorithmic system — what democratic accountability mechanisms would remain meaningful?
Think of a scenario where Менее риторический, более аналитический Вводит конкретный мысленный эксперимент Think of a scenario where a self-driving vehicle must choose between two equally harmful outcomes: existing legal frameworks offer no clear resolution.

⚡ As if + past vs as if + past perfect: временная точность

As if + past simple — нереальное настоящее:

'Companies behave as if regulation were irrelevant.'

→ В настоящий момент они ведут себя так, как будто (но это не так сейчас)

As if + past perfect — нереальное прошлое:

'The company responded as if no warnings had ever been issued.'

→ Их реакция в прошлом выглядела так, как будто (но предупреждения были)

⚠ В формальном академическом тексте 'as if + were' (subjunctive) предпочтительнее 'as if + was'.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Contemporary AI governance frameworks are designed as if the fundamental questions of liability, transparency, and accountability had already been resolved — as if, that is, the regulatory categories developed for human agents and corporate entities could be seamlessly mapped onto systems whose decision-making processes are, by design, opaque even to their creators.

① are designed as if... had already been resolved — as if + past perfect: критика текущей политики через нереальное прошлое

② as if, that is — parenthetical reformulation (C13) встроен внутрь второго 'as if': двойная гипотеза

③ the regulatory categories developed for human agents — reduced participial post-modifier внутри hypothetical clause

④ whose decision-making processes are, by design, opaque even to their creators — sentence adverb 'by design' (C13) + relative clause
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Companies act as if they don't have responsibility for AI harms.

Правильно: Technology companies operate as if the social consequences of their systems were entirely external to their domain of accountability.

Почему: После 'as if' в нереальном настоящем — past subjunctive ('were', не 'don't have'). Subjunctive 'were' работает для всех лиц

Ошибка: Imagine a world where AI makes all decisions and what would happen.

Правильно: Imagine a world in which all consequential civic decisions were delegated to algorithmic systems — what democratic accountability structures would remain functional?

Почему: 'Imagine a world where' требует subordinate clause + риторический вопрос или следствие. Нельзя просто добавить 'and what would happen' — это не завершённая структура
🔵 Examiner's Eye — As if / Imagining

✔ 'As if + past perfect' для критики текущей политики → аналитическая элегантность без прямого обвинения (TA + LR)

✔ 'Imagine a world in which... were... — what would remain?' → риторический мысленный эксперимент = Band 8 TA

✔ 'As it were' для метафоры → показывает осознанность нестандартного употребления (GRA + LR)
⚠ Снижает балл: 'As if + will/would' — грамматически невозможно в нереальном условном; 'as if + present simple' используется только в реальном сравнении

📕 Grammar 4 · Интеграция и Band 8 Upgrade

Контрфактическое мышление как аргументативная архитектура: от предположения к анализу
🟣

Теперь ты знаешь три инструмента гипотетического мышления.

В Grammar 4 мы видим, как они работают вместе в аргументативном эссе: гипотетический сценарий → контрфактический анализ → аналитическое воображение — как три шага одного интеллектуального движения.
Аргументативная задача Инструмент Пример Логическая функция
Показать риск будущего Were + to / Should Were AI to be granted legal personhood, the entire architecture of human rights law would require reconstruction. Конкретизирует абстрактный риск через сценарий
Установить историческую причину Had... / But for But for the absence of binding international AI standards, the proliferation of surveillance capitalism would have been substantially constrained. Доказывает причинность через её отсутствие
Показать недостаточность меры Even if... would still Even if algorithmic transparency were mandated, users would still lack the technical literacy to evaluate the outputs they receive. Опровергает предложенное решение логически
Критиковать текущую ситуацию As if + past subjunctive Policymakers continue to regulate AI as if it were simply a more efficient version of existing technologies. Разоблачает ложную аналогию
Создать мысленный эксперимент Imagine a world where Imagine a world in which all hiring decisions were made by algorithms with no human review — what notion of procedural justice would remain coherent? Приглашает читателя почувствовать последствия

📐 Band 8 Counterfactual Paragraph — полная архитектура

Тезис (прямое утверждение): The absence of binding international AI governance frameworks represents one of the most consequential policy failures of the current era.

Контрфактический анализ (Had... / But for): Had such frameworks been established during the formative years of the commercial internet — when the infrastructural dependencies that now make retroactive regulation so difficult were still being formed — the current concentration of algorithmic power in a small number of private entities would have been substantially less likely to emerge.

Текущая критика (as if): Instead, regulators have responded as if each successive technological platform were a novel and unprecedented phenomenon, rather than a variation upon a recurring structural pattern of market concentration and regulatory arbitrage.

Мысленный эксперимент (Imagine): Were this pattern to continue unchecked, one might reasonably ask: what remains of democratic accountability in a world in which the infrastructural conditions of public life are determined by systems whose objectives are opaque, whose operators are extraterritorial, and whose consequences are irreversible?
🚀 Band 8 Upgrade

Band 6: AI is dangerous if not regulated. If we don't regulate it, bad things will happen.

Band 7: Without adequate regulation, AI systems may produce harmful outcomes that prove difficult to reverse. Had stronger governance frameworks been in place earlier, some of these risks might have been mitigated.

Band 8: Were autonomous AI systems to be deployed at scale without binding international governance frameworks — a scenario that, it must be acknowledged, falls within the bounds of current technical and commercial trajectory — the consequences for epistemic autonomy, labour market stability, and democratic accountability would, had the formative window for preventive regulation now passed, be considerably more difficult to address than proponents of a permissive approach have, thus far, been willing to acknowledge.
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Full Counterfactual Integration

✔ Were... to + Had... + as if + Imagine в одном абзаце → системное владение гипотетическим мышлением = Band 8 TA + GRA

✔ Counterfactual как аналитический метод (причинность, достаточность, альтернатива) → высший уровень TA

✔ Смешение с C9 (fronting), C12 (complex NP), C13 (comment clauses) → синтез уровня C = Band 8 GRA
⚠ Снижает балл: Гипотетический сценарий без аналитического вывода — это не аргумент; всегда показывай, что следует из мысленного эксперимента

📖 Reading 1 · The Alignment Problem

Академическое чтение: контрфактическое мышление в ИИ-дискурсе
📖 The Alignment Problem: Hypothetical Risks, Counterfactual Reasoning, and the Ethics of Precaution

It is worth noting, at the outset, that debates surrounding the long-term risks of advanced artificial intelligence are, in a distinctive sense, inherently counterfactual: they concern not what has happened but what might happen, under conditions that do not yet obtain. Were a system with genuinely general cognitive capabilities to emerge — a development that leading researchers regard as, at minimum, a non-negligible possibility within the coming decades — the question of whether its objectives would remain aligned with human values cannot, it would appear, be resolved by extrapolation from the behaviour of current systems.

Had the developers of early social media platforms modelled the long-term societal consequences of engagement-maximising recommendation algorithms — rather than treating them as if they were simply neutral conduits for user preference — the epistemic and democratic harms that have since materialised might, at least in part, have been anticipated and constrained by design. This counterfactual observation does not, admittedly, demonstrate that similar foresight would have proved sufficient to prevent the harms in question. Even if algorithmic transparency had been mandated from the outset, users would still have lacked the technical and critical literacy required to exercise meaningful oversight over systems whose complexity was, by design, irreducible.

What this analysis suggests, I would contend, is that the precautionary logic applicable to AI development is not one of prediction but of structured imagination: the capacity to construct detailed, technically grounded counterfactual scenarios and to ask, systematically, what governance mechanisms would have been necessary to produce different outcomes. Imagine a world in which such imaginative rigour had been applied to social media in 2008 — it is, tellingly, far from obvious that the institutional preconditions for such application existed. The question of whether they exist, or can be created, for AI is the defining governance challenge of the present moment.

📖 Reading 2 · Governing the Ungovernable

Контрфактическое рассуждение в нормативном аргументе
📖 Governing the Ungovernable: Counterfactual Scenarios and the Ethics of AI Precaution

The governance of artificial intelligence confronts a structural paradox: the pace of technological development renders anticipatory regulation technically difficult, yet the irreversibility of many potential AI-driven social transformations makes retroactive regulation politically and practically insufficient. Had the institutional responses to earlier transformative technologies — most notably the commercial internet — been designed with a more rigorous counterfactual imagination, the argument runs, the accumulation of unchecked market power and the systematic exploitation of personal data that now characterise the digital economy might have been substantially curtailed. This argument is not, admittedly, without its critics.

Even if such foresight had been available, one might contend, the political conditions necessary to translate anticipatory analysis into binding regulation were, in all likelihood, absent. The regulatory capture that has characterised the relationship between technology firms and governmental bodies would, but for extraordinary political will of the kind rarely available in stable democratic conditions, have obstructed even well-designed precautionary frameworks. As it were, the governance challenge was not primarily epistemic but political: not a failure to imagine consequences but a failure to construct and sustain the coalitions necessary to act upon them.

Were this diagnosis correct — and it is, I would contend, broadly more convincing than purely technocratic accounts — then the appropriate response to the current AI governance challenge is not simply more sophisticated regulation but a more fundamental rethinking of the political conditions under which technology governance is even possible. Imagine a world in which digital infrastructure were treated as a public utility subject to democratic oversight rather than as private property subject only to market discipline — what different institutional landscape might have been inherited, and what different choices might remain available to us now?

📝 Задания к Reading 2 — Нормативный аргумент

1. Второй абзац содержит 'would, but for extraordinary political will... have obstructed'. Разберите эту конструкцию: это 'but for' + Type 3? Или смешанный conditional? Как 'but for' функционирует грамматически и риторически в этом контексте?

2. 'As it were, the governance challenge was not primarily epistemic but political' — здесь 'as it were' используется не как 'as if', а как метафорическая оговорка. Объясните: что именно она смягчает? Что было бы без неё?

3. В третьем абзаце: 'Were this diagnosis correct — and it is, I would contend, broadly more convincing...' — инвертированный Type 2 с встроенным epistemic parenthetical. Автор делает нечто необычное: подтверждает гипотезу прямо внутри условной клаузы. Какой риторический эффект это создаёт?

4. 'Imagine a world in which digital infrastructure were treated as a public utility...' — здесь два вопроса в конце: 'what different institutional landscape might have been inherited' (прошедшее) и 'what different choices might remain available' (настоящее). Это смешанный counterfactual. Объясните временную логику.

✍️ Writing Task 1 · Bar Chart Description

Гипотетическое и контрфактическое мышление в описании данных
✍️ Writing Task 1 · Bar Chart (170 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

The bar chart below shows public trust in AI-powered decision-making systems across five sectors (healthcare, criminal justice, financial services, education, and employment) in 2023, measured as the percentage of survey respondents who expressed 'high' or 'very high' confidence in AI-generated decisions. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

Strikingly, public trust in AI decision-making varies considerably across sectors in ways that, it would appear, reflect the perceived stakes and reversibility of the decisions involved rather than any consistent assessment of the underlying technology's reliability. Were the data to be interpreted straightforwardly, one might conclude that healthcare AI commands the greatest public confidence — a finding that is, notably, not easily reconciled with the well-documented opacity of clinical AI systems.

① Strikingly (sentence adverb, C13) + complex NP subject: аналитический комментарий к данным

② it would appear — parenthetical hedging: интерпретация, не утверждение

③ Were the data to be interpreted straightforwardly — inverted Type 2: гипотетическая интерпретация

④ a finding that is, notably, not easily reconciled — appositive NP + sentence adverb в середине predicate

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Описана вариация + предложена интерпретация через гипотетическую рамку

CC (7–8): Sentence adverb → comment clause → hypothetical → appositive: четырёхступенчатая прогрессия

LR (7–8): reversibility, reconciled, opacity, perceived stakes — точная академическая лексика

GRA (7–8): Inverted conditional в дескриптивном тексте — редко и убедительно

📐 Структура эссе:

Overview (2 пр.): Strikingly [паттерн]. Were the data to suggest a uniform trend, it would — but it does not.

Body 1 (3–4 пр.): Наибольшее доверие: Had public awareness of AI opacity been higher, trust levels might have differed.

Body 2 (3–4 пр.): Наименьшее доверие: As if the risks in criminal justice were merely technical rather than ethical.

✍️ Writing Task 2 · Advantages / Disadvantages Essay

Гипотетическое мышление в аргументации: преимущества и риски ИИ
✍️ Writing Task 2 · Advantages & Disadvantages Essay (270 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

Artificial intelligence is increasingly being used to make important decisions in areas such as healthcare, criminal justice, and employment. What are the advantages and disadvantages of relying on AI for such decisions, and what is your overall assessment?

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

Were the potential of AI-assisted decision-making to be evaluated solely on the basis of its demonstrable capacity to reduce human cognitive error and scale consistent judgement across large populations, the case for its adoption in high-stakes domains would, arguably, be compelling. The evidence, it would appear, broadly supports the contention that well-designed algorithmic systems can, under controlled conditions, outperform individual human decision-makers on narrowly defined tasks.

① Were the potential... to be evaluated solely on the basis of — inverted Type 2 тезиса: ограничивает условие, при котором аргумент работает

② the case for its adoption... would, arguably, be compelling — epistemic hedging в consequence clause

③ it would appear — parenthetical comment clause (C13): дистанцирование от утверждения о доказательствах

④ under controlled conditions — limiting adverbial: ограничение области применимости (хеджинг C13 уровня 4)

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Преимущества и недостатки развёрнуты; позиция выражена через Were... + I would contend

CC (7–8): Inverted conditional в Intro → Had it been → as if → Imagine в Body 1–3: единая гипотетическая архитектура

LR (7–8): cognitive error, high-stakes, algorithmic, procedural justice, opacity — C2 лексика

GRA (7–8): Inverted Type 2 + comment clause + limiting adverbial в двух предложениях

📐 Структура эссе:

Intro (2 пр.): Were... to be evaluated → тезис с 'arguably'. Ограничение сферы применимости.

Advantages (3–4 пр.): Had human decision-making not been subject to bias... / Imagine a world where diagnostic AI were available to all...

Disadvantages (3–4 пр.): Even if algorithmic transparency were mandated... / Systems operate as if procedural justice were irrelevant...

Own Assessment + Conclusion (3 пр.): Were such systems to be governed by binding ethical frameworks... / I would contend... / What this analysis suggests is...

🎓 Урок C14 завершён!

✅ Unreal present/future conditionals: Were + to-inf / Should + inversion — формальные инвертированные формы для гипотетического анализа

✅ Counterfactual past: Had... / But for / Even if... would still / Only if — четыре логических функции (причинность, достаточность, альтернативы, единственное условие)

✅ As if / As though / As it were / Imagine a world where — риторическое и аналитическое воображение

✅ Контрфактическое мышление как метод: не просто грамматика, а аналитический инструмент для установления причинности и оценки альтернатив

✅ Band 8 Counterfactual Paragraph: тезис → Had... → as if... → Were... → What this suggests

💡 Central Question — Ответ:

«Невозможные» предложения делают аргумент реальнее по единственной причине: они конкретизируют абстрактный риск или принцип в воображаемый, но детально прописанный сценарий. Читатель не просто понимает — он представляет. Контрфактическое мышление — это не уклонение от реальности. Это самый точный способ установить, что в реальности было необходимым, что — достаточным, и что — возможным, но упущенным.
