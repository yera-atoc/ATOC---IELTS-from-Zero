Nominalisation · glagol → sushchestvitelnoye · plotnost smysla

Pochemu akademicheskiye avtory «zamorazhivayut» deystviya v sushchestvitelnyye?

Tematika: Kognitivnaya nauka · Slovar: C1–C2 · Writing: Opinion Essay 260 sl.

ZACHEM ETO NUZHNO? - Moment ozareniya

Prochtay dva predlozheniya. Oba verni. Oba ob odnom. No tolko odno zvuchit kak nauchnaya statya.

Band 6: Scientists discovered that stressed people make worse decisions.

Band 8: The discovery of a consistent correlation between stress-induced cognitive impairment and deterioration in decision-making quality has profound implications for behavioural economics.

Odna ideya. Raznaya plotnost. Razny mir.

Raznitsa - eto nominalizatsiya. Glagoly i prilagatelnyye prevratilis v sushchestvitelnyye - i kazhdoye iz nikh nesyot v sebe tseluyu mysl.

📐 GRAMMAR 1 · Nominalizatsiya: mekhanizm i akademicheskaya funktsiya

Kak deystviye stanovitsya obyektom - i pochemu eto menyayet vsyo?

🔗 Svyaz s urovnyami A i B

V urovne A ty ispolzoval glagoly: 'Governments must reduce pollution.'

V urovne B ty dobavil akademicheskiye svyazki i slozhnyye vremena.

V urovne C: chto proiskhodit, kogda 'reduce' stanovitsya 'reduction', a 'pollute' - 'pollution'? Pochemu eto silneye?

Nominalizatsiya - eto prevrashcheniye glagola ili prilagatel'nogo v sushchestvitel'noye. Eto ne ukrasheniye stilya: eto smena grammaticheskoy perspektivy. Kogda deystviye zamorazhivayetsya v sushchestvitel'noye, ono prevrashchayetsya v obyekt, kotory mozhno izuchat, izmeryat, kritikovat. Imenno tak rabotayet akademicheskiy diskurs.

Iskhodnaya forma Tip Nominalizatsiya Suffiks Primer v predlozhenii Band 8
decide glagol decision -ion The decision to restructure public institutions reflects...
develop glagol development -ment Sustainable development requires balancing economic growth...
discover glagol discovery -y The discovery challenged foundational assumptions of the field.
analyse glagol analysis -sis A comprehensive analysis of the data reveals...
fail glagol failure -ure The failure of regulatory mechanisms to adapt...
complex prilagatel'noye complexity -ity The complexity of global supply chains renders...
stable prilagatel'noye stability -ity Political stability is undermined by institutional corruption.
effective prilagatel'noye effectiveness -ness The effectiveness of this approach remains empirically contested.
legitimate prilagatel'noye legitimacy -cy Questions of democratic legitimacy pervade contemporary debate.
ambiguous prilagatel'noye ambiguity -ity Ethical ambiguity characterises decisions in frontier medicine.

❌ TIPICHNYYE OSHIBKI pri nominalizatsii

✗ 'The analyse of data shows...' → Nepravilnaya forma: nuzhno 'analysis' (ne *analyse kak sushchestvitel'noye)

✗ 'The develop of technology...' → Nuzhno 'development': glagol develop ne ispolzuyetsya kak sushchestvitel'noye napryamuyu

✗ Slishkom mnogo: 'The systematisation of the regularisation of the normalisation...' → izbytochnaya nominalizatsiya delayet tekst nechitayemym

Pochemu eto proiskhodit: student pytayetsya 'zvuchat akademichno', no ne ponimayet, kogda nominalizatsiya rabotayet.

⭐ Model Sentence - Band 8

"The rapid proliferation ① of misinformation across digital platforms

has necessitated ② a fundamental reconsideration ③ of regulatory frameworks

governing online discourse ④."

① 'proliferation' - nominalizatsiya ot 'proliferate'; modifitsirована prilagatel'nym 'rapid'

② 'has necessitated' - glagol, upravlyayushchiy ob'yektom-nominalizatsiyey (neobkhodim, chtoby predlozheniye 'dvigatosya')

③ 'reconsideration' - nominalizatsiya ot 'reconsider'; postmodifitsirована predlozhnym oborotom 'of regulatory frameworks'

④ 'governing online discourse' - prichastny oborot postmodifitsirует 'frameworks' → informatsionnaya plotnost bez lishnikh slov

👁 Examiner's Eye: nominalizatsiya i Band 8

✅ Nominalizatsiya + postmodifikatsiya → slozhnaya NP → nemed-lenno povyshayet GRA i LR

✅ Pravilnaya kollokatсiya: 'rapid proliferation', ne '*fast proliferation' → Band 8 LR

✅ Nominalizatsiya v pozitsii podlezhashchego delayet predlozheniye bolee akademichnym

⚠️ Opasnost: slishkom mnogo nominalizatsiy → tekst teryayet yasnost. Ekzamenator otsen. balans.

📐 GRAMMAR 2 · Postmodifikatsiya: kak upakovat informatsiyu v NP

Chto idyot posle sushchestvitel'nogo - i kak eto sozdayet akademicheskuyu plotnost?

🔗 Svyaz s urovnyami A i B

V urovne A: prostyye NP - 'the problem', 'the government'.

V urovne B: predmodifikatsiya - 'the severe economic problem', 'the central government'.

V urovne C: postmodifikatsiya - informatsiya POSLE yadra NP. Eto to, chto otlichayet Band 7 ot Band 8.

Postmodifikatsiya - dobavleniye informatsii posle yadernogo sushchestvitel'nogo. V akademicheskom tekste eto glavny mekhanizm sozdaniya informatsionnoy plotnosti. Tri osnovnykh tipa: predlozhny oborot (of-phrase), prichastny oborot (participial phrase), pridatochnoye (relative clause).

Tip postmodifikatsii Struktura Primer Funktsiya
Predlozhny oborot (of-NP) N + of + NP the erosion of democratic norms Svyazyvayet abstraktsiyu s konkretnym ob'yektom
Tsepochka of-fraz N + of + N + of + N the collapse of the illusion of consensus Mnogourovnevaya iyerarkhiya ponyatiy
Prichastny oborot (Ving) N + Ving + ... frameworks governing digital infrastructure Aktivny protsess kak atribut
Prichastny oborot (Ved) N + Ved + ... assumptions challenged by empirical evidence Passivnoye sostoyaniye kak atribut
Pridatochnoye (which/that) N + which/that + clause a legitimacy deficit that no electoral process can resolve Rasshirennoye opredeleniye cherez suzhdeniye
Infinitivny oborot N + to + infinitive the capacity to reshape institutional behaviour Potentsial ili naznacheniye

❌ TIPICHNYYE OSHIBKI v postmodifikatsii

✗ 'The problem of the society' → Artikl' lishniy: 'the problem of society' (abstraktnyye sushchestvitel'nyye bez artikl'а)

✗ 'A framework governing of the data' → Prichastny oborot ne trebuyet predloga: 'a framework governing data'

✗ Slishkom dlinnaya tsepochka: 'the failure of the implementation of the regulation of the use of...' → nechitayemo; maksimum 2-3 urovnya

⭐ Model Sentence - Band 8 (slozhnaya NP)

"The persistent failure ① of institutions charged with overseeing ② the deployment

of autonomous decision-making systems ③ to adapt ④ to the pace of technological change

represents a structural vulnerability ⑤ with far-reaching consequences."

① Yadro NP: 'failure' + predmodifikator 'persistent'

② Prichastny oborot: 'charged with overseeing' - postmodifitsirует 'institutions'

③ Of-tsepochka: 'the deployment of autonomous decision-making systems' - postmodifitsirует 'overseeing'

④ Infinitiv 'to adapt' - postmodifitsirует 'failure' (failure to do sth)

⑤ 'Structural vulnerability with far-reaching consequences' - eshcho odna slozhnaya NP kak predikatіv

👁 Examiner's Eye: slozhnyye NP i postmodifikatsiya

✅ Odna khorosho postroyennaya slozhnaya NP demonstriruyet bolshe GRA, chem tri prostykh predlozheniya

✅ Of-postmodifikatsiya + prichastny oborot v odnoy NP → Band 8 sintaksis

✅ Izbegay 'there is/are + noun' - zameni slozhnoy NP v pozitsii podlezhashchego

⚠️ Oshibka prodvinutogo studenta: dlinnaya NP s oshibkoy v soglasovanii (singular/plural). Prover: gde yadro NP?

📐 GRAMMAR 3 · Nominalizatsiya kak argumentativny instrument

Kak vybor mezhdu glagolom i sushchestvitel'nym menyayet pozitsiyu avtora?

🔗 Svyaz s urovnyami A i B

V urovne B ty uchilsya ispolzovat hedging: 'It is argued that...', 'tends to...', 'may suggest...'

V urovne C: nominalizatsiya - eto vstroenny khedzhzhing. Kogda ty pishesh 'the erosion of trust' vmesto 'trust is eroding', ty konstatiruyesh - bez otsenki. Eto akademicheskaya neytralnost cherez sintaksis.

Glagol'naya forma aktsentirует deystviye i deyatelya. Nominalizatsiya aktsentirует yavleniye i yego posledstviya. V akademicheskom pis'me eto razlichiye - ne stilisticheskoye, a argumentativnoye. Rassmorim tri funktsii nominalizatsii.

Funktsiya Glagolnaya versiya Nominalizovannaya versiya Band 8 Chto izmenilos?
Neytralizatsiya deyatelya Governments failed to protect... The failure of governance structures to protect... Deyatel' ischezayet → aktsent na sistemnom yavlenii
Sozdaniye temy predlozheniya People are increasingly afraid of AI. Growing public apprehension regarding AI has prompted... Strakh stanovitsya ob'yektom izucheniya, a ne emotsiyey
Svyazka mezhdu abzatsami We said trust is eroding. This is important. This erosion of institutional trust has significant implications... 'This erosion' anaforicheski svyazyvayet s predydushchim abzatsem
Akademicheskaya kauzalnost Because AI spread quickly, problems appeared. The rapid proliferation of AI gave rise to a range of unforeseen challenges. Prichina i sledstviye - oba nominalizovany → formal'nost

Osobenno vazhna tretya funktsiya - anaforicheskaya svyazka. Eto odin iz samykh moshchnykh mekhanizmov kogezii (CC) v Task 2: vzyat klyuchevy glagol iz predydushchego predlozheniya, nominalizovat yego i postavit v pozitsiyu 'this + [nominalizatsiya]' v nachale sleduyushchego.

❌ TIPICHNYYE OSHIBKI - nominalizatsiya i argument

✗ Ispol'zovaniye nominalizatsii bez glagola-skazuyemogo: 'The increasing polarisation and the collapse of consensus and the erosion of trust...' - predlozheniye bez glagola!

✗ Smesheniye registrov: 'The getting angry of people...' - nelzya nominalizovat neformolny glagol napryamuyu; nuzhno 'public anger' ili 'intensification of social resentment'

✗ Nepravilnaya kollokatssiya: '*a big development' vmesto 'a significant development' - nominalizatsii trebuyut akademicheskikh prilagatel'nykh

⭐ Model Sentence - Band 8 (anaforicheskaya svyazka)

[Kontekst predydushchego predlozheniya:] '...trust in public institutions has progressively declined.'

"This erosion of institutional trust ①, compounded by ② the proliferation of unverified information

across digital platforms ③, has precipitated ④ a crisis of democratic legitimacy ⑤

whose full implications remain incompletely understood."

① 'This erosion' - anaforicheskaya nominalizatsiya: 'declined' → 'erosion'; 'this' ukazyvayet na predydushcheye predlozheniye → CC Band 8

② 'compounded by' - prichastny oborot vvodit dopolnitel'ny faktor bez novogo predlozheniya

③ Postmodifitsirovanная NP: 'proliferation of unverified information across digital platforms'

④ 'has precipitated' - akademichesky glagol vmesto 'caused' → LR Band 8

⑤ 'a crisis of democratic legitimacy' - eshcho odna nominalizovannaya NP kak ob'yekt

👁 Examiner's Eye: anafora i kogeziya

✅ 'This + [nominalizatsiya]' v nachale predlozheniya/abzatsa → CC Band 7-8 avtomaticheski

✅ Predlozheniye vyshe demonstriruyet 4 raznykh akademicheskikh struktury - ekzamenator eto zamechayet i otmechayet v GRA

✅ 'has precipitated' vmesto 'caused' - odin glagol podnimayet LR na polballa

⚠️ Peregruz: yesli v odnom predlozhenii 5+ nominalizatsiy - tekst stanovitsya trudnym dlya chteniya

The Cognitive Architecture of Belief Formation

The acquisition of beliefs represents one of the most contested domains within cognitive science, not least because the mechanisms underlying this process resist straightforward empirical investigation. Unlike the formation of preferences, which is amenable to behavioural measurement, the crystallisation of deeply held convictions involves processes that are, to a significant degree, inaccessible to conscious introspection. The proliferation of dual-process theories - most prominently associated with the distinction between fast, automatic cognition and slow, deliberative reasoning - has offered a partial resolution to this problem. Yet the simplification inherent in such frameworks carries its own costs: the reduction of complex epistemic phenomena to a binary opposition obscures the gradations, interactions, and contextual dependencies that characterise authentic cognitive experience. Of particular significance is the observation that the susceptibility of individuals to ideological manipulation correlates not with intellectual deficiency but with the very sophistication of their reasoning capacities. This counterintuitive finding - sometimes termed the smart idiots effect - suggests that the deployment of advanced reasoning skills in the service of motivated cognition may amplify, rather than correct for, pre-existing biases. The implications of this recognition for educational policy are, at minimum, unsettling.

📚 READING 2 · Advanced Academic Text

Nominalizatsiya i postmodifikatsiya v deystvii: kognitivnaya nauka i prinyatiye resheniy.

Rationality, Bias, and the Limits of Cognitive Optimism

The entrenchment of cognitive bias as a subject of serious scientific inquiry has done little, paradoxically, to diminish its influence. Despite the proliferation of behavioural interventions - nudges, debiasing programmes, and institutional redesign - the eradication of systematic error from human judgement has proven resistant to straightforward correction. This persistence calls into question not merely the effectiveness of individual remediation strategies but the foundational assumption that rational cognition represents a reachable, if imperfectly approximated, ideal. The contribution of dual-process theory to this debate has been substantial, if contested. The characterisation of cognitive processes as operating along a fast-intuitive versus slow-deliberative axis provides an explanatory framework of considerable rhetorical clarity, yet the oversimplification inherent in such binary models risks obscuring the fluid, context-dependent nature of actual decision-making. Not only does the model fail to accommodate the full gradation of cognitive engagement, but its widespread adoption in policy circles has resulted in the misapplication of behavioural insights to contexts where their relevance is, at best, empirically unestablished. The implications of this recognition extend beyond academic debate. The institutionalisation of nudge-based policy instruments across diverse governance contexts reflects an aspiration towards evidence-based governance that is, in principle, laudable. Whether such aspiration can survive contact with the heterogeneity of human motivation and the irreducibility of value pluralism to any single optimisation framework remains, however, an open and perhaps unanswerable question.
