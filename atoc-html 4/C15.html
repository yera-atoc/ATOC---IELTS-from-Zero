Register Mastery

Почему один и тот же смысл в разных регистрах — это разные сообщения?

Тематика: Климат и энергетическая политика · Словарь: C1–C2 Academic · Writing: Report + Discursive Essay

💥 Урочный крючок — Зачем это нужно?

Четыре способа сказать одно и то же о климатической политике:

Informal: The government totally messed up on climate — they basically ignored the science for years.

Neutral: The government failed to act on climate change despite clear scientific evidence.

Formal: Successive administrations demonstrably failed to translate the scientific consensus on anthropogenic climate change into commensurate policy action.

Hyper-formal (legal/bureaucratic): It is submitted that the measures adopted by the relevant governmental authorities have, notwithstanding the available evidence base, fallen materially short of the standard required by international obligations.

Смысл один. Но каждый регистр создаёт разные отношения между автором, читателем и предметом. Регистр — это не выбор слов. Это выбор позиции.

📘 Grammar 1 · The Register Continuum: от разговорного к формальному

Lexical, syntactic, and pragmatic dimensions of register — три оси контроля
🟣

Студенты уровня B знают: 'не используй сленг в эссе'.

Студенты уровня C понимают, что регистр — это трёхмерная система: лексика (какие слова), синтаксис (какие структуры), прагматика (какая позиция автора к читателю).

Три оси регистра

Ось 1 — Лексика: выбор между словами разного регистра при одном значении.

Informal → Neutral → Formal: go up → increase → escalate → undergo a sustained upward trajectory

Ось 2 — Синтаксис: выбор структур (активный/пассивный, личный/безличный, простой/сложный).

Informal → Formal: 'We should do something.' → 'It is submitted that concerted action is required.'

Ось 3 — Прагматика: дистанция между автором и читателем; явность/скрытость авторской позиции.

Informal → Formal: 'I think...' → 'It would appear that...' → 'It is widely contended that...'

INFORMAL / SPOKEN

Contractions: it's, don't, won't

Phrasal verbs: cut down, speed up, look into

Vague language: kind of, sort of, things like that

First person direct: I think, we should, in my view

Short sentences, coordination: and, but, so

Emotive language: terrible, amazing, shocking

NEUTRAL / STANDARD

Full forms: it is, do not, will not

One-word verbs: reduce, accelerate, examine

Precise language: significant, considerable, substantial

Hedged first person: it seems, research suggests

Compound sentences with however, therefore

Evaluative but measured: problematic, concerning

FORMAL / ACADEMIC

Nominalisations: reduction, acceleration, examination

Latinate vocabulary: mitigate, attenuate, ameliorate

Impersonal constructions: it is widely acknowledged

Sentence adverbs: crucially, notwithstanding, arguably

Complex NPs with pre/post-modification (C12)

Hedged evaluative: arguably counterproductive, it would appear

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The demonstrably inadequate policy response to anthropogenic climate change — a response that successive administrations have, it is widely acknowledged, calibrated to the demands of electoral cycles rather than to the imperatives of physical science — constitutes, notwithstanding the formal commitments undertaken under the Paris Agreement, one of the most consequential governance failures of the contemporary era.

① demonstrably inadequate policy response — formal register: evaluative adverb + Latinate adj + nominalised NP (C12)

② it is widely acknowledged — impersonal passive comment clause (C13): maximum pragmatic distance

③ calibrated to the demands of electoral cycles rather than to the imperatives of physical science — Latinate verb + parallel NP structure (C9 balance)

④ notwithstanding the formal commitments undertaken under — formal concession marker (C11) + reduced participial post-modifier
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Climate change is really bad and we need to do something about it ASAP.

Правильно: The accelerating pace of anthropogenic climate change constitutes an urgent and multidimensional governance challenge that demands substantive and immediate policy reform.

Почему: 'Really bad', 'do something', 'ASAP' — informal markers inappropriate in academic writing. Замени эмотивную лексику на точную номинализованную оценку

Ошибка: In my opinion I personally think that renewable energy is the best solution.

Правильно: It is widely contended that the accelerated transition to renewable energy sources represents the most technically viable pathway to decarbonisation.

Почему: Двойное личное маркирование ('In my opinion I personally') — избыточно в любом регистре. В академическом — замени на impersonal или hedged formal structure
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Register Continuum

✔ Последовательно формальный регистр на протяжении всего эссе → LR Band 7–8: нет смешения уровней

✔ Nominalisations + Latinate vocabulary + impersonal passive → три одновременных маркера формального регистра

✔ Отсутствие contractions, phrasal verbs, vague language → базовая, но необходимая основа Band 6+
⚠ Снижает балл: Смешение регистров (formal sentence → informal phrase → formal again) — хуже, чем последовательный neutral; экзаменатор воспринимает это как потерю контроля над языком

📙 Grammar 2 · Lexical Register Upgrade: замена слов по регистровой лестнице

Verbs · Adjectives · Connectors · Reporting verbs — систематическая Latinate замена
🟣

Знать академические слова — недостаточно.

Нужно знать почему одно слово академичнее другого при одном значении — и уметь выбирать точно, а не случайно.
Категория Informal/Neutral Formal upgrade Ещё более formal Разница в оттенке

Глагол показа show demonstrate / reveal indicate / attest to / bear witness to 'Attest to' — формально-юридический; 'bear witness' — риторически сильный

Глагол решения fix / deal with address / resolve redress / ameliorate / mitigate 'Redress' — исправление неравенства; 'ameliorate' — облегчение; 'mitigate' — снижение риска

⚡ Reporting verbs: регистровая иерархия

Level 1 (neutral): says, thinks, believes, shows

Level 2 (academic): argues, contends, suggests, demonstrates, reveals, indicates

Level 3 (high formal): posits, submits, avers, asserts, advances the claim that, takes the position that

Тонкость: reporting verb тоже выражает отношение автора к источнику. 'Suggests' — осторожно. 'Demonstrates' — принимает как доказанное. 'Claims' — дистанцируется или сомневается. 'Asserts' — подчёркивает, что это мнение, не факт.
Задача Weak (Band 5–6) Strong (Band 7–8) Регистровый механизм
Описать тренд Emissions have gone up a lot in recent years. Global greenhouse gas emissions have undergone a sustained and pronounced upward trajectory over the past three decades, notwithstanding periodic fluctuations attributable to economic contractions. undergo + NP + notwithstanding + reduced participial
Описать провал политики The government didn't do what it needed to do. Successive administrations have demonstrably failed to translate the scientific consensus on climate risk into commensurate regulatory action. demonstrably (sentence adverb) + Latinate verb + nominalised NP
Выразить необходимость We really need to act now. The imperative for immediate, substantive, and internationally coordinated policy reform is, I would contend, now incontrovertible. nominalised 'imperative' + stacked pre-modifiers + epistemic parenthetical
Процитировать позицию Some scientists say this is dangerous. A growing body of empirical literature contends that the current trajectory of greenhouse gas accumulation poses existential risks to the biophysical systems upon which human civilisation depends. reporting verb 'contends' + complex NP subject + relative clause with formal 'upon which'
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: Renewable energy is getting more and more popular.

Правильно: Renewable energy technologies have achieved substantial and accelerating market penetration, driven by declining production costs and increasingly supportive regulatory environments.

Почему: 'Getting more and more popular' — разговорное прогрессивное. В формальном тексте: nominalised NP + passive с причинной клаузой

Ошибка: Claims has been made that climate change is fake.

Правильно: It has been asserted by a small number of commentators — contrary to the overwhelming scientific consensus — that anthropogenic climate change is overstated.

Почему: 'Claims has been made' — ошибочное согласование. Правильно: 'Claims have been made' или лучше: 'It has been asserted that' (impersonal + formal register)
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Lexical Register

✔ Latinate verb ('ameliorate', 'mitigate', 'attenuate') вместо phrasal → немедленный LR Band 7+ сигнал

✔ Reporting verb с точным эпистемическим значением ('contends' vs 'claims' vs 'demonstrates') → TA + LR

✔ Nominalisation вместо phrasal verb ('undergo a reduction' vs 'go down') → формальность + плотность (GRA + LR)
⚠ Снижает балл: Latinate слово ради латинского — пустой формализм; экзаменатор ценит точность, а не длину слова

📗 Grammar 3 · Syntactic Register: формальные синтаксические структуры

Preposition fronting · Formal negation · Cleft emphasis · Impersonal it · Non-finite clauses
🟣

Регистр — не только словарь.

В уровне C мы изучаем синтаксические структуры, которые сами по себе сигнализируют о формальном регистре — независимо от выбранных слов.
Структура Informal/Neutral Formal equivalent Почему формальнее
Preposition fronting (relative clause) the policy that we rely on the policy upon which we rely / the framework within which decisions are made Предлог перед relative pronoun = письменный академический стандарт
Formal negation not... any no / none of 'No significant evidence' звучит формальнее, чем 'not any significant evidence'
Fronted negative (C9) We have never seen this before. Never before have such challenges confronted policymakers. Инверсия после фронтированного отрицания = высокий формальный регистр
Formal quantifiers a lot of / lots of a considerable number of / a substantial proportion of / the overwhelming majority of Точные количественные NP вместо разговорных
Cleft for emphasis The main problem is money. It is the inadequacy of financial commitment, rather than technical capacity, that constitutes the primary obstacle. It-cleft с 'rather than' = формальная эмфаза + разграничение (C9)
Non-finite clause instead of 'because' Because emissions rose, temperatures increased. Emissions having risen sharply, a corresponding increase in global mean temperatures became statistically inevitable. Nominative absolute: высший формальный уровень
Impersonal it + formal verb People know that... / It's clear that... It is widely acknowledged / It has been demonstrated / It bears noting that Impersonal it = максимальная прагматическая дистанция (C13)

📐 Nominative Absolute: самая формальная non-finite clause

Обычная причинная клауза: 'Because emissions have risen sharply, temperatures have increased.'

Nominative absolute: 'Emissions having risen sharply, a corresponding increase in global temperatures has become statistically inevitable.'

Структура: NP + having + past participle / being + past participle , main clause.

Другие примеры:

'All regulatory options having been exhausted, the case for binding international treaty obligations becomes overwhelming.'

'The scientific consensus being now beyond reasonable dispute, the remaining question is one of political will rather than epistemic uncertainty.'

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

Never before in the recorded history of international climate diplomacy have the technical means for a managed energy transition been more readily available, nor the political will to deploy them more conspicuously absent — a paradox that is, upon which one might reflect, attributable less to ignorance than to the structural incentives governing the behaviour of states and corporations in a competitive international order.

① Never before... have — fronted negative inversion (C9): highest formal register; rhetorical force

② nor the political will... more conspicuously absent — parallel negative structure with ellipsis: Band 8 CC

③ upon which one might reflect — fronted preposition relative + formal impersonal 'one': double formality signal

④ attributable less to ignorance than to the structural incentives — Latinate adjective + 'less... than' formal comparison
❌ Типичные ошибки

Ошибка: The policy that we depend on is not working.

Правильно: The policy framework upon which effective climate governance depends has demonstrably failed to produce the emissions reductions it was designed to achieve.

Почему: 'The policy that we depend on' — informal relative + personal pronoun. Формальный вариант: 'upon which... depends' + impersonal subject

Ошибка: There are a lot of reasons why countries don't cooperate.

Правильно: A considerable number of structural and political factors militate against the kind of sustained international cooperation that meaningful climate governance requires.

Почему: 'A lot of reasons why' — разговорное. Замени: 'a considerable number of factors' + Latinate verb 'militate against' + formal relative
🔵 Examiner's Eye — Syntactic Register

✔ 'Upon which / within which / through which' → мгновенный LR + GRA сигнал; редко используется студентами Band 6

✔ Fronted negative inversion ('Never before have...') → самая эффектная формальная структура для кульминационного аргумента

✔ Nominative absolute ('The evidence having been reviewed...') → Band 8-exclusive structure; немедленно замечается экзаменатором
⚠ Снижает балл: Nominative absolute без правильного синтаксиса ('Emissions having rose...') — грубая ошибка; используй только если уверен в форме

📕 Grammar 4 · Register Consistency & Band 8 Upgrade

Полный регистровый контроль: как избежать сдвигов и выдержать формальность от первого слова до последнего
🟣

Самая частая регистровая проблема Band 6–7 — не незнание формальных структур.

Это непоследовательность: два формальных предложения — одно разговорное — снова формальное. Экзаменатор замечает каждый сдвиг. В Grammar 4 мы строим систему регистрового контроля.
Источник регистрового сдвига Типичный пример Исправление Правило
Phrasal verb в формальном контексте The policy was set up to reduce emissions. The policy was designed to achieve measurable reductions in greenhouse gas emissions. Заменяй все phrasal verbs на Latinate одночленные глаголы
Personal pronoun в академическом эссе I believe that renewable energy is the best solution. It is submitted that renewable energy technologies represent the most technically viable pathway to decarbonisation. Замени 'I believe/think/feel' на impersonal или hedged formal
Contraction It's widely known that emissions are rising. It is widely acknowledged that greenhouse gas concentrations are undergoing a sustained upward trajectory. Никаких contractions в IELTS Writing — без исключений
Vague quantifier A lot of countries are trying to reduce emissions. An increasing number of national governments have undertaken formal commitments to emissions reduction targets. 'A lot of' → 'a considerable number of / a substantial proportion of / the overwhelming majority of'
Emotive intensifier Climate change is a really serious problem. Anthropogenic climate change constitutes one of the most consequential governance challenges of the contemporary era. 'Really / very / extremely' → degree adverb + formal evaluation: 'demonstrably / arguably / profoundly'
Informal connector Also, renewable energy creates jobs. Moreover, the expansion of the renewable energy sector has demonstrably generated significant employment in regions previously dependent upon fossil fuel extraction. 'Also/And/But' at sentence start → formal connector + full formal clause

📐 Register Audit Checklist — проверяй перед каждой сдачей

□ Нет contractions (it's, don't, can't)?

□ Нет phrasal verbs там, где есть Latinate замена?

□ Нет 'I think / I believe / in my opinion'?

□ Нет 'a lot of / lots of / loads of'?

□ Нет 'really / very / extremely' перед прилагательным (замени на 'profoundly / demonstrably / arguably')?

□ Нет 'And / But / Also' в начале предложения (замени на формальный коннектор)?

□ Все relative clauses с 'that' проверены: не лучше ли 'upon which / within which / through which'?

□ Все причинные клаузы 'because' проверены: не лучше ли nominative absolute или given that / in light of?

📖 Reading 1 · The Political Economy of Climate Inaction

Академическое чтение: безупречный формальный регистр в реальном тексте
📖 The Political Economy of Climate Inaction: Structural Incentives, Temporal Horizons, and the Limits of International Cooperation

It is widely acknowledged, within the scholarly literature on environmental governance, that the failure of successive international climate agreements to produce emissions reductions commensurate with the scale of the physical challenge is attributable not primarily to a deficit of technical knowledge or financial capacity but to the structural misalignment between the temporal horizons governing political decision-making and the geological timescales upon which the consequences of current emissions will be most acutely felt. Crucially, the costs of ambitious climate action are concentrated in the present electoral cycle, while the most significant benefits accrue to populations not yet born — a temporal asymmetry that no existing international institutional architecture has yet proved capable of resolving.

Notwithstanding the formal commitments undertaken by the majority of the world's major economies under successive international agreements, the gap between stated policy objectives and actual emissions trajectories has, it must be acknowledged, widened rather than narrowed over the three decades since the scientific consensus on anthropogenic climate change was established beyond reasonable dispute. More precisely, it is not the absence of international agreements but the demonstrably inadequate mechanisms for monitoring, enforcement, and accountability within those agreements that constitutes the binding constraint upon effective collective action.

What the foregoing analysis suggests, I would contend, is that the prevailing approach to international climate governance — premised as it is upon the voluntary commitments of sovereign states operating within a system of formally equal but substantively asymmetric bargaining power — is structurally incapable of producing outcomes proportionate to the scale of the challenge. Never before have the technical preconditions for a managed global energy transition been more thoroughly established; yet the political preconditions for translating that technical capacity into binding collective action remain, it would appear, as elusive as ever.

📖 Reading 2 · The Case for a Global Carbon Price

Формальный регистр в политическом аргументе
📖 The Case for a Global Carbon Price: Economic Efficiency, Political Feasibility, and Distributional Justice

Among the instruments available to the international community for addressing the challenge of climate change, a globally coordinated carbon pricing mechanism is, arguably, the most economically efficient. The case for such a mechanism rests upon the straightforward observation that greenhouse gas emissions constitute a negative externality whose costs are borne not by those who generate them but by the global commons — a situation that, in the absence of corrective pricing, will persist indefinitely and whose consequences, it must be acknowledged, will be felt most acutely by those who have contributed least to its genesis.

Notwithstanding the theoretical elegance of this argument, the political obstacles to the implementation of a binding global carbon price are, it would appear, formidable. The asymmetric distribution of abatement costs across national economies — with carbon-intensive emerging economies facing a disproportionate burden relative to their historical contribution to atmospheric concentrations — constitutes, in the judgement of many analysts, the most significant structural impediment to the construction of a durable international consensus. Never before have the competing imperatives of economic development, historical equity, and physical necessity been more sharply in tension.

What the evidence therefore suggests is that the question of global carbon pricing is, at its core, not a technical but a political one: not a matter of identifying the optimal price signal but of constructing the institutional architecture within which sovereign states can be induced — rather than compelled — to accept constraints upon their emissions that are perceived as equitable, enforceable, and compatible with continued economic development. It is submitted, I would contend, that achieving this requires not merely more sophisticated economic modelling but a fundamental rethinking of the governance frameworks through which international environmental cooperation is structured and legitimated.

📝 Задания к Reading 2 — Регистр и аргументация

1. 'A globally coordinated carbon pricing mechanism is, arguably, the most economically efficient.' Разберите все регистровые маркеры в этом предложении: лексика (Latinate?), синтаксис (структура NP?), прагматика (sentence adverb?).

2. 'Never before have the competing imperatives... been more sharply in tension.' Это fronted negative inversion. Почему автор выбрал именно это место для инверсии? Что должно было бы стоять здесь без инверсии — и как это меняет риторический эффект?

3. 'through which international environmental cooperation is structured and legitimated' — preposition fronting + formal passive. Перепишите в informal варианте. Подсчитайте количество регистровых изменений, которые вы сделали.

4. Текст содержит 'It is submitted, I would contend' — это два epistemic markers подряд. Один ('It is submitted') — impersonal, другой ('I would contend') — personal. Зачем автор использует оба? Это избыточность или намеренный риторический приём?

✍️ Writing Task 1 · Line Graph (170 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

The graph below shows the share of electricity generated from renewable sources in five countries (Germany, China, USA, India, and Brazil) between 2000 and 2022. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

The data reveal a pattern of broadly divergent trajectories in renewable electricity generation across the five countries surveyed, with the most pronounced upward trend recorded in Germany — a development attributable, in no small measure, to the sustained policy commitment embodied in successive iterations of the Energiewende framework — and the most substantial absolute increase in installed renewable capacity achieved by China, notwithstanding its considerably lower proportional share of total generation.

① broadly divergent trajectories — formal NP: nominalised 'trajectories' + Latinate 'divergent' + hedging 'broadly'

② the most pronounced upward trend — formal superlative NP: Latinate 'pronounced' instead of 'big/large'

③ a development attributable, in no small measure, to — appositive NP + formal expression ('in no small measure' = formal understatement)

④ notwithstanding its considerably lower proportional share — formal concession marker (C11) in mid-sentence

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Главный паттерн (divergence) + две выделенные страны с разными обоснованиями

CC (7–8): Appositive NP + notwithstanding: два уровня структуры в одном предложении

LR (7–8): trajectories, pronounced, attributable, embodied, proportional — все Latinate, нет phrasal verbs

GRA (7–8): Complex NP subject + appositive + concession в одном предложении

📐 Структура эссе:

Overview (2 пр.): Formal NP для главного паттерна. Notwithstanding для ключевого исключения.

Body 1 (3–4 пр.): Latinate verbs для трендов: 'undergo a sustained increase / record significant growth / achieve'. Never before для кульминации.

Body 2 (3–4 пр.): Preposition fronting для сравнений: 'relative to which / in comparison with which'. Nominative absolute для причин.

✍️ Writing Task 2 · Discursive Essay (270 слов, цель: Band 7.5)

Задание:

Some argue that individual lifestyle changes — such as reducing meat consumption, using public transport, and minimising air travel — are the most effective way to address climate change. Others believe that systemic changes, such as government regulation and corporate responsibility, are more important. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

📌 Model Opening — Band 8:

It is widely contended that the discourse surrounding individual versus systemic responsibility for climate change reflects, in microcosm, a more fundamental tension within environmental governance between the liberal emphasis upon individual agency and the structural argument that the causes of — and solutions to — anthropogenic climate change are irreducibly collective in nature.

① It is widely contended that — impersonal passive (C13) + formal reporting: maximum pragmatic distance

② in microcosm — formal Latin phrase as adverbial: signals high register immediately

③ the liberal emphasis upon individual agency — preposition fronting ('upon') + formal NP

④ irreducibly collective in nature — Latinate adverb + adjective: Band 8 evaluative phrase

👁 What the Examiner Sees:

TA (7–8): Оба взгляда представлены без caricature; собственная позиция выражена через formal hedged structures

CC (7–8): It is widely contended → Notwithstanding → Never before have → It is submitted: четыре разных формальных маркера

LR (7–8): Полный отказ от phrasal verbs, contractions, vague language — Register Audit пройден

GRA (7–8): Impersonal + preposition fronting + nominative absolute + fronted negative в разных частях эссе

📐 Структура эссе:

Intro (2 пр.): It is widely contended + in microcosm. Тезис с 'I would contend' в конце.

View 1 — Individual (3–4 пр.): Latinate verbs + preposition fronting. 'Notwithstanding its intuitive appeal...'

View 2 — Systemic (3–4 пр.): Nominative absolute + formal comparison. 'Never before have structural incentives been more clearly...'

Own Opinion + Conclusion (3 пр.): It is submitted that... / attributable not to X but to Y / upon which effective governance depends.

💡 Central Question — Ответ:

Один и тот же смысл в разных регистрах — это разные сообщения, потому что регистр кодирует не только содержание, но и отношение: между автором и читателем, между автором и предметом, между высказыванием и истиной. 'I think this is bad' — это мнение человека. 'It is widely acknowledged that this constitutes a consequential failure' — это позиция, встроенная в институциональный дискурс. Смысл похож. Регистр — разный. И именно это различие IELTS измеряет как Lexical Resource.
