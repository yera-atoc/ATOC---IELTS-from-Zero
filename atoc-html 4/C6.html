🎯 Зачем это нужно? — Момент озарения

Прочитай два предложения об одном и том же:

Band 6: "The court uses a system. In this system, evidence must be presented before a judge. The judge then decides whether it is admissible."

Band 8: "The court operates an evidentiary framework whereby all submissions are subject to judicial admissibility review — a process wherein the presiding judge retains discretionary authority to exclude material likely to prejudice a fair determination."

Три предложения стали одним. Смысл не потерян — он уплотнён. Это не просто Grammar. Это информационная архитектура: способность строить предложения, которые несут несколько логических слоёв одновременно. Именно это экзаменатор называет 'complex structures used accurately and flexibly'.

📐 Grammar 1 · Whereby — относительное наречие механизма

«Система, посредством которой...» — встроенное описание механизма

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне A: который/which/that — базовые относительные местоимения для описания предметов и людей.

В уровне B: расширение через whose, whom, где/when/why как относительные наречия.

В уровне C: whereby, wherein, whereupon — относительные наречия высокого формального регистра, которые заменяют предлог + which и создают информационную плотность, характерную для юридического, академического и технического дискурса.
Структура Что заменяет Пример Band 8 Семантический фокус
N + whereby + clause by which / through which / under which The adversarial system represents a procedural architecture whereby competing legal arguments are subjected to adversarial scrutiny before an independent adjudicator, with factual determinations ultimately resting on the balance of probative evidence. Механизм, процедура, способ функционирования
a process/system/mechanism whereby by means of which / through which Restorative justice programmes offer an alternative to custodial sentencing whereby offenders engage directly with affected parties in a structured mediation process designed to address harm, acknowledge responsibility, and negotiate reparative obligations. Описание процесса — особенно эффективно в академических определениях
a [NP] whereby [clause], [result] complex causal chain The doctrine of precedent establishes a hierarchical case-law structure whereby lower courts are bound by the ratio decidendi of superior court rulings — a constraint whereby judicial consistency is maintained at the cost of doctrinal flexibility. Двойной whereby: первый описывает механизм, второй — его следствие

⚡ Whereby: ключевые правила и ограничения

1. Whereby следует за существительным или noun phrase — оно не открывает предложение самостоятельно.

2. Whereby относится к механизму, процессу или системе — не к людям и не к абстрактным понятиям без процессуального значения.

3. Whereby + полное предложение (S+V) — в отличие от предлог+which, whereby не требует переноса подлежащего.

✅ a system whereby evidence is evaluated — правильно (система как механизм)

✅ a procedure whereby defendants may appeal — правильно (процедура)

❌ Justice, whereby all people should be treated equally... — неправильно (justice не механизм/процедура)
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
Whereby the law states that... The legal framework whereby disputes are resolved... Whereby не открывает предложение. Оно следует за noun phrase, которую модифицирует.
A system whereby to submit evidence... A system whereby evidence is submitted by the parties... Whereby требует полного предложения (S+V), не инфинитивного оборота.
The judge, whereby she decided the case... The judge, who presided over the case, determined... Whereby не используется для людей. Для людей — who/whom.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

International human rights law has developed an enforcement architecture whereby treaty obligations, having been ratified by signatory states, create justiciable rights that domestic courts in dualist jurisdictions may nonetheless decline to apply in the absence of enabling legislation — a gap whereby the gap between international commitment and domestic enforceability remains one of the most persistent structural weaknesses of the contemporary human rights system.
① whereby + clause (первый) — описывает механизм международного права: как договорные обязательства создают права
② having been ratified — reduced relative (passive perfect participial): упаковывает 'after they have been ratified' в 3 слова
③ justiciable rights — юридический термин: права, которые можно защитить в суде (не просто 'legal rights')
④ in dualist jurisdictions — post-modifier: точная юридическая классификация без объяснения
⑤ a gap whereby (второй) — анафора + whereby: называет проблему и встраивает её описание одновременно

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ N + whereby + clause → +GRA: advanced relative adverb; сигнал academic/legal register

✅ Whereby в определении процесса → +LR: точное описание механизма без громоздких 'by means of which'

✅ Двойной whereby (механизм + следствие) → +CC: логическая цепочка внутри одного предложения

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Использовать whereby после абстрактных существительных без процессуального значения (justice, freedom, equality) — семантически неверно и снижает LR

📐 Grammar 2 · Wherein / In which case / Whereupon

«В рамках которого / В таком случае / После чего» — встроенная логика

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне B ты использовал 'in which' как более формальный вариант 'where' или 'in that'.

В уровне C wherein, in which case и whereupon — это не просто формальные варианты. Каждый выполняет точную логическую функцию: wherein локализует, in which case создаёт условную логику, whereupon описывает немедленное следствие.
Конструкция Логическая функция Пример Band 8 Когда использовать
N + wherein + clause Локализация: в котором / внутри которого / в рамках которого The constitutional doctrine of proportionality establishes a framework wherein the restriction of fundamental rights may be justified only where the legislative objective is sufficiently compelling and the means employed represent the least intrusive option available. Когда нужно указать на пространство, контекст или условия — внутри которых что-то происходит
[clause], in which case [result] Условная логика: в таком случае / тогда Courts may apply judicial notice to facts of common knowledge, in which case the normal requirement of evidential proof is waived and the judge may instruct the jury accordingly without further demonstration. Если Х — тогда (автоматическое) Y; логическое следствие из предыдущего условия
[event], whereupon [immediate result] Немедленное следствие: после чего / вслед за чем The presiding judge admitted the contested evidence, whereupon defence counsel entered a formal objection — subsequently preserved for appellate review — on the grounds of its prejudicial effect outweighing its probative value. Юридический нарратив: одно действие немедленно влечёт другое; более формально, чем 'after which'
except where / save where / unless in cases wherein Ограничивающее исключение The principle of non-retroactivity prohibits criminal liability for acts that were lawful at the time of commission, except in cases wherein international law recognises the act as a crime against humanity irrespective of domestic legal status. Юридические исключения и ограничения

⚡ Wherein vs. Where vs. In which — стилистическая шкала

Конструкция Пример Регистр
where a situation where conflict arises Нейтральный — Band 5–6
in which a situation in which conflict arises Академический — Band 6–7
wherein a framework wherein conflict is adjudicated Юридический / высокоакадемический — Band 7–8
in which case evidence is contested, in which case admissibility is reviewed Логически точный — Band 7–8
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
The case wherein the defendant lied... The case in which the defendant was found to have misrepresented material facts... Wherein локализует контекст/пространство, не описывает событие. Для конкретных событий — in which.
The judge ruled, in which case the defendant appealed. The judge ruled against the motion, in which case the defence immediately lodged an appeal. In which case требует чёткого предшествующего условия. Без него связь неясна — снижает CC.
Whereupon, the court decided... The prosecution presented new evidence, whereupon the court granted a brief adjournment. Whereupon не открывает предложение самостоятельно — оно связывает два события, оба должны быть названы.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The principle of double jeopardy establishes a jurisdictional constraint wherein a defendant who has been acquitted of a criminal charge may not be prosecuted again for the same offence — a protection that operates, in which case subsequent evidence of guilt emerges, as a fundamental limit on state power rather than a procedural technicality, and one whereupon its abrogation is contemplated, the rule of law itself is placed in jeopardy.
① wherein — локализует принцип: внутри этого ограничения действует запрет
② who has been acquitted — стандартный defining relative: информация об ответчике
③ in which case — условная логика: что происходит, если появляются новые доказательства
④ rather than a procedural technicality — антитеза (C18 preview): не X, а Y
⑤ whereupon its abrogation is contemplated — whereupon как логическое следствие предположения

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ Wherein + clause → +GRA: academic/legal relative adverb; +LR: precise register

✅ In which case → +CC: встроенная условная логика, заменяет громоздкое 'If this happens, then...'

✅ Wherein + in which case + whereupon в одном абзаце → демонстрирует range of relative structures

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Перепутать wherein (локализация) с whereby (механизм) — семантически неточно; экзаменатор, знакомый с юридическим языком, это заметит

📐 Grammar 3 · Reduced Relative Clauses — причастные обороты

«evidence presented by... / conditions required for...» — максимальная компрессия

🔗 Связь с уровнями A и B

В уровне A ты встречал причастия как определения: 'the man running' = 'the man who is running'.

В уровне B ты научился использовать причастные обороты для стилистического разнообразия.

В уровне C reduced relative clauses — это прежде всего инструмент информационной плотности: они позволяют встроить несколько уровней описания в одну noun phrase, создавая тот самый академический стиль, который экзаменатор видит как GRA Band 8.
Тип Полная форма → Редуцированная Пример Band 8 Условие применения
Active Present Participle NP + who/that + V(s) → NP + V-ing The doctrine governing the admissibility of hearsay evidence has undergone significant judicial development over the past three decades, reflecting a broader shift toward pragmatic evidential inclusivity over strict categorical exclusion. Субъект relative clause = субъект главного предложения; действие одновременное
Passive Past Participle NP + that is/are/was + PP → NP + PP The constitutional rights guaranteed by the European Convention are subject to limitation only where the derogating state can demonstrate that the restriction meets the proportionality test established in Strasbourg jurisprudence. Passive relative clause: that is guaranteed → guaranteed
Participial Phrase (Perfect) NP + that has/had + PP → NP + having + PP The legislature, having failed to enact the requisite enabling legislation within the prescribed timeframe, left the treaty obligations ratified by the executive branch without domestic legal effect — a constitutional lacuna recognised by subsequent judicial review. Having + PP: для предшествующего действия
Adjective Phrase NP + that is + adj → NP + adj (postpositive) International courts face a persistent jurisdictional challenge: states unwilling to submit to compulsory adjudication may simply refuse to participate, leaving judgments rendered in absentia without meaningful enforcement mechanism. Adj/PP postpositive: 'states unwilling' вместо 'states that are unwilling'

⚡ Пять уровней постмодификации noun phrase — Band 8 пример

Простая NP: «the rights»

+ Adjective: «the fundamental rights»

+ PP: «the fundamental rights of accused persons»

+ Reduced relative (passive): «the fundamental rights of accused persons guaranteed under international law»

+ Reduced relative (having): «the fundamental rights of accused persons guaranteed under international law, having been repeatedly affirmed by international tribunals»

+ Full relative: «... which nevertheless remain systematically violated in jurisdictions lacking independent judicial oversight»
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
The case judged by the Supreme Court... The case adjudicated by the Supreme Court / The case determined by... 'Judged' — слишком общее; в академическом и юридическом тексте 'adjudicated', 'determined', 'decided' точнее.
The defendant, found guilty, he was sentenced. The defendant, having been found guilty on all counts, was sentenced to... Dangling modifier: субъект reduced relative должен совпадать с субъектом главного предложения. 'He was sentenced' — избыточное местоимение.
Rights protecting by the constitution... Rights protected by the constitution / Rights that the constitution protects... Active participial ('protecting') неверен для passive значения. Rights не protect — они protected.

⭐ Model Sentence — Band 8

The principle of legality, consistently affirmed by superior courts across common law jurisdictions and now widely regarded as a foundational constraint on legislative power, requires that criminal offences be defined with sufficient precision to enable citizens to regulate their conduct accordingly — a standard not always met by broadly drafted statutory provisions susceptible to expansive judicial interpretation.
① consistently affirmed — reduced relative (passive): заменяет 'which has been consistently affirmed'
② now widely regarded as — второй reduced relative (passive): параллельная конструкция
③ a standard not always met by — nominalization + reduced passive: 'a standard that is not always met'
④ broadly drafted statutory provisions — stacked pre-modifiers (C12 preview): три прилагательных перед существительным
⑤ susceptible to expansive judicial interpretation — postpositive adjective phrase: вместо 'that are susceptible to...'

🔵 Examiner's Eye — что видит экзаменатор Band 7–8

✅ Passive PP (postmodifier) → +GRA: compressed relative; +LR: academic verb choice

✅ Having + PP → +GRA: perfect participial = временная последовательность без придаточного

✅ Stacked post-modifiers (PP + reduced relative + PP) → +GRA Band 8: сложная NP как знак зрелости

⚠️ Что снижает балл: Dangling participle (субъект reduced relative не совпадает с субъектом главного) — грамматическая ошибка, хорошо заметная экзаменатору; снижает GRA значительно

🟢 Band 8 Upgrade — трёхуровневая трансформация

Band 6 The court has a system which means that defendants can appeal against decisions that they think are unfair.
Band 7 The court operates a system whereby defendants retain the right to appeal against judgments they consider procedurally or substantively unjust.
Band 8 The appellate architecture of common law jurisdictions establishes a tiered review system whereby lower court determinations, having been subjected to procedural scrutiny, may be set aside on grounds of either legal error or manifestly unreasonable factual inference — a mechanism designed to reconcile judicial finality with the imperatives of substantive justice.

📌 Band 8 абзац: интеграция C3+C4+C5+C6 — эталонный пример

The adversarial model of criminal justice — a procedural architecture whereby competing narratives of fact are subjected to rigorous adversarial testing before an independent tribunal — rests on a set of foundational assumptions regarding the capacity of contested oral evidence to produce reliable factual determinations. Much as this model has demonstrated genuine strengths in protecting defendants against the coercive power of the state, its structural dependence on witness testimony, increasingly recognised as epistemically unreliable, constitutes a persistent challenge that procedural reform efforts have addressed only partially. It is precisely the gap between the model's normative aspiration — a framework wherein truth emerges from adversarial contest — and the cognitive realities of human memory and perception that critics of adversarial procedure most cogently identify. Were the system to incorporate more systematically the insights of cognitive psychology into its evidentiary rules, the procedures currently governing eyewitness identification — widely regarded as among the most unreliable forms of evidence — would require fundamental reconsideration.

① whereby — описывает процессуальный механизм во вступительной NP

② Much as (C5) — concession: признаёт достоинства модели перед критикой

③ increasingly recognised as epistemically unreliable — reduced relative (passive)

④ It is precisely... that (C4) — cleft: выдвигает центральное противоречие

⑤ wherein — локализует нормативный идеал в рамках модели

⑥ Were the system to incorporate (C3) — Were-инверсия: гипотетическое решение

⑦ widely regarded as among the most unreliable — reduced relative: компрессия оценочного суждения
❌ Типичные ошибки — и ПОЧЕМУ они ошибки
Неправильно Правильно Почему это ошибка
Rights of persons who accused of crimes... Rights of persons accused of crimes / Rights of persons who have been accused... 'Who accused' — активный, неверный. Persons don't accuse themselves. Нужно passive: accused of / who have been accused.

📖 READING 1 — Академический текст

The Structural Logic of Legal Language

Legal drafting represents perhaps the most demanding form of written English in terms of informational precision, syntactic complexity, and register consistency. The obligation of legal texts to be simultaneously comprehensive and unambiguous produces a characteristic syntactic architecture wherein clause upon clause is embedded within the primary sentence — not for rhetorical effect, but because each embedded element eliminates a potential interpretive ambiguity that could prove consequential in adjudication.

Courts have developed sophisticated interpretive doctrines whereby the apparent meaning of a statutory provision may be displaced by purposive construction — a process wherein the judge's task is not to apply the literal words of the legislation but to identify the legislative intent that those words, however imperfectly, were designed to express. This doctrine, having been subject to considerable scholarly controversy regarding its democratic legitimacy, nonetheless represents the dominant approach to statutory interpretation in most common law jurisdictions — a development that reflects both the increasing complexity of modern legislation and the pragmatic recognition that no text, however carefully drafted, can anticipate every circumstance to which it will be applied.

The tension between textual fidelity and purposive interpretation represents, in its deepest form, a question about the proper relationship between legislative and judicial power — a constitutional question, in which case it arises within a codified constitutional order, that may be resolved by explicit textual provision, but which, in jurisdictions lacking a codified constitution, requires ongoing negotiation between the branches of government. Granted that neither extreme — absolute literalism nor unconstrained purposivism — commands serious jurisprudential support, the boundaries of legitimate judicial creativity in statutory construction remain genuinely contested and institutionally significant.

📖 READING 2 — Академический текст

Justice, Information, and the Architecture of Legal Sentences

The density of legal language is not an accident of professional habit but a structural necessity arising from the peculiar demands of legal reasoning. A legal proposition, unlike an ordinary descriptive statement, must be both precisely bounded — excluding circumstances to which it is not intended to apply — and sufficiently general to capture all the cases it is designed to govern. This dual requirement produces the characteristic legal sentence: a multi-layered construction wherein qualifications, exceptions, and conditions are embedded at every syntactic level, creating a text that is simultaneously determinate and flexible.

The relative clause, particularly in its reduced participial form, represents one of the primary syntactic instruments whereby this density is achieved without sacrificing readability beyond acceptable limits. A statute providing for the detention of persons reasonably suspected of having committed offences carrying a maximum sentence of more than two years — to take a typical example — contains, in a single noun phrase, five distinct layers of modification, each of which narrows the application of the provision. Were these modifications expressed as separate sentences, the cumulative effect would be cumbersome; compressed into a single phrase, they achieve a precision that separate statements could not replicate without considerable redundancy.

Notwithstanding the genuine communicative achievements of legal drafting at its best, it is equally true that the same techniques of compression and embedding are frequently deployed in ways that obscure rather than clarify — producing texts wherein the nominal complexity serves the interests of those who profit from interpretive ambiguity rather than those who need to understand their legal rights and obligations. For all its technical sophistication, a legal system wherein the law is not practically accessible to those subject to it raises profound questions of democratic legitimacy that no amount of judicial interpretive creativity can fully resolve.

💡 Central Question — ответ:

Упаковать максимум информации в минимум слов — это не сокращение. Это архитектура. Reduced relative clauses и whereby/wherein не убирают смысл — они убирают лишние слова, оставляя только структуру мысли. Экзаменатор видит не краткость, а контроль: автор знает, что важно, и оформляет это с точностью, которую нельзя сымитировать без понимания.
